# Let’s Rebuild
I finished Shoe Dog this morning. Moving read. I’ve logged my notes elsewhere. 

I then listened to a 1.5 hour audio lecture on Awaken the Giant Within by Tony Robbins.

Conclusion: I have a lot of inner work to do; negative self-beliefs that I need to repair and build positive ones. 

If my mind is where all my expressions come from, I have a lot of work to do on my mind:
- Lack of self-confidence and assurance: I’m always doubting myself, reducing myself; even as a leader in church. I shrink in the presence of people, feel like they don’t respect or regard me. 
- Impulse control & GREEEEDDDDD: Especially as it affects my finances. I don’t budget. When my emotions get really dark, I start spending extravagantly. To punish myself? Even when I know it’s going to lead to a painful outcome, I still keep going on. This is a problem.
- Sexual morality: Not that big of a deal, but I need to tackle my propensity toward lust. Big time.
- Sense of self: This is a huge one. I don’t have an anchor. Not even the New Creation seems to be that for me right now. I think I’m materialistic in this regard. I don’t have an anchored sense of self. I try to project subtly. All kinds of ways. I’m incredibly shy around women, and I project around them too. This is a big problem. I feel very small, Daniel. I don’t think I deserve big, good things. This shows in my relationship to God. How I navigated my choice of a partner too.
- Overwhelm: This is more external. I don’t have control over my time. This is a huge huge one. Everyone wants me to do something for them and nothing for me. I’m financially stuck. I’m productivity stuck. How do I make the space to move forward? Pleros. Dad. Tending to a pregnant wife. It’s all so stifling.
- I need to have a clearer head, maybe related to the previous point. In programming tasks, I suck at breaking things down and reasoning in a scoped manner.
- Drive for Souls: Ah! The biggest of them all. There’s zero drive for ministry. All rooted in discouragement, doubt and unbelief; I literally don’t think it’s worth trying. I don’t see how to get from here to there. The task seems so huge. The men so many, the competing voices so many. Like what impact can I even make here. 5, 6 10 disciples following me? You’re kidding. 

I recall many many men with promise that wilted. My fault? Theirs? Makes no difference. I doubt myself as much as I doubt God. Which I think is the same thing. 
- See I don’t believe that when you ask God for something , there are guarantees that it would be. Especially in ministry. That coupled with the sheer absence of any self-efficacy, I always expect to fail. Expect to not be effective to make a man commit to God’s Purpose. These are deep issues that need quite some time and focus to deal with.

Interesting, discipline is not my problem. Overwhelm and chaos are.
