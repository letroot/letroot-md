A Wind of Change is stirring

I think he was livid on Tuesday, and it was yesterday I recorded the state of eCommOS and sent it to him. Maybe I should include the loom link here. I'll do that, I guess, do that now.

This morning I had a two-hour prayer session. I'm feeling pretty stirred by God's spirit to pray for myself in terms of ministry effectiveness. I know my heart is quite weak when it comes to the conviction and consciousness of ministry. I've used so many things to numb it, pouring myself into work, all sorts of things, but the truth of the matter is I'm not a solid example of a pastor. I need to change. I need to radically up my effectiveness. I need to radically gain and work in the drive of God's spirit for the lives of men. I need to become an example to the membership of Ibadan Church.

It seems like there's an order the Lord is inspiring in my heart:

- Friday and Saturday, no food; that is, you fast two days out of every week.
- Sunday mornings, you may or may not eat.
- Friday and Saturday, no food.
- Sunday, you must go out for one hour to heal the sick.
- Every night between 9 p.m. and 10 p.m., you must be reading or going through your Christian to-read list.

Right now I'm on "Healing the Sick" by T.L. Osborn and "Soul Winning" by T.L. Osborn. I think I prefer the hard copy of "Healing the Sick" to the audiobook. I've been listening to the audiobook, but man, it's been hard to follow through. I'm going to have to sit down with these two books tomorrow and really dive into them.

Of course, I'm supposed to read every morning, so I don't know. Maybe I'll move my Christian reading to the night and do my non-Christian, non-fiction reading in the morning. I think that can work.

I'm supposed to read my Bible now instead of journaling. Here's the thing: I don't think setting goals is the first thing for me. I think building self-efficacy is the first thing for me. I want to get to that point where I believe I can actually do effective ministry. Until I get to that point, I can't set goals that I would commit to. That self-efficacy is not just for ministry but also for my natural assignments. I don't have goals, to be honest. I'm just taking each day, making progress each day, because I don't know what to set as a goal, because I'm not yet sure if what I'm trying now to be a work. I don't think that's a good place to be.

I think I first need to have that solid hope and faith that e-comm can become a SaaS that is bringing in millions of Naira every month and set a goal for that. The goal is going to force me to confront my fears and the things I'm scared of doing or the things I don't feel up to.

Let me just say the goal: my goal is that by April 19 I would have a hundred customers for e-commerce.

How about that? My goal is that by the end of May I would have reached a thousand new people on UI campus with flyers and I would have ten people regularly attending church through me. That's a goal I can start to pray toward, build my faith toward, and then get the goal. Yeah, you know what those are my goals. I'm going to write them down. How about that? What else?

I really, really am burning for ministry effectiveness at this point. I want to become the maximally effective minister, gifts of the Spirit; I'm there. homiletics; I'm there, doctrinal breadth; I’m there.

I need to have the fire, drive, initiatives.

That's the kind of spirit I want to have. Yeah.
