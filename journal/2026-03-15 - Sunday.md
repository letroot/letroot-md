- Another takeaway — observe your mind from a distance. Emotion is Information and Motivation

> > This is part of his framework for distress tolerance, where he explains that emotions serve two primary functions:
> 1.  Information - they signal what matters and what might be at risk \(e.g., fear tells you something is threatening\)

2.  Motivation - they drive you to take action \(e.g., fear motivates you to run away or protect yourself\)
> Dr. K emphasises that emotions are not behaviours themselves—they provide signals that should be examined rather than blindly acted upon. The key is to ask "What is my fear telling me?" and then "What is it telling me to do?"—flipping reaction into strategy.
