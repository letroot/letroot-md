## On God’s Revelation in God’s Purpose

Yeah so I think it's a pattern. We start with Abraham. He calls him. It gives him a revelation because Abraham first builds his altar. Abraham doesn't first build the altar; God first speaks to him. God appears to him. He receives revelation and encounter.

- Jacob an encounter
- Isaac an encounter
- Moses an encounter
- Samuel an encounter
- Daniel multiple encounters

There's the place of working with what you know, which I think is the major way God works with is those he calls. You have the law of Moses, what God's purpose and plan is, passed down from generation to generation and all. Everyone just had that to work with and they are prophets and priests to teach, to call them back to order but you can't miss the place of the encounters that God will give to leadership.

As a leader in God's purpose and plan, let's see Paul as an example: an encounter with Ananias, an encounter with Peter, encounters, I believe, the Lord Jesus encounters. Of course there is a lot of fasting and praying, just waiting on the Lord.  Acts 13, Acts 13:1-3, rather encounters. Second Corinthians 12, another encounter.

You see it all over scripture that God will relate with leadership that way, really do that. She via counters, you could see like that so that's something I think I need to pay more attention to, right? If you feel so disconnected from God's purpose, you feel so un-driven, so dry, like you're not even sure, you don't even know what God is trying to do on the campus and you are there and you are the pastor of that campus. I think it's unheard of, right? I think you have to take that initiative, like they did in the book of Acts, to just go and wait on the Lord for as long as you need to to download heaven's hearts, heaven's spirits about the land that you are on. It's a pattern.
