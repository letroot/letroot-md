# How We Grew Our App to $30K/Month

## Summary
In this episode, [[Pat Walls]] interviews [[Alejandro]] and [[Mario]], the founders of [[Push Scroll]], a mobile app that blocks social media until the user completes physical exercises. They achieved $30,000 in monthly recurring revenue (MRR) within four months of launch by utilizing a "content-first" strategy. By creating a viral [[TikTok]] video using placeholder footage to validate the idea before writing any code, they ensured market demand and built a massive waitlist, proving that distribution is the most critical factor for [[Consumer Apps]].

## Key Insights
*   **[[Content-First Validation]]**: The founders created a "fake" product demo video that garnered 80,000 views and 500 comments begging for the app. This validated the concept before a single line of code was written.
*   **[[Distribution-Led Growth]]**: Instead of building first and marketing later, they mastered virality first. Distribution-first thinking allows for rapid scaling—one video with 6 million views converted directly to $10,000 MRR.
*   **[[Push Scroll]] Model**: The app hijacks the user's dopamine loop. To unlock social media like [[TikTok]] or [[Instagram]], users must perform push-ups, squats, or planks, earning 1 minute of screen time per repetition.
*   **[[Embarrassing MVP]]**: The initial version had only three screens: a simple push-up detector, a block list, and a lock screen. Speed to market during the viral peak was prioritized over UI polish or code quality.
*   **[[Hard Paywall Strategy]]**: The app launched with a $30/year hard paywall. This provided immediate validation that users were willing to pay for the solution, rather than just downloading a free tool.

## Coach's Corner

**On Distribution-First Thinking:**
As a founder, your biggest risk is not "Can I build it?" but "Will anyone care?" [[Alejandro]] and [[Mario]] flipped the script by solving for distribution before development. Most [[SaaS]] founders spend months in a "coding cave" only to launch to crickets. If you can't sell the *idea* of your product in a 15-second [[Short-Form Content]] hook, the product itself probably won't save you. You must become a master of [[Virality]] and [[Market Demand]] before you become a master of the stack.

**On The Power of Visual Novelty:**
The success of [[Push Scroll]] was rooted in its "Visually Heavy" nature. Doing push-ups to unlock a phone is a striking, easily understood visual. For your own projects, ask: can a 5-year-old understand the value proposition in 3 seconds? If your app is too complex for a [[TikTok]] attention span, you are fighting an uphill battle. You need a "Curiosity Gap"—a hook that makes people stop scrolling because they've never seen that specific transformation before.

**On The "Embarrassing" MVP and Execution Speed:**
The founders built their MVP in just two weeks with only three screens. This is a masterclass in [[Lean Startup]] principles. They didn't worry about [[Scalability]] or a perfect [[User Experience]] initially; they focused on the core loop that solved the problem. If you are a young man building a business, stop obsessing over your [[Tech Stack]] and start obsessing over your [[Feedback Loop]]. If you aren't slightly embarrassed by your V1, you launched too late.

**On The Organic-to-Paid Bridge:**
They didn't start with [[Paid Ads]]. They used organic content to hit $10k MRR, which acted as a massive [[A/B Testing]] ground. By the time they started scaling with [[UGC]] and [[Influencer Marketing]], they already knew exactly which hooks and formats converted. Your organic feed is your research department. Once you find a "Winning Ad" format that gets 6 million views for free, you have a license to print money with paid acquisition.

**On Solving Fundamental Human Desires:**
[[Push Scroll]] works because it addresses two core human desires: the need to stop a [[Dopamine Addiction]] (doom-scrolling) and the desire to be physically fit. It doesn't just block an app; it replaces a negative habit with a positive one. When brainstorming your next [[SaaS]] or app, look for "High-Friction" problems in people's daily lives. If you can bridge the gap between where they are and where they want to be through [[Gamification]], you have a winner.

## Action Items
*   [ ] **Validate via Content**: Create a 15-second "fake" demo for your next feature or product idea and post it to [[TikTok]] or [[YouTube Shorts]].
*   [ ] **Research the Niche**: Spend 1 week "warming up" a new social account by interacting solely with content in your target niche to understand why videos go viral.
*   [ ] **Simplify the MVP**: Audit your current project and identify the 3 screens that constitute the absolute minimum core loop.
*   [ ] **Implement a Hard Paywall**: Test a simple yearly or monthly subscription early to confirm that people will actually trade money for your solution.
