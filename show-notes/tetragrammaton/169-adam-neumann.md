# Adam Neumann

## Summary
Adam Neumann, the co-founder of WeWork and Flow, sits down with Rick Rubin to discuss his entrepreneurial journey, the intersection of spirituality and business, and the exact timeline of how he built his companies. He provides a first-person perspective on the creation of Green Desk, the explosive rise of WeWork to a $47 billion valuation, and the subsequent launch of his new residential real estate venture, Flow.

## Key Insights
*   **The Power of the Flow State**: Neumann describes experiencing life moments that fueled a spiritual quest, ultimately leading him into a profound [[Flow State]]. Once he hit this state, things began moving rapidly, culminating in the creation of Green Desk, WeWork, and the birth of his first child.
*   **Spiritual Awakening in Business**: Introduced to practices like Kabbalah and inner work by his wife Rebekah, Neumann integrated spiritual principles into his business philosophy. He emphasizes resilience, believing that "the light will be revealed" even during the darkest, most challenging moments of an entrepreneur's journey.
*   **The Birth of Green Desk**: Co-founded with [[Miguel McKelvey]] in 2008, [[Green Desk]] was an eco-friendly co-working space in Brooklyn. They filled the first floor incredibly fast using just five Craigslist ads, proving the viability of the shared workspace model.
*   **The Pivot to WeWork**: Recognizing the massive potential, Neumann approached their landlord, Mr. Gutman, proposing a $20 million investment for 50% equity to scale Green Desk nationally. Gutman declined but offered to buy them out for $3 million (netting Neumann and McKelvey $1.5 million for their 50% stake). This capital injection directly funded the launch of [[WeWork]] in 2010.
*   **The Next Chapter with Flow**: Founded in 2022, [[Flow]] is a residential real estate company focused on branded, tech-enabled apartment communities. The core innovation is creating a sense of ownership, where a portion of rent could potentially be invested into the company, merging the concepts of community-driven living with equity building for renters.

## Business Timeline
*   **Pre-2008**: Neumann undergoes a period of spiritual seeking, unlocking a deep "flow state" that accelerates his life's trajectory.
*   **2008**: **Green Desk is established.** Operating out of a partially vacant Brooklyn building, Neumann and McKelvey launch the eco-friendly co-working concept and achieve immediate traction via Craigslist.
*   **Late 2008 - 2009**: The Buyout. Neumann pitches a national expansion. The landlord declines but buys the founders out for $3M, providing the seed capital for their grander vision.
*   **2010**: **WeWork launches** in New York City with the ambition to build a global, community-driven co-working brand.
*   **2010s**: **The Hyper-Growth Era.** Driven by heavy venture funding (including major backing from SoftBank's Masa Son), WeWork scales explosively, reaching a peak valuation of roughly $47 billion.
*   **Circa 2019**: **The Exit.** Following a failed IPO attempt and intense public scrutiny of the company's valuation and governance (later dramatized in "WeCrashed", which Neumann claims was only 30% accurate), Neumann steps down from WeWork.
*   **2022**: **Flow is founded.** Neumann re-enters the market with a massive new venture aimed at revolutionizing residential real estate through community and fractional ownership mechanisms.

## Coach's Corner
**On Recognizing the "Green Desk" Opportunity:**
Daniel, notice how Green Desk wasn't the final vision—it was the wedge. Neumann used a single floor in Brooklyn and five Craigslist ads to validate a multi-billion dollar hypothesis. As you build your SaaS, don't get paralyzed by the grand vision of [[WeWork]]. Launch your version of Green Desk. Find the cheapest, fastest way to prove that strangers will pay for the value you're offering.

**On Leverage and Knowing When to Sell:**
When Neumann pitched his landlord for $20M and got a $3M buyout offer instead, he didn't cling to Green Desk out of ego. He recognized that the $1.5M payout was the exact leverage he needed to build the *real* company he wanted. In your journey, you may build side projects or micro-SaaS tools. If someone offers you an exit that provides the capital or freedom to build your magnum opus, take it. Don't fall in love with the stepping stone.

**On Integrating Spirituality and Resilience:**
Neumann credits his success to hitting a "flow state" and adopting spiritual frameworks that gave him resilience. The entrepreneurial path is brutal, filled with near-death moments for the business. Your technical skills (React, Tailwind) are essential, but your internal operating system—your mindset, your daily practices, your ability to stay centered when metrics drop—is what will dictate your long-term survival. Cultivate practices that ground you outside of your MRR.

**On Engineering Ownership:**
Look at Neumann's core thesis with Flow: aligning incentives by allowing renters to build equity. As a founder, think about how you can engineer ownership among your early users. Can you build affiliate structures, community boards, or gamified rewards that make your users feel like they own a piece of the ecosystem you're building? When people feel ownership, they stop being customers and become evangelists.
