# Living in a garage to building the $1B+ 'Savannah Bananas'

## Summary
Jesse Cole transformed a struggling minor league baseball team into the [[Savannah Bananas]], a global sports entertainment phenomenon valued at over $1 billion. By applying the "Fans First" philosophy and studying legendary entertainers like [[Walt Disney]] and [[PT Barnum]], he created "Banana Ball"—a fast-paced, highly engaging version of baseball. Despite early financial ruin and local backlash, Cole's relentless focus on customer experience, radical transparency, and continuous idea generation built a massive, loyal following and a multi-million person waitlist.

## Key Insights
*   **The Power of Attention**: [[Attention]] must precede creation. If people don't know who you are, the best product won't matter. Cole emphasizes doing the "exact opposite" of whatever is normal to be truly unforgettable.
*   **The Fans First Philosophy**: Becoming a "friction fighter." The Bananas eliminate traditional revenue streams (like stadium ads and ticket fees) to remove customer friction. They focus on metrics like feeding fans within five minutes, prioritizing long-term loyalty over short-term profit.
*   **The Creative Engine (Over The Top Sessions)**: A rigorous creative process inspired by [[Saturday Night Live]]. The team holds weekly "OTT" idea sessions where every staff member pitches new skits, trick plays, and promotions to ensure no two shows are ever the same.
*   **Radical Resourcefulness & In-House Control**: They manage logistics, merchandise, and broadcasting entirely in-house. They even stream games for free on [[YouTube]] rather than taking traditional TV deals, rejecting sponsorships to keep the focus solely on fan connection.
*   **World-Building Over Just Baseball**: The goal is to build a comprehensive entertainment world (like [[Disney]] or the [[UFC]]), leading to expansions like the Indianapolis Clowns and international "World Tours."
*   **Talent Strategy (Attracting vs. Recruiting)**: Fostering a culture of enthusiasm and growth. With a 12,000-person waitlist to work there, they hire young, hungry individuals, often promoting from within (e.g., interns becoming team president). 


## The 11 Fans First Leadership Principles
Inspired loosely by Amazon's 14 leadership principles (and playfully choosing 11 because K is the 11th letter of the alphabet, the symbol for Potassium, the key element in a banana), Jesse Cole's team abides by these 11 rules:

1. **We're Fanatical About the Fan** - The foundational rule.
2. **Entertain Always** - Every touchpoint should be an experience.
3. **Play the Long Game** - Prioritize long-term loyalty over short-term revenue.
4. **Whatever's Normal, Do the Exact Opposite** - Don't let people get comfortable with "normal."
5. **Ideas Are Everything** - The starting point of all creation and growth.
6. **Constantly Curious** - Learn from the best *outside* your industry, not inside it.
7. **Everything Speaks** - A Disney principle; if you lose the details, you lose it all.
8. **Fewer Things Done Better** - Focus relentlessly on what you can be the best in the world at (e.g., cutting events to focus solely on Banana Ball).
9. **Relentlessly Resourceful** - Finding creative ways to get what you need (e.g., trading speeches for hundreds of thousands of dollars in office furniture).
10. **Uphold the *Highest* Standards** - Not just "high" standards, because everyone defines "high" differently.
11. **Always Plus the Experience** - Constantly asking how to add that extra 1% to make it magical.


## Rigorous Monitoring of Metrics
Rather than obsessing over traditional financial KPIs, Cole rigorously monitors the metrics that directly impact the customer experience. He views revenue simply as a "scorecard" that shows if they are doing the right things. 

Key experience metrics he tracks include:
*   **Wait times for merchandise:** His goal is to get everyone through the merch line in under 10 minutes. When stadium logistics made this difficult, they invested in massive "outdoor mall" tents to improve throughput.
*   **Time to feed fans:** At their home stadium in Savannah, every fan can get their all-you-can-eat food within 5 minutes (down from an hour in their early days).
*   **Entertainment density:** Tracking the speed of the game, the number of trick plays, and "ball four sprints" per game.
*   *Note:* In contrast to his obsession with these fan metrics, Cole says he only holds one financial meeting a year to review the budget and decide where to reinvest.

## Coach's Corner
As a young man building a business, it's easy to get tunnel vision—obsessing over the technical details of your SaaS or the day-to-day grind, while losing sight of the bigger picture. Jesse Cole's story is a masterclass in treating your business, your marketing, and your life as an interconnected "experience."

**On Marketing & Removing Friction:**
Whether it's your product or how you present your brand, consider how you can become a "friction fighter" in everything you do. Where are you making it too hard for people to buy from you, support you, or understand your vision? Stop marketing like everyone else. If your peers are doing standard cold outreach or blending into the noise, what's the "exact opposite" you can do to stand out? Cole built a billion-dollar brand by making the product unforgettable before he worried about monetizing every interaction. Focus on grabbing attention first—build a "world" around your product that others actively want to be part of.

**On Productivity & Winning the Morning:**
You can't build that world if your own mind is chaotic or reactive. This is where mastering your morning becomes your ultimate leverage for productivity. Implement the 6-step SAVERS routine (Silence, Affirmations, Visualization, Exercise, Reading, Scribing) to set the tone for your day. Before the world demands your attention, you have to claim it for yourself. 

Pair that discipline with Cole's daily habit of generating 10 new ideas ("Idea Muscle"). If you spend your morning scribing 10 ideas a day—about marketing angles, product features, or life improvements—that's 3,650 ideas a year. You only need a few of them to hit big to change the trajectory of your business and your life. Building a great SaaS—and a great life—isn't just about executing tasks; it's about continuous, disciplined creativity.

## The 6-Step Morning Routine (SAVERS)
A concept often associated with "winning the morning" and personal development is the 6-step SAVERS routine from *The Miracle Morning* by Hal Elrod:
*   **Silence:** Meditation, prayer, or deep breathing to start the day calmly.
*   **Affirmations:** Positive statements to align your mindset and goals.
*   **Visualization:** Mentally rehearsing your day or visualizing your long-term success.
*   **Exercise:** Getting your heart rate up and moving your body.
*   **Reading:** Consuming a few pages of a self-improvement or educational book.
*   **Scribing:** Journaling your thoughts, ideas, or gratitude.
