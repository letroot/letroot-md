# Living in a garage to building the $1B+ 'Savannah Bananas'

## Summary
Jesse Cole transformed a struggling minor league baseball team into the [[Savannah Bananas]], a global sports entertainment phenomenon valued at over $1 billion. By applying the "Fans First" philosophy and studying legendary entertainers like [[Walt Disney]] and [[PT Barnum]], he created "Banana Ball"—a fast-paced, highly engaging version of baseball. Despite early financial ruin and local backlash, Cole's relentless focus on customer experience, radical transparency, and continuous idea generation built a massive, loyal following and a multi-million person waitlist.

## Key Insights
*   **The Power of Attention**: [[Attention]] must precede creation. If people don't know who you are, the best product won't matter. Cole emphasizes doing the "exact opposite" of whatever is normal to be truly unforgettable.
*   **The Fans First Philosophy**: Becoming a "friction fighter." The Bananas eliminate traditional revenue streams (like stadium ads and ticket fees) to remove customer friction. They focus on metrics like feeding fans within five minutes, prioritizing long-term loyalty over short-term profit.
*   **The Creative Engine (Over The Top Sessions)**: A rigorous creative process inspired by [[Saturday Night Live]]. The team holds weekly "OTT" idea sessions where every staff member pitches new skits, trick plays, and promotions to ensure no two shows are ever the same.
*   **Radical Resourcefulness & In-House Control**: They manage logistics, merchandise, and broadcasting entirely in-house. They even stream games for free on [[YouTube]] rather than taking traditional TV deals, rejecting sponsorships to keep the focus solely on fan connection.
*   **World-Building Over Just Baseball**: The goal is to build a comprehensive entertainment world (like [[Disney]] or the [[UFC]]), leading to expansions like the Indianapolis Clowns and international "World Tours."
*   **Talent Strategy (Attracting vs. Recruiting)**: Fostering a culture of enthusiasm and growth. With a 12,000-person waitlist to work there, they hire young, hungry individuals, often promoting from within (e.g., interns becoming team president). 

## Coach's Corner
As a technical SaaS founder, it's easy to get bogged down in product features and code while neglecting the end-user experience. Jesse Cole's approach is a masterclass in [[User Experience (UX)]] and [[Marketing]]. Consider how you can become a "friction fighter" in your own SaaS—where are the annoying "ticket fees" or "wait times" in your onboarding? Furthermore, his daily habit of generating 10 new ideas ("winning the morning") is a powerful mental model. Building a great SaaS isn't just about writing code; it's about building a "world" your users want to be a part of, much like Banana Ball. Don't be afraid to do the "exact opposite" of your competitors to stand out.

## Action Items
*   [ ] **Audit your user journey**: Identify and eliminate three points of friction for your customers today (be a "friction fighter").
*   [ ] **Establish a daily idea habit**: Spend 10 minutes each morning writing down 10 new ideas related to marketing, product features, or customer delight.
*   [ ] **Review your pricing model**: Are there hidden fees or confusing tiers that alienate users? Simplify it.
