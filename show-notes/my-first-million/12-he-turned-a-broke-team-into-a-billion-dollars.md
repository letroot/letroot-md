# He turned a broke team into a billion dollars

## Summary
[[Sam Parr]] and [[Shaan Puri]] discuss the incredible story of [[Jesse Cole]], the founder of the [[Savannah Bananas]]. Cole took a nearly bankrupt minor league baseball team and, through relentless experimentation, showmanship, and a focus on "fan-first" entertainment, turned it into a massive touring phenomenon (Banana Ball) that earns tens of millions a year. They also discuss concepts from [[Unreasonable Hospitality]] by [[Will Guidara]] and how going above and beyond the expected norm is the true secret to building a beloved brand.

## Key Insights
*   **Insanely Great**: [[Steve Jobs]] didn't just want great products; he wanted them *insanely* great. The "insane" part is doing things that make no logical sense to others (like finishing the inside casing of a computer no one will see) but are crucial for the product's soul.
*   **The Idea Muscle**: Jesse Cole forces himself to write down 10 new ideas every single day. Most are terrible, but you have to work your idea muscle to get to the truly innovative concepts.
*   **They Only Remember You're Weird**: A mental model from [[George Mack]]. Meeting expectations gives you zero credit. You only get remembered for doing things that are genuinely out of the ordinary (weird). For example, the Savannah Bananas cover the sales tax on all their tickets so fans pay exactly the advertised price.
*   **Unreasonable Hospitality**: A concept from [[Will Guidara]] (former owner of Eleven Madison Park). The idea is to go completely overboard to delight a customer. It means spending an allocated percentage of your margin to do wacky, memorable things for your clients.
*   **Strategy Requires Disagreement**: A framework from [[Dan Porter]] of [[Overtime]]. If everyone in your industry agrees with your strategy (e.g., "we have great customer service"), it's not a strategy; it's table stakes. A real strategy is something others would actively disagree with or think is a waste of time.
*   **The "Are You Down?" Hiring Filter**: When hiring, look for people who are just "down" to try crazy ideas without immediately shooting them down or asking for data where none exists. Fragile, new ideas need a team that won't crush them prematurely.

## Coach's Corner
As a technical founder, it's easy to fall into the trap of only caring about the code, the product metrics, or things you can perfectly measure. But what the Savannah Bananas and Unreasonable Hospitality show is that *emotion* and *brand* are built in the unscalable, "insane" details. Ask yourself: what is one weird, unreasonable thing you could do for your first 100 SaaS customers? Maybe it's a personalized video welcome, or paying for something they don't expect. Don't wait until you're huge to care about the experience. As Shaan noted, you don't get big and then worry about the details; you worry about the details and *then* you get big.

## Action Items
*   [ ] **Brainstorm**: Write down 10 totally out-of-the-box marketing ideas for your SaaS today. Do this every day to build your "idea muscle."
*   [ ] **Audit**: Review your customer onboarding flow. Find one place where you can add a touch of "unreasonable hospitality."
*   [ ] **Reflect**: Does your current growth strategy pass the "disagreement test"? If your competitors would all agree with your plan, you need to get weirder.
