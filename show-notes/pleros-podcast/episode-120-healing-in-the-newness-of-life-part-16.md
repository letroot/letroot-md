# Healing in the Newness of Life - Part 16

## Summary
In this episode, Pastor Akim emphasizes that supernatural healing is readily available to believers in the newness of life, acting as both a defense and a primary response to sickness. He clarifies that healing is effected by faith—whether the faith of the minister, the recipient, or a third party—and explores the dynamic between faith, unbelief, and doubt. Using examples like the Centurion, the Syrophoenician woman, and the healing of the epileptic child in Mark 9, the teaching reveals how human responsibility intersects with God's provision for healing.

## Key Insights
*   **Healing is Available Today**: While bodily immortality is a future promise to be fulfilled at the Lord's return, [[Supernatural Healing]] is fully available today in the [[Newness of Life]].
*   **The Triad of Healing**: Supernatural healing should serve as a believer's defense (Exodus 15:26), a primary and joint response to disease (alongside [[Natural Healing]]), and sometimes the only solution.
*   **Faith is the Mechanism**: The scriptural accounts consistently show that healing is effected by faith. Jesus constantly affirmed this: "Your faith has made you whole" or "According to your faith be it unto you." It is man's responsibility to receive what God has made available.
*   **Sources of Faith**: Healing can be activated by the faith of the minister (e.g., Jesus healing the epileptic child), the recipient (the woman with the issue of blood), or a third party (the [[Centurion]] or the [[Syrophoenician woman]]).
*   **The Battle with Unbelief**: In the account of the epileptic child ([[Mark 9]]), the father cried out, "Lord, I believe; help my unbelief!" This highlights the very real struggle believers face when genuine faith is challenged by emotional doubt or a lack of deep-seated consciousness.

## Coach's Corner
**On Taking Responsibility:**
Daniel, the concept that God has made provision but requires man's responsibility (faith) to effect it is directly applicable to entrepreneurship. The market opportunities are there, the tools to build your SaaS are available, but nothing happens until you take responsibility and execute. Don't wait for success to drop into your lap. You have to actively lay claim to your goals through decisive, daily action.

**On Managing Your "Unbelief":**
The father in Mark 9 had faith, but he also recognized his doubt ("help my unbelief"). As a founder, you will experience this duality constantly. You believe in your vision, but you'll have moments of intense doubt when things don't go as planned. Acknowledge the doubt, but don't let it dictate your actions. Push through it and keep building. Your "faith" in your business is demonstrated by your continued execution despite the uncertainty.
