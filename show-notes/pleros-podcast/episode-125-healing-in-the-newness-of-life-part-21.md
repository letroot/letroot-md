# Healing in the Newness of Life - Part 21

## Summary
Pastor Akim deepens the discussion by showing that [[Faith]] is not just conviction but ongoing stand, reckoning, and persistence. He explains that both believers and unbelievers may receive healing because faith receives what God has made available, but believers have the added advantage of knowing healing as part of their covenant reality in the [[Newness of Life]]. The episode also warns ministers against condemning weak people instead of helping them, stressing that faith grows and varies across a spectrum.

## Key Insights
* **Faith Receives; It Does Not Command God**: Faith is presented as the receiving posture toward what God has already made available in [[Redemption]].
* **Believers and Unbelievers Can Receive**: Because God is good and healing is available, even unbelievers may receive by faith, though believers should walk in healing as a known right in Christ.
* **Walking in Healing Is Like Walking in Character**: The reality is available, but its expression in daily life still involves ongoing yielding, reckoning, and choice.
* **Faith Is a Spectrum**: Weak faith, strong faith, little faith, and unbelief all describe varying states of a person's stand.
* **Do Not Condemn the Weak**: Ministers should not turn faith teaching into blame. Jesus rebuked in order to help, not to crush people.
* **Persistence Matters**: The key issue is not whether someone once agreed with truth, but whether they remain established in it long enough to receive or minister effectively.

## Coach's Corner
**On The Difference Between Insight and Endurance:**
Daniel, founders often overvalue insight and undervalue endurance. You can have the right strategy, the right market thesis, and even the right product instinct—and still lose if you do not remain established long enough for those truths to mature into outcomes. This episode is really about that gap. Conviction starts the work. Persistence carries it.

**On Building Without Becoming Harsh:**
There is also a leadership lesson here. When you're under pressure, it becomes easy to interpret weakness—your own or other people's—as moral failure. But harshness rarely produces better long-term work. It usually produces fear, avoidance, and performative behavior. If you want to lead well, including leading yourself, you need standards without contempt. Correct the problem, but do not build a culture of accusation in your own head.

**On Choosing Your Stand Daily:**
The comparison between healing and character is useful. In startup life, many of the things you want are already available in seed form—clarity, discipline, courage, focus, patience. But they still have to be walked in. You do not become focused once forever; you choose focus again. You do not become courageous once forever; you act courageously again. That daily stand is what compounds.
