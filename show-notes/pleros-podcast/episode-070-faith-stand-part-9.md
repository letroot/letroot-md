## Faith at the Center of Christian Life

All right, welcome back to the Pleros podcast. This is your daily dose of God's word transforming you to fulfill God's purpose for your life. Now, we're looking at faith stand—and I could say welcome back to faith stand. This is how you are transformed to fulfill God's purpose. And I will be right because we are really at the center of the Christian faith. We are discussing what is at the heart of it because that's exactly how you got saved: by faith.

But now we're talking about how you grow and how you walk in all that God wants you to do; it is by faith. Faith is at the center of the Christian life. If you misunderstand it, you are going to miss out on certain things that should be part of your life.

Now, we must be careful not to import ideas unnecessarily. Because the subject matter of faith has a wide variety of teachers, teachings, opinions, ideas, summations, and conclusions, we have to strive as much as God can help us to stay as close to the truth revealed in God's word on this subject matter as possible.

## Honesty About Results

Maybe I should say it at this juncture that one thing we must be honest about is faith, because faith has to do with results and it is easily verifiable by results. Say we are talking about healing and someone is yet to receive their healing, or we're talking about any other kind of need. The immediate concern of "I expect a result that I'm not seeing" can come up.

There must always be this honesty on the part of the preachers of God's word—or at least I'll say on my part—to say there are times where the answer is: we don't know why things happen or why some things are the way they are. But we know what God's word has said, and that means we stick with God's word independent of what we don't know.

We are human beings. We have limited knowledge. We definitely do not know everything. I mean, we don't even know the materials that make up certain planets. We don't know whether there are certain things that have not been discovered on other planets. We don't know so many things in the natural. So this attitude of "there are things we don't know" is fine.

We must be willing to say that upfront, but say it so that we can focus on what we know. What we know is enough for us to live by, and we'll stick with it independent of any contradiction there may be until you see the results. So that must be your attitude.

I've not got to talking about things like healing. There's going to be a separate series on that for the podcast. And I say that because healing is an awesome subject matter that involves our faith and other areas of our lives where we must walk in the wisdom of God. I used to use the example of someone hitting his head on the wall and at the same time having faith for healing from headache; he will not even know when he's healed. There are times people are doing the wrong things and they are staying in faith. Yeah, it's sort of like deceiving yourself. So you are contradicting yourself. You have to be doing right in the natural and then you should also be doing right in the supernatural. But let's leave all of that for later.

## Faith Stand: Growth and Renewal

Presently we're just looking at faith stand. That's how we grow to fulfill God's purpose for our lives. Now, we have seen that growth happens by faith. Let's just say we've seen that from Romans 6 where Paul told them that you know you are already dead to sin, you are alive to God. The old man is gone, but you need to reckon yourselves. He told them: present your members not as instruments of unrighteousness but rather as instruments of righteousness.

Romans 12:1 will repeat the same thoughts to them when he told them to present themselves as a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God. By so doing, they are not being conformed to the world but rather they are transformed by that renewing—that presenting is the renewing or refreshing of their mind.

## Helpers of Your Joy

So there is no doubt that what Paul was teaching in Romans 6 and Romans 12 is what we can rightly call faith stand. That's because 2 Corinthians 1:24 makes it clear that was the work he had towards them. He told them, "I don't have dominion over your faith." I can't control you.

Preachers do not have control over other people. When we lead the church, we can put order in the church, but we must be careful with the word control because that might mean usurping people's will to act in a particular way. That is not reflective of the scripture in any way because the scripture is very loud in respect of freedom of conscience.

Whilst there's order in how the church is set up—and that's why you find the Bible talk about people that are unruly (when order is laid down, they go against the order)—the idea of having dominion would be a completely different ball game. In the order that we set up in church, the work of the preacher is just to help people's faith. That is all.

So you can set up the order and then you have done your part: "we are to meet at this time," "this is how to grow," "this is what you're supposed to do." Next, you find different people at different levels of their growth—some behaving and some misbehaving. Your job is to help them so that they can line up with what they are supposed to do. We are just helpers of the faith of people. If he says "I do not have dominion over your faith, I'm a fellow helper of your joy," at that point it means all we can do is supply the knowledge for their faith. Supply the knowledge that brings them into conviction. Also supply the inspiration or exhortation that will cause them to take a stand on God's word.

## Knowledge Leading to Worship

So it's one thing I come to church and I preach a very powerful message about who we are in Christ, about redemption. Sometimes I could just be teaching on any other subject matter. Indeed, when we teach on any subject matter, it should usually dovetail into redemption and the new creation because this is the essence of God's word—from the very get-go, He just wanted us to have His life and walk in His life.

So today we might find, as a matter of necessity in our theology, that we deal with fine details of doctrine here and there. But if we cannot tie it back to what should make for our walk—which includes fellowship with God, worship of Him, prayer, and then our pursuit of His purpose or our assignment—then no matter how honoring people might be of the knowledge that is shared, the essence of that knowledge is lost if it doesn't lead to worship or walk.

It's very important that we have the right perspective to how we approach the faith. We don't want to err on the side of "you can teach anything and get away with it" because you want to speak to the work we are meant to have in worship, in our assignments, or even in our personal life. But neither can you err on the side of the knowledge of God's word not affecting our walk. That would not be God's expectation.

## The Persistence of Faith

Faith is really important. We thank God for people like Kenneth E. Hagin that have helped our understanding on this subject matter, and several people as well shedding more light. Presently, we have been focused on two principles of faith:
1. The fact that faith is of the heart and the mouth.
2. The persistence of faith.

I think this issue of the persistence of faith is probably what faith stand is at the center. Faith stand is standing there and insisting on what is already true—true of God, true in His word—until we see the results of the same. We stand there insistently.

I showed it the last time in Luke 11, if you recall what the Lord Jesus said there. In Luke 11, He talked about the man that had a friend come visit him in the midnight and didn't have anything to offer him. And so, he went to his other friend who was already asleep with his children on the bed. And I mean, he was persistent. NKJV uses the word persistence. KJV uses the word importunity, and we could simply say he was unashamed.

So, of course, persistence is there. He was just not going to give up. He did not care. And there is that type of attitude to faith: "Well, no matter how it looks, I'm here. I'm going to get this thing. I'm so sure about it." There is that kind of confidence that we have.

The Lord ended that by saying, "So I say to you, ask and it be given to you." Now I want you to remember that what led to that parable was the Lord's Prayer (or the "Our Father" prayer). So the Lord had just taught them how to pray. He then told a parable that speaks to the need for being unashamed, the need for having importunity or being persistent. Because that means He's saying prayer has this attitude of persistence, of insistence.

I understand that there are certain texts of scripture people have read and by those texts they have become weary about being persistent in faith. For example, people can read the instruction of the Lord about not having "vain babblings" and they say, "Oh, that means don't repeat anything in prayer." This mindset would be incorrect because there were repetitions in scripture of prayer.

There is no guarantee of lack of repetition. Language is limited. Even when you pray in tongues, there's a tendency to say, "Oh, maybe that person is repetitive." What really counts is that genuinely in your heart you are persisting in faith about the issue. Your expressions might differ or sometimes might be the same, but it's not the case that you are just going through a recitation. That is the challenge—recitation has become a habit for some. But genuinely, if you're persisting in faith, that is what it means. That's what the Lord is speaking to. He is against this mechanical repetition or recitation; what delivers the result is rather that insistence, that persistence until the job is done.

So He says: whoever asks receives, whoever seeks finds, whoever knocks the door will be open to the person.

## The Parable of the Unjust Judge

Now let me show you the second parable we should look at from the Lord on the persistence of faith. You need to be persistent when it comes to faith. The second parable, even though this one has the possibility of being an eschatological narrative, is in Luke 18:1. It spoke about the widow and the unjust judge.

In Luke 18:2, "There was a certain judge who did not fear God nor regard man." Now there was a widow in that city and she came to him saying, "Get justice for me from my adversary." And he would not for a while, but afterwards he said within himself, "Though I do not fear God nor regard man, yet because this widow troubles me I will avenge her, lest by continual coming she wearies me."

Now can you see that it's just sheer persistence? Something has to fall. The persistence, if a man is in faith, is the utter conviction about the truth of what you are standing on. This is the truth. So I am not going to give up on it no matter what.

You often hear people who have exercised faith for very difficult situations and seen results sometimes say that even if it hadn't happened for maybe 50 years, they would still be there. Of course, it shouldn't be that long, but there must be that conviction that is already set up to be that persistent. The kind of conviction that is set up to be—if I may say—eternally persistent is the kind of conviction that defines the faith stand of which we speak.

## Conclusion

So the Lord said, "Hear what the unjust judge said. And shall God not avenge his own elect who cry out to him day and night, though he bears long with them?"

There is the argument that this is about the eschatological fulfillment of redemption—the return of the Lord consummating His plans in this age. But He says He is going to avenge His elect, and I think the lesson on faith is present notwithstanding.

Verse 8: "I tell you that he will avenge them speedily. Nevertheless, when the Son of Man comes, will he really find faith on the earth?"

The kind of faith we have eschatologically—that Christ has done redemption and that redemption will be consummated—is the kind of faith we should also have for our walk presently. That faith that does not give up. That faith that is persistent.

And so this very important principle of faith is necessary. Very likely in the next episode, we'll be wrapping faith stand to move into a new subject matter. But I do want you to appreciate the importance of persistence as baked into the conviction that we call faith and that makes for faith stand.
