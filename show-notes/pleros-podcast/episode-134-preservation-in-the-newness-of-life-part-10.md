## Everything Needed Is Already Given

Well, as believers in Christ Jesus, we've seen that at the instance of our faith, we have entered into a new life, and it's in that life that we're going to grow to fulfill God's purpose. Everything we need to fulfill God's purpose has been given us in the life that we have and have entered into in Christ.

There's a text, 2 Peter 1 and verse 3, that says he has given us also all things that pertain to life and godliness. So we might say everything that is of him has given on to us. Some might say it's everything that we need for our life and for our walk in godliness has been given on to us. But for certain, we have all that we need already to fulfill God's purpose given on to us. There's nothing that God is withholding from us anymore.

Maybe another text to mention in this regard would be Ephesians 1:3. Blessed be God, the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with all spiritual blessings in heavenly places in Christ. So when we speak of spiritual blessings, you might want to say, "Well, spiritual blessings are not natural things." But spiritual blessings are things that are possible by God's spirit. So even the immortality of our bodies is a spiritual blessing. It's a blessing by God's spirit, even though it's natural in its effect.

So it's not just spiritual things that pertain to the new nature that we have in our spirit like love, joy, peace, all those things that we now have. It's spiritual blessings across the board, or spiritual blessings, let me say it more accurately, that is, things that can be done by God's spirit.

## Healing and the Cosmos

Now one of such we've seen is healing. In the new life God has given on to us, the best thing that could happen to our body is for our body to become immortal, but that would only happen at the end. Also in this new life, the best thing that could happen to the natural world, to the cosmos, is for it to become incorrupt. Albeit, those things will only be effected at the end in God's plan. And whilst we are still in this redemptive age for the sake of the consummation of God's plan and purpose, we would have to deal with mortal bodies and corrupt cosmos.

And for that reason we must also ask the question, what then by God's spirit that effects these things at the end is available to us today? For our bodies we have already answered healing is available, but what about for the cosmos?

Well, the real answer here will be relative to us in the cosmos what is available to us. That is, when the cosmos that is corrupt gets sick and maybe certain evils happen or break out, are there things that are available to us that we can be in faith for? Or do we simply resign to the natural order of things?

## Walking by Faith, Not by Sight

Well, there are things available to us. In fact, it's very important that when we recall this text that says, "We walk by faith, not by sight," it's calling us to looking at things from the newness of life or from redemption rather than just the natural.

So Paul is not one to ignore the natural things we can observe and reason as part of truth, but we now have a perspective as those who have believed the gospel that goes beyond just the natural order of things where we can believe in what is available in the spirit. We don't just speak to the natural state of our humanity, not the natural state of the world. We must point to the things that are available to us in redemption.

So we walk by the realities of the newness of life, not just by the realities of the natural world. We walk by faith, not by sight. It's calling to what we have already believed, the redemption God has accomplished, the new life he has given us, not just by the natural order of the world.

Do we walk by sight as well? Of course you do that as well. You look both sides of the road before you cross. That's why you take some necessary precautions as you should. That's why, based on the things you have observed, you have seen sights, you know how to conduct yourself, to bathe as you should, brush as you should, all those type of things because of the things humanity has learned by observation and reason.

But we also have a perspective in our walk that is not just that we should be supernatural. We should be given to walking in the newness of life. It's not a loss, it's even a gain and advantage. That means there's a complementary work of the supernatural in the natural world.
