## Beholding the Glory of the Lord

And with that fact on our mind, our beholding is what transforms us. So it is not enough that our faces are unveiled. It is not enough that we know. It is more important that we are beholding. So that you now know is fine, but your beholding is what transforms you. Let me just go there. So we could read it. 2 Corinthians 3:18: "But we all with unveiled face beholding as in a mirror the glory of the Lord are being transformed into the same image from glory to glory just as by the spirit of the Lord."

## Faith of the Heart and Mouth

Now, I haven't said that much. We began; we saw a couple of things about faith because if faith is how all of this happens—this reckoning, this presenting, this transformation, this beholding—we need to know what faith is. We said there are two major things we should know about faith:

1.  **Faith is of the Heart and the Mouth**: In the heart, we agree and accept for ourselves the truth of God's word. There is faith that simply agrees that something is the case, like demons believe there is God and they tremble. Then there is faith that accepts for oneself. Someone says, "I believed on the Lord Jesus." It's not that I just believe that there is Jesus or I don't believe the work He did, but I believe for myself. In that case, you are not just agreeing but accepting for yourself.

Someone can say, "I believe there is healing." This is where some people will call the first faith I described, where you simply agree with the truth but not accept for oneself. I think some may call it mental assent, traceable to John Wesley. Some might say, "I agree there is Jesus, probably did something, but who knows." So the person agrees but the person doesn't accept for himself. When we say there is faith in one's heart, you agree with the truth and also accept it for yourself.

## Confession and Growth in Faith

But then when we now talk about the mouth side of faith, at this point you are speaking, you are affirming. You are affirming that which you have accepted for yourself. Say I preached the gospel to you. You accept that, "Wow, the Lord has done this work for you," and you accept it for yourself. Then you say, "Lord, I thank you. I believe you did this, and you did it for me, and I receive it. I am saved now. Amen." That singular act—accepting it for yourself and making that acceptance clear with your confession—there is zero doubt that you have received the gospel at that point.

But do you know the difference between what just happened at salvation and what happens in our growth? When you receive the gospel as salvation, you have all of God's Spirit, if I may use it that way just for emphasis. You have God's Spirit, and not in a measure. You have the fullness of Christ in you. Ephesians 1:23, John 1:16, we could say you have the fullness of Christ in you; you are one spirit with the Lord; you are a partaker of divine nature. At that point, you got it all. You were blessed with all spiritual blessings.

But when we talk about faith stand for our growth and our walk, it is not one instance of affirmation that receives. It is more of a stand for growth that transforms, that affects your walk, and causes you to fulfill God's purpose. So at this juncture, when you have this second kind of faith, which is the faith for your walk (you could call it walking faith, standing faith, different descriptions), the effect it has is on your reckoning or your consciousness—the state of your reckoning or the state of your consciousness. And you are being transformed there as you are affirming, "Oh, I'm a new creation. I'm a new creation."

You are being transformed as you are renewing your mind, as you are refreshing your mind, as you are presenting. I hope you get this. As you are being transformed, then you are walking because if you remember Romans 12:2 says, "Be not conformed, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind that you may be able to do." So, you are so transformed that you are walking in the character of God. You are walking in divine healing and health. You're so transformed that you are walking in any supernatural provision that we have by God's Spirit.

## Persistence of Faith: The Seed Principle

The first principle of faith is of the heart and the mouth. And the second principle of faith is that faith is persistent until the result. I want to say that one of the core reasons the Lord Jesus used the example of the seed in describing faith—"if you have faith as a mustard seed"—is to speak to this subject matter of the persistence of faith.

Also, the reason why we can account for what we may call measures or degrees to faith is because of this issue of the persistence of faith. A seed is persistent, if we may think of it that way. When you plant the seed, it dies and then, taking up all the other things, it gathers itself back; it gets its roots and springs up until it bears fruits. So that idea of persistence when it comes to the seed, if you recall the parable of the sower, particularly the good soil, the seed that was planted, the Lord told us about patience in how that seed grew. That's even in Luke 8:15: "The ones that fell on good ground are those who having heard the word with a noble and good heart. Keep it. Do you see that? They keep it and bear fruit with patience." You could just say with persistence. I tell you, you cannot wish away the place of persistence to faith. Like you'll just stay there. You keep it and you bear fruit with patience. So the persistence of faith is the core principle of faith.

## Unwavering Conviction and Action

Now the mechanics of faith—how it works—is heart and mouth. But the core, the very nature of faith—what we describe as that conviction that sticks with that thing unchangingly—is the persistence of faith: "This is the truth, and I stay here." I'm not saying it happens automatically, otherwise you won't find consistent instructions to reckon, to present, to behold. I'm saying if the conviction is right and one acts on those instructions that followed from that conviction, you should be unwavering. You ought to stay there to persist until whatever result is done—maybe character transformation, maybe something in the supernatural, maybe healing and health. Very important. You stay in faith. Oh, thank you, Lord. You stay in faith. You stay in faith.

You insist. You persist on the power of God working in your body. The power of God working over you. The power of God working for you. You stay in faith. The persistence of faith, a core principle of faith. So we saw two parables told by the Lord. The friend that was without any shame just persisted, insisted, and we saw the other one, which is the widow and the unjust judge. And we saw the kind of persistent faith that she had.
