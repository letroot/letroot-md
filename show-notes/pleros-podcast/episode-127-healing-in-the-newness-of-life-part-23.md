## Faith as Conviction and Faith as Reckoning

Now having said that, we are now focused on not just understanding how that faith that affects healing works, but how we are strong in faith and full of faith to effect and receive healing. In terms of understanding how that faith works, we have seen that we have to recognize two kinds of faith. There's faith as conviction, and there's faith as reckoning or consciousness.

Faith as conviction is not what affects healing. It is the baseline for healing, where I believe there is healing, God can heal, God wants to heal, and that one is basic. You have that faith. You're convinced of that. But then there is the other part, which is faith as reckoning, or faith as consciousness, or faith as faith-stand, which is your stand of faith.

This is not a matter of your state of conviction. It is rather how you are standing actively by faith. Now again, faith is not an order. Faith is God's design for us to exercise our freedom to receive, to reject, to walk in, to stop walking, and so on. So if we understand faith in that context, we simply exercise it so that we can walk in what we desire. It is the freedom to exercise what we want. It's not an order to test people or to save people. That is not it at all.

## Faith Can Waver

With that settled, this second kind of faith is on a spectrum and can waver. That's something we must not ignore. It was shown to us well enough in God's word. We saw the Lord teach on that. Paul did. James did. Even Peter, when he says be steadfast in faith, it shows that you can be weak.

So we must be aware that this can happen. People can waver in faith. You can be like Peter: you are in faith, walking on water, and then for reason of the tumultuous waters and the winds, you can be in fear. You can be like Jairus. You're already in faith, and by reason of another report you can go into fear. You can get anxious.

That's why Paul says you should be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication, let your requests be made known unto God with thanksgiving. You can be edged into unbelief. And we're not talking about rejecting something in conviction. In consciousness, you can be edged by your exposure, by influence around you, in your consciousness or your reckoning, into unbelief.

You can see where people change environment around maybe an atmosphere of faith, and they go somewhere else and they become weak, not because they have not seen some things and their conviction remains the same, but their reckoning has changed. This is just the truth. That's just what happens. You might have even experienced it personally, where you were much stronger in faith, not as conviction but as reckoning or consciousness, at a time than another.

## Abraham as the Pattern of Strong Faith

So it matters a lot that we understand how faith works for healing. We have tabernacled with Paul's explanation in Romans 4 as the best way to explain how faith works for healing. Romans chapter 4 and verse 19 says, not being weak in faith, Abraham was not weak in faith. He did not consider his own body already dead, nor the deadness of Sarah's womb.

So he's showing us here how he could have become weak in faith if he spent so much time just looking at the natural state of things. There is nothing wrong with knowing the facts. There's nothing wrong with that. It's good, it's fine. There will be no case, there will be no basis of exercising faith in a particular regard, if you did not know that there was something wrong. So knowing the facts is fine.

It's like knowing that there's a mountain is the first step to addressing the mountain. But there's something wrong with being consumed by that. He says he did not consider, and he uses that word which is to fully observe, like his own mind is consumed by it. Your whole mind is taken over by it. At that point you are going to be wavering in unbelief. The state of your reckoning will be unbelief. Even if your conviction is different, you'll be so overwhelmed by just the problem that you cannot be full of faith about the solution.

## Strengthened in Faith, Giving Glory to God

So he says, at the promise of God, he did not waver in unbelief. There is a place for knowing the fact. That place exists and that one is okay. But there is something beyond just knowing the fact, which is being overwhelmed by the problem. What did Abraham do? He did not, at the promise of God, waver in unbelief, but was strengthened in faith.

You could go read the account. Abraham was not full of faith in an instant at all. Abraham was in unbelief for much of the instance before he got into faith and became full of faith. So you can be at a point where you're like, I don't know, I'm not sure about it. Then at a point you're like, yeah, I agree, there's healing power. Now from that point, that's a good point. Rather than just agree and then go back to considering the problem so much, it's now time to get strengthened in faith.

So we must learn how to have strong faith and how to be full of faith to effect healing. It says he was strengthened in faith, giving glory to God. Giving glory to God is like affirming it. Thank you Lord for your power that works in my body. Thank you Lord, I am healed. Thank you Lord that this is done. Thank you Lord for what you have done.

So Abraham was strong in faith. He was saying, thank you Lord, I'm a father of many nations. I'm Abraham, a father of many nations. I bring forth the seed that God wants.

## Reckon Yourselves and Present Yourselves

Now why was Paul teaching this? Interestingly, he was teaching it for two reasons: one, for the conviction of the audience he was speaking to, but also for their reckoning as well. Because by the time you get to Romans 6, you start to see him tell them, don't you already know this, but you also need to reckon.

Faith would not just be convinced. Faith will be strengthened by reckoning. Faith will not just be convinced of a truth. It's going to be strengthened by reckoning until it is full of it and walks in it. Which is precisely what Paul then shows us in Romans 6.

He told them in verse 3, "Do you not know?" So you are convinced about what Christ has done, that you are dead to sin, that you are alive to God. You are convinced. It says in verse 6 that your old man has been crucified with Christ. You are convinced that Christ, having died and been raised from the dead, you have been set free from death and have been brought into life. You have been brought into the newness of life.

But having said all of that, verse 11 says, "Likewise, reckon yourselves to be dead indeed to sin and alive to God in Christ Jesus our Lord," showing us the exact same thing Abraham did. So in this case, Abraham was said to give glory to God, which is reckoning and affirming God's promise. But in this case, talking about who we are, we are to reckon ourselves dead to sin and alive to God. So this is character in the newness of life, the new nature and character that we have in Christ. We are not just knowing it; we are reckoning it.

He would describe that act of reckoning as presenting ourselves in verse 13: "Do not present your members as instruments of unrighteousness to sin." So don't keep presenting what used to be. That is letting your entire mind be on the problem. He did not consider his own body already dead. Why is he saying do not present your members as instruments of unrighteousness? Because you can waver into unbelief.

You have already had the conviction, but then you start to think, well, has anything really changed? And then you already have that conviction: I'm a new man. I'm dead to sin, alive to God. And you start wondering, has anything really changed? Maybe I'm just the same person. Don't present yourself like that.

Remember that word present. Don't stand beside yourself as unrighteous. No. It says, present yourself as alive from the dead. Present yourself to God as alive from the dead, and your members as instruments of righteousness. You got to present. Stand beside it. Stand beside it.
