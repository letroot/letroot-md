Now, it's easier to say this is right from the descriptive standpoint if it aligns with what actually is. So, what you say is right if it corresponds with what actually is. If you say that person is tall and it corresponds with the person actually being tall, then what you say is right. Or if you say the book is black and it corresponds, we'll say you're right. But when we speak of that conduct is right, what quality makes it right? Now, conducts because they are prescriptive, they often are regarded as subjective.

## Five Dimensions of Morality in Practice

Now, in edge cases, we must be aware of how righteousness or morality will play out in such situations. And that's what we're going to wrap up with in this episode. So, there are five things we must know when it comes to how morality plays out in any situation.

Number one, there's moral truth, which is what we have discussed. Life is right and death is wrong. Number two, there is moral intention. And number three, there's moral instruction. So, because we always want to do life and don't want to do death, we are likely going to instruct, "Oh, in this situation, don't do this. In this situation, do that." Which is where we see the distinction between the 10 commandments and the law of Moses where one is codifying moral truth and the other one is giving moral instructions based on moral truth. Then, there's moral expression.

So, there are times you even see in the law of Moses as well as today that some of the instructions might say, "Kill someone that is trying to do something like this." Because the intention is the furtherance of life based on moral truth. But that instruction that says kill, that expression when you kill doesn't look like life. Yet, the outcome, moral outcome, is the furtherance of life.

So, let's say we are speaking about terrorists, we are speaking about people that are given to arms and evil or to other people and they are killed. The expression of them being killed seems to be death, but the intention is life. So, the outcome is life. And this is why we say when it comes to moral objectivity, our moral truth must always be life is right. Our moral intention must always be to do life. But, the moral instruction and expression may not always be life for the sake of the moral outcome of life.
