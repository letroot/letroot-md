## Review of Favour in the Newness of Life

We're continuing our teaching on favour in the newness of life and we hope to put to conclusion what we are looking at relative to favour and wealth, prosperity, money, natural well-being. The reason is we've been on that for a while and we want to put everything together conclusively so that we can have a thoroughgoing understanding of how they interact.

First thing we'll do is to remind us that there is such a reality as favour which is in this life that God offered to man from inception, which is available today and will be consummated at the end. We saw favour in the life of the Lord Jesus. Luke 2:52 — he increased in wisdom and stature, in favour with God and with men. Also Luke 2:40 as well — he mentioned that the child grew, became strong in spirit, filled with wisdom, and the grace of God was on him. So this looks like favour as a result of the working of God's spirit. He's growing, he's filled with the spirit, he's strong in the spirit, he's filled with wisdom, the grace of God is on him — there's something special, something distinct, something attractive about him.

In the other case, increased in wisdom and stature, and that same verb "increased" is used for in favour with God and men. So something about his growth, being that he's God become man — we're looking at it from the standpoint of his humanity — something about his growth also produced increase and growth in favour.

## Favour in the Church and the Ministry of Angels

We could also see favour with the church. Acts 2:47 — they were praising God and having favour with all the people. And in this case, the favour seems to be related with the ministry of angels because of the next statement. It says, "The Lord added to the church daily those who were being saved." This idea of an ongoing working of the Lord with them is something we can also see from the four gospels. Matthew 28:20 — "I am with you always to the end of the age." Mark 16 — "The Lord working with them, confirming the word with signs following." So something was going on. There was an accompanying working of the Lord with them, which, of course, reading the entirety of scriptures together, you'd understand that that is by the ministry of angels.

So we said favour operates by the spirit and the ministry of angels. Another text — Exodus 11:3, where it said the people had favour in the sight of the Egyptians. And Exodus 12:36 mentioned that the Lord granted them favour in the sight of the Egyptians, therefore they got granted their request. So there is such a reality cutting across the Old and New Testament text about favour.

## Defining Favour

Favour we've described or defined, if we may, as the benevolent or gracious working of God's goodness proactively, actively, and reactively for someone's pleasure or their fulfillment of God's purpose. So sometimes it's just for their good, like one's personal good, and many times it is relative to one's pursuit and fulfillment of God's purpose.

All of that said, we've established the basics. Favour works by the spirit. Favour works by the ministry of angels. By the spirit, it works when we are growing and walking in the spirit.

## Favour and Walking in the Spirit

A couple of texts on that. Proverbs 3:1-4 — when you keep God's word on your heart, you meditate on it, you are standing on it, in so doing, verse 4 says you are going to bring favour and high esteem before God and men on yourself. Same thing in Proverbs 11:27 — he that diligently or earnestly seeks good shall attract or procure favour for himself, but trouble comes to those who seek evil. Proverbs 22:29 — the man diligent in his work shall stand before kings, not mere men.

So when someone is walking in the spirit, walking in love, walking in righteousness, walking in integrity, walking in diligence, discipline, etc., then in that sense the person is procuring favour, is increasing favour with God and men. In this sense, when I say favour works by the spirit, the things we would usually see that we describe as favour when you are growing and walking in the spirit will be things like an attractiveness to the person's personal character, a distinctiveness about the person in their natural work, or a distinctiveness by the grace of God for the measure that we have in the work of ministry.

We ran through the texts on that — Ephesians 4:7, Ephesians 3:7-10. I don't want to say too many things so I'm not overloading one episode, but just a reminder because I'm headed somewhere.

## Favour by the Leading of the Spirit and the Ministry of Angels

Also we see favour work not just by distinctiveness and effectiveness, but also by the leading of the spirit. The spirit of God can give us initiatives that lead to favourable outcomes or successful results.

Now if you also watch the ministry of angels, even though we saw the ministry of angels operating with preservation, we said that the responsibility owing to the reign of sin and death — and ordinarily as ministers or servants in God's purpose, whilst man is the heir — their work would have still been to work with man to fulfil purpose. And in that sense, they would have been ministering favour or working out things in man's favour in his work in God's purpose. So we said favour is a primary responsibility of angels. They are helping, aiding our pursuit of God's purpose. We saw Psalm 103, verses 20 and 21 — they do his word, they do his will, or you might say his pleasure (which can also be used for will because of Psalm 40, etc.).

## Favour, Wealth, and Natural Well-Being

If we have that recognition that at origin, angels were designed to work with man in the pursuit of God's purpose, then we need to ask the question: how does favour relate with particularly this issue of provision, money, wealth, prosperity, and natural well-being?

What we've done in earlier episodes is to create a demarcation and a proper framing on this subject matter. We said usually when there is no right framing, you can have ungodly pressure. Sometimes we give the idea that God's expectation for every believer is that they are meant to be wealthy. And how exactly do we define wealthy? If you notice, many times people push you to the edge, so it always goes to the other side — "Well, how do you like, you know, excessive wealth, etc.?" And they turn around. The scripture doesn't seem to be like that.

In fact, we're reading 1 Timothy chapter 6, which is encouraging believers to have a mindset that with food and clothing, therewith you should be content. Now, one who reads that text alone, ignoring every other text of scripture, will say, "Well, God is just about meeting our needs." But if you read the entire thought of the apostles and even the Lord Jesus, you get a sense that God's idea of what is the baseline, what is most foundational for everybody when we're speaking of natural well-being or welfare, is our needs and basic wants being met.

One text that makes that clear would be Ephesians 4:28. "Let him that stole steal no more, rather let him work with his hands that which is good, that he may have to give to him that is in need." So this is not a case of someone whose needs are just met, but also their basic wants are met, so they can also give. You also see 2 Corinthians chapter 9 and verse 8. It says, "God is able to make all grace abound towards you, you having all sufficiency in all things, may have an abundance for every good work." There's a lot we could say about the text because the preceding chapter was about people that were poor and still were willing to give, who were going through a difficult time, a trial of affliction, and were willing to give. So the grace is a grace of generosity, but it still shows that God's desire is a state where we can have our needs met and then our basic wants, so that we can give. Because when someone is really poor, you really shouldn't be demanding of them to do some things. They might barely be just eating hand to mouth, so maybe they don't have anything to spare.

## God Desires the Natural Well-Being of All People

Therefore, it's important that we understand what God's word is saying when it comes to wealth, provision, prosperity — He desires the natural well-being and welfare of all people. The reason why this is important is we cannot say, understanding or discerning from scripture, that he wants people poor. If that were the case, it should have been a consistent thing that people were left poor or that people that were poor were not attended to. God doesn't want people poor, understandably. We live in a world where that happens to be the case, and we're instructed to be generous, to meet the needs of the poor, to give to those who are in need. Like I just mentioned Ephesians 4:28, the Lord Jesus had a purse that was used to attend to the needs of the poor. The church consistently were striving to meet the needs of those who were behind.

What God desires for everyone is their natural well-being or their natural welfare, where their needs and their basic wants are met.

## How God Meets Our Needs

Now, what we need to do is to ask: how does God meet those needs? If you pay attention to what the Lord taught in Matthew chapter 6, he told us the way the Lord meets the need — where God meets the need — is by the way he has ordered the world already. When he says, "Your heavenly Father feeds the birds of the air," you have surely never seen where there was a very big hand with a lot of grains on it and birds were feeding off of that hand. No. The way they get fed is they go find the things God has provided in the world, in the cosmos, on the earth for them, and that's how they get fed. Which means God's desire for our natural well-being and welfare requires our productivity.

However, the Lord is pointing out his favour. He has made things available, and on that note we can also say, not just naturally has he made things available, his supernatural hand accompanies our pursuit.

## Three Factors from Origin

So here's what we want to do. At origin, what was God's design for provision, prosperity, wealth, and natural welfare? At origin, before sin comes into the picture, in the paradigm of biblical theology, what was the state of things in terms of natural welfare, natural well-being, prosperity, wealth, etc.? You notice there are three factors that affect these things from origin.

First is the internal factor. We are instructed to be productive. That is on us. He put man in the garden to tend and keep it. He is the one to tend and keep.

But there are external factors — where he is, the conditions around him. Those are external factors.

Now the third factor, of course, as we are now seeing, is that there is the favour of God. There are people who are to minister to help man's pursuit.

So God intends to have our needs met by our productivity (internal factors), by his favour, and amidst the external condition of the world.

## After the Fall: Productivity, External Conditions, and Favour

Now, we must be aware — right from the mention of sin, it seems we are told that the external factors may not be all that favourable. Yet, man is told to be productive. So we see there is still the mention of productivity as at the ending of Genesis 3. There is the mention of the adverse nature of external conditions. And we can still be assured, as we'll see from the rest of the scripture, that there is God's favour that works with us as we do what we do.

So as believers, our disposition ought to be this: In this age, despite the reign of sin and death, by our productivity and faith in God's favour, despite the external conditions, we can have our needs met. That's the essence of Matthew 6 — that you can, by productivity and God's favour, have natural well-being.

Now, but across scripture, God is not opposed to being wealthy. That means going beyond just natural well-being, going beyond your needs and basic wants being met. But God does not demand that. Natural well-being is instructed right from the beginning — you are to tend and keep the garden, you are to ensure you meet your needs and basic wants. 2 Thessalonians 3:10 says, "He that does not work should not eat." Your needs and basic wants are to be met by productivity. That one is instructed, and God's favour backs us for that.

## Exceptional Circumstances and the Generosity of the Saints

Now, unfortunately, we must admit there are times, despite what God has done naturally and what he does supernaturally, for some reason or another, some people will be in difficult conditions. Like 2 Corinthians 8 shows us — they were going through a trial of affliction. Like Acts 11 as well shows us — Agabus spoke by the spirit that it was going to be a famine, and the response at that point was not to ask for the productivity of those people. This is an exceptional situation, and God's word instructs in such cases the generosity of all the saints to meet the needs of those in that type of situation.

So there is an expectation you might see in God's word that you are to be productive whilst in faith for God's favour amidst the external conditions of the world, to have your needs and basic wants met, to have natural well-being.

## Desiring More Than Natural Well-Being

However, beyond that, you can desire of your own accord — your ambition, your pursuit — that you want even more than just natural well-being. And there is absolutely nothing wrong with that. In fact, a lot of people would desire that, and it's very fine. You can go for it.

Now, we must admit some people don't want the strain, the stress, the demand, the time of all of those type of hustle and bustle. They just want to be fine and have a family and pursue God's will and purpose for them, doing their natural assignment, spiritual assignment, wherever they are. But others may want more and it's okay. But we can't say God is demanding that, because he's not, or God is promising that. That's not what he has promised to them. He has promised that by your productivity and his favour, you can and you should have natural welfare. Where there are difficulties, the generosity of the saints ought to step in. That is what is scripturally taught.
