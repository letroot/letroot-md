## Why Healing Matters in the Newness of Life

This should be our very last episode on healing in the newness of life. We've come a long way already, and we are seeing that healing, as one of the realities in the newness of life, is important to our walk in and fulfillment of God's purpose. At one time or the other, we may need to receive healing for ourselves as we continue our journey in God's purpose.

Also, even as we carry out our spiritual assignments, there will be time and again the need to minister and effect healing in the bodies of people, just for their benefit, sometimes even in the grounding of the message itself. So it's important that we appreciate the place of healing in the newness of life.

And one thing we're settled on is that healing is not just a possibility, but is readily available now in the newness of life to be effected, unlike immortality which will be effected at the end by the Lord, and incorruption. Healing is available now and is to be effected by us in our walk with God.

## Healing Is Effected by Faith

With that said, our next question is: how is healing effected? I have said that it's by faith. It's effected by the faith either of the recipient, the person who needs healing, the faith of the minister, or even the faith of third parties as well can effect healing.

What we did next is to understand the faith that effects healing. Is this faith merely conviction? And what we settled is this: the faith that effects healing is not faith as conviction. It's faith as reckoning or consciousness, what we may otherwise call faith-stand.

So there is faith as conviction. You know two plus two equals four. You don't need to think about it. It's just what you're convinced about. You know something is white. You know you are male or you are female. And sometimes you're not even conscious of it. You just take it for granted.

But there is faith as consciousness, which is not just a conviction that you have. You know you're a new creation. That's something you know. You probably don't waver about it. But you might not be conscious of it, and instead be conscious of something to the contrary. That's where issues about walk could differ from simply descriptive issues like two plus two equals four.

So when we're talking about issues like your identity and your walk, other things can come in and affect your reckoning or your consciousness even when your conviction is right. So it's so important that we have that understanding of the faith that effects healing.

## Faith Can Waver Without Conviction Changing

The faith that effects healing is not merely your conviction. And that's why we saw several examples to establish this, like Matthew chapter 17 and the apostles. These people had been given authority and power. They had healed the sick. All kinds of miracles they had already done by the time. But then they faced another challenge, and the Lord says, "You are faithless and perverse."

You have turned away from what is positive to what is negative. We can't say these people stopped having conviction. Otherwise, it would be strange that they were ministering, and even that they came to ask the Lord, "Why could we not cast this out?" So it seemed they were still convinced of God's willingness and even their own authority to heal, but something had changed.

Maybe because of the kind of case they were facing, their mind had started to switch into doubt. Is this really that simple? Do we need more things? They were not as firm and as rooted maybe as they should have been, arguably. And the Lord said, "It's because of your unbelief, and this kind goeth not out but by prayer and fasting."

We saw this issue of faith as reckoning that can change, that can waver, in Peter's case as well, walking on water. Peter was walking on water, he was in faith, then he saw the winds and he saw the waters, and he was afraid. Then he began to sink. Very important teaching that the Lord made there from that experience. And the Lord saved him first, very important, and he says, "O you of little faith, why did you doubt?"

So it would be strange if anyone said Peter was someone who lacked conviction, or even lacked faith as reckoning to start, but something happened that changed the state of things for him. Same thing with Jairus relative to his daughter. He was in faith that if the Lord ministered she would live, but people came with the wrong report and said, "What's the point?" The Lord said, "Fear not. Don't switch. Don't move away from faith into fear."

There's always that possibility and potential. And even if you're sliding into it, you say, "No, no, no, no." You go back. You're standing firm in faith. Don't waver. James said the same thing: "If any man lacks wisdom, let him ask in faith, nothing wavering." For he that wavers or doubts is like a wave of the sea.

So we have seen that tension between faith and unbelief, between believing and doubting, and we said that's not a tension of conviction; it's a tension in reckoning or consciousness. Very, very, very important.

## How Do We Become Strong and Full of Faith?

Now, with all of that said, our concern now is this: how then do we have strong and full faith to effect or receive healing in our bodies, or minister the same to others?

Remember, we have looked at all the texts about how faith can increase, or how it can decrease; how we can be full of it or be of little faith; how we can be strong in faith or weak in faith; how faith can grow exceedingly; how people can increase in faith; how people's faith can be perfected where it's lacking. Which is all about this second kind of faith, this faith as reckoning, reckoning faith, consciousness faith, whatever one would call it, our faith-stand.

How do we have strong and full faith? The example we've been looking at is Abraham in Romans chapter 4. Paul deliberately set Abraham up as an example of having faith. Interestingly, not just faith for righteousness, but he used the example of Abraham's faith for healing to explain faith for righteousness, the conviction that we have righteousness and how we ought to be strong in the same. Because this entire situation was premised on the fact that Abraham becomes an example of walking in the steps of faith, or of the faith, Romans 4:12.

## Abraham Did Not Consider the Problem

Now, in Romans 4:19 it says Abraham was not weak in faith. He did not consider his own body already dead. Abraham at the beginning was in unbelief. I mean, maybe even as a matter of conviction, who knows? I don't know. But he was really not having it at that point.

He was again found in faith. The Lord had spoken to him and he was not weak in faith this time around. He didn't put his heart, his attention, he didn't allow his mind to be consumed by the problem, whether it's the deadness of his own body or the deadness of Sarah's womb.

He did not waver at the promise of God through unbelief, or at the promise of God he did not waver in unbelief. So he wasn't doubting, he wasn't putting all his attention on the problem. He put his attention, his mind, his heart, on the solution, but was strengthened in faith, giving glory to God.

And as I said, giving glory to God there is simply thanksgiving. It's like, "Oh yes, thank you Lord. I'm going to receive the promise. I'm going to have the seed. I'm the father of many nations. Oh glory to God. Thank God for this. Oh, it's all by God's power."

## Thanksgiving, Prayer, and Walking in Christ

This is very similar to what we saw in Philippians 4:6. It says to be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving, very important, with thanksgiving, let your requests be made known unto God. This is not just you make your request known; it is done. It is done.

Now we see this idea of with thanksgiving. In fact, the very same word thanksgiving used in Philippians 4:6 appears again in Colossians 2 about how we walk in Christ. See Colossians 2:6: "As you have therefore received Christ Jesus the Lord, so walk in him."

Remember, we received Christ by faith. Now we have to walk in Christ by faith-stand as well. So walk in him, rooted and built up in him, established in the faith as you have been taught. You walk in him by faith. Rooted is trying to tell us here that faith makes us unwavering. When we are strengthening ourselves in faith, we become rooted. We are so strong we cannot be moved.

Rooted and built up in him, established. All of these words are words of firmness and fixation. You are fixed, established in the faith as you have been taught. So your being taught is not yet your being rooted. Your being taught means you now have knowledge, maybe conviction. But in your walk, you are rooted, built up, established by faith, in the faith.

You are established in the faith as you have been taught, abounding, increasing, or walking in it with thanksgiving. You're like, "Glory to God, I'm dead to sin. I'm alive to God," in thanksgiving. You are giving thanks. "Thank God, this is who I am. Oh, I'm full of love. That's who I am." You are abounding in it with thanksgiving. That's so important.

## Reckon Yourselves and Present Yourselves

Same thing in Philippians 4:6. So we are not anxious. We pray, we supplicate with thanksgiving. That's why after Romans 4, Paul in Romans 6 will use the example of Abraham to speak to the Romans about their walk in righteousness. Their members are members of righteousness. Their entire person is now to walk in righteousness. And how are they supposed to walk in righteousness? He says, "You already know. Now reckon yourselves."

So that same idea of thanksgiving, that same idea of giving glory to God, is what is at play here. You are reckoning. You are presenting. Reckon yourselves to be dead to sin and alive to God. "Oh, thank God I'm dead to sin. Thank God I'm alive to God." You are reckoning. You are presenting.

"Oh, my body is an instrument of righteousness." He told them in verse 13 of Romans 6, "Do not present your members as instruments of unrighteousness." So the influence around us can weaken our faith and cause us to present ourselves wrongly. You must be found considering what is yours, what is available, what belongs to you in Christ. You are reckoning. You are affirming. You are thanking God. You are conscious of what is yours.

## Presenting Ourselves in Prayer

Do you know where we are supposed to do this act of reckoning ourselves? Already by the fact that he has mentioned thanksgiving and giving glory to God, it ought to tell us that this presenting ourselves, this reckoning of ourselves, should happen in the place of prayer.

Yes, it ought to tell us that this reckoning and presenting of ourselves should happen in the place of prayer. But I want to show you: Paul in Romans again will tell them where they ought to do this act of presenting and reckoning. Romans chapter 12, verse 1: "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies..."

You see that? Same thing. You present yourselves, not as instruments of unrighteousness, but as a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God. You are already holy, acceptable unto God. You can't be presenting the problem.

Now, for healing, which we know is available and is to be effected, you are presenting yourself as who you are. You present your body as a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God. And it says, "This is your reasonable worship," your latria, which is talking about temple worship.

That word is often used in the book of Hebrews talking about the worship done in the temple. Now, of course, we know the true temple is now with us. The Father and the Son have made their home with us, John 14:23. John 4:20-24, we worship in the Spirit. So whenever we are praying, we are worshipping.

Part of our prayer and worship is presenting ourselves. A core part of prayer is standing beside who we are, affirming who we are. "Oh, thank God I'm this." We are strengthening our faith.

## Strengthening Faith Before the Command

Now, I mean, when you minister, you are commanding, you are declaring. And that's part of what we do in faith. But there is the place of strengthening yourself. Hallelujah. There is a place of strengthening yourself.

"The healing power of God always works in my body." You are affirming, you are strengthening yourself. "From the top of my head to the soles of my feet, I am the healed of God." What makes potent your command of faith is usually the strength of your faith that you have built by presenting yourself.

So it tells us, present yourself. This is your reasonable worship. I want you to notice something in verse 2: "Do not be conformed to this world." What is that? Conformed to this world is that you are becoming perverse. You are twisting things. Your mind is going that way. You have the conviction of truth, but your mind is going the way of the world.

But be transformed by the renewing of your mind, that you may be able to prove or do that which is the good, acceptable, and perfect will of God. That's so important. There is the possibility, even after our conviction, for our reckoning or our consciousness to be different, to be conformed to the world, to ignore the things that are ours in Christ.

So when it comes to healing, we must not be found consumed by the problem, but full of faith about the solution.

## Mark 11 and Staying Fixed in Faith

Let's wrap up with Mark 11:23-24. Healing is available in the newness of life, and we effect it. Mark 11:22, the Lord Jesus said, "Have faith in God."

"Whosoever shall say to this mountain, 'Be removed and be cast into the sea,' and shall not doubt in his heart, but believe that the things he saith shall come to pass, he shall have whatsoever he saith." If he is full of faith, he is fixed in faith, he is standing in faith about what he has commanded, he will have what he says.

He does not doubt, but believes. He is full of faith and fixed in faith about what he said. He will have what he says. Therefore I say to you, whatever you ask when you pray, believe you receive them and you will have them.

You can see that this act of believing is an ongoing reality, as much as there has been the act of speaking at once. When we minister healing or effect healing in our bodies or those of others, there is the command that sickness and disease and pain should be gone. There is the affirmation that sickness and disease and pain leave our body and our body is healed.

But there is also sustained being fixed in faith about the result. When we are effecting healing by faith, we are in faith about the process as well as about the result. "The healing power of God always works in my body. Sickness and disease has no place in my body. The power of God works in my body driving out every sickness and disease. I am healed of God. God's power works in my body repelling every sickness and disease, expelling every sickness and disease. Therefore, I am healed of God."

Notice that you are in faith about the process and about the result, and you stay fixed in faith where it is your reasonable worship in the place of prayer.

## Prayer Puts an End to Unbelief

So prayer is one place where we put an end to unbelief. We stay fixed and strong and established in faith, and we eliminate sickness, disease, and the things of death and darkness. And that's why the Lord told them in Matthew 17, "It's because of your unbelief, and this kind goeth not out but by prayer and fasting."

So one of the wisest things we can do to receive healing is not just to exercise faith for our healing, but to stay in faith in prayer so that we are strong and full of faith about what we are believing for until the results are effected.

It's not just the one instance of the exercise of faith, but the staying full and strong in faith in prayer. Not begging or requesting, but affirming, standing, and giving thanks, effecting the healing power of God in our bodies.

So I encourage you, stay in faith. Two things: exercise faith and stay in faith, particularly in prayer. That's how you effect healing for yourself and for others. Stay in faith. Stay in faith. Exercise faith and stay in faith to be strong in faith, full of faith until the healing is wrought.
