## What Does Favour Mean?

What does it mean when we see favor? So favor is the active working of God's goodness for the benefit of someone but particularly as you're going to see for the fulfillment of his purpose or for that person's fulfillment of God's purpose. So it's the active working of God's goodness. And you can say it's the proactive working of God's goodness.

The reason why I'm using that word active or proactive as opposed to reactive is that favor does not require that something has gone wrong. Like healing requires that there's a wrong and then you are being healed. Preservation requires that there is a problem and then you are being preserved from the danger or delivered from whatever oppression of whatever sort. But favor is different. Favor would be whether or not there's a wrong. Favor is adding to something that is naturally happening. So favor is an aid to what's naturally happening, being good to someone.

So if all the goodness you do is when someone is in a wrong, then you are being merciful. Or someone is suffering and going through pain or whatever and then you show goodness towards the person, you are being merciful to the person, you are compassionate towards the person. You might express feelings of pity and all. But if independent of whatever the person is going through, you just show so much graciousness towards the person, then the person has your favor of benevolence.

## An Illustration of Favour

Let me use an illustration. Assuming you find out, oh, someone is in need. Let's use something financial now. Someone is in need. Person is in a fix. The person needs someone to come through. Maybe a lot of bills are coming up. So the person needs to clear some bills and then you find that like oh my god like how much is that? Then you send it to the person and you clear the bills for the person. You know it's debatable whether that's favor in the fullest sense of the word because I mean you might be moved by just the pain and you have to do something about it and all. But if independent of how wealthy the person is you just always have it in heart to show graciousness towards the person, you send in gifts, you send maybe money whatever, then that's a different word and that's a different kind of play and that's exactly what we are talking about here — the active proactive working of God's goodness alongside what is done in the natural for the good of someone or for the fulfillment of purpose.

That's the best way I would probably be able to describe it for now. But that's what the word itself means: to be good, to be gracious, to be benevolent, to give a gift. So it's not just showing mercy or compassion, it is just graciousness, just being good, like going out of your way to just show a lot of goodness towards someone. That's the idea of the word favor.
