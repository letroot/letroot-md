# Healing in the Newness of Life - Part 19

## Summary
Pastor Akim continues by explaining how healing is effected through [[Faith]] while also clarifying the role of natural means inside God's created order. He argues that using rest, food, medicine, or surgery is not a denial of faith but a lawful use of the [[Cosmos]] for life and righteousness. From there, he begins tightening the definition of faith, showing that the issue is not mere mental agreement that God can heal, but a maintained stand that refuses to drift into doubt or unbelief.

## Key Insights
* **The Created Order Is Not the Enemy**: [[Natural Healing]], medicine, and practical bodily care belong to God's world and can be rightly harnessed for good.
* **Faith Is More Than Agreement**: It is possible to believe in principle that God heals and still fail to stand steadily in that truth when circumstances press back.
* **Jesus Taught Faith Explicitly**: Pastor Akim points to [[Matthew 17]], [[Matthew 21]], [[Mark 11]], and [[Luke 17]] to show that Jesus did not merely perform miracles; He also instructed people in the workings of faith.
* **Do Not Let Circumstances Define the Stand**: The pressure of symptoms, delay, or contrary evidence can move a person from conviction into wavering.
* **Believing and Receiving Are Connected**: In [[Mark 11]], the command is not merely to avoid doubt but to actively believe that one receives.
* **The Faith Question Is Practical**: The main issue is whether one remains established in the promise, not whether one once nodded in agreement with it.

## Coach's Corner
**On Founder Conviction vs Founder Stability:**
Daniel, a lot of founders have "belief" in the abstract. They say they believe in their product, believe in the market, believe in the mission. But abstract belief is cheap. The real question is whether that belief survives friction. Can it survive a slow week? A buggy launch? A flat conversion rate? A difficult customer call? That is where this episode hits hard. In business, as in the teaching, the issue is often not initial conviction but maintained position.

**On Using Reality Without Worshipping It:**
There is a mature founder posture here: you look at reality without bowing to it. You examine churn, but you do not let churn write your identity. You study the bug report, but you do not conclude from one bug that the whole company is broken. You use the natural means available—analytics, feedback, strategy, design, sleep, structure—without becoming psychologically enslaved to them.

**On Standing Through Pressure:**
The habit you need is not emotional hype. It is disciplined steadiness. That means continuing to do the right things long enough for the signal to emerge. Founders often abandon a sound path because they expected emotional certainty to carry them. It won't. Systems, repetition, and deliberate thinking are what keep belief from collapsing under pressure.
