## Healing in the Newness of Life

We're still studying healing in the newness of life and we are approaching the end of this teaching. One thing we have done is to establish healing as available today to us in the newness of life. And we did that by showing us what the life that the Lord Jesus has given to us is all about.

It is the life that God originally intended for man at the beginning which would be fully effected at the end. What we then saw at that juncture was that there are some realities that are available in that life but would not be effected until the end, particularly immortality and incorruption. So immortality for our body and incorruption in matter would only be effected at the end.

The question we then have to ask is what is available to us relative to our body and relative to matter or the cosmos today. Now for our body we have been answering that we have seen that healing is what is available to us in our bodies today. And we saw that by seeing how the same Spirit that effects immortality at the end is the same Spirit that is today affecting healing in our bodies.

## Healing by the Power of the Spirit

That is particularly clear from the ministry of the Lord Jesus. If you look at the ministry of the Lord Jesus, we very often saw him heal the sick, but he particularly mentioned that it was being done by the power of the Spirit. Matthew 12:28, Luke 5:17, the power of the Lord was present to heal, Luke 8, I believe, the woman who was healed of the issue of blood, power went out of him and he healed her, Mark 5 as well.

So it was very clear that this was happening by the power of the Spirit. Now because we've seen that healing is available today in the newness of life, the real question we had to answer is how is it effected now. Of course, it is not going to be effected by the Lord at his own discretion. Per what we saw within God's word, it is effected by us, and it is effected by faith.

Don't forget that the value of this teaching is still the context of our walk in God's purpose. And whatever God makes available to us, he considers it important, necessary for our walk in his purpose. It means that invariably in our walk in his purpose we would need to either receive healing for ourselves or minister the same to others.

## Who Can Effect Healing

And that's why we must be taught. We must be aware of what is available to us as touching healing in the newness of life. For example, in your spiritual assignment, you find yourself having to minister to others, but sometimes for yourself you might have situations where you respond to certain sickness or disease by faith in the healing power of God.

And there might be times where even some conditions or situations can only be resolved by the healing power of God. That makes it all the more important that we pay attention to this. So healing is effected by men on earth today, and it is effected by faith.

We saw any person can minister or effect healing. The recipient can receive the healing by their faith. The minister can effect the healing by their faith. A third party's faith can be at play or can be the receiving faith for another person's healing.

That was seen, for example, in the case of the centurion's servant. Jesus had not seen such great faith, not in Israel. It was seen in the case of the Syrophoenician woman, and he said, "Woman, great is your faith," and told her, "Your daughter is made whole," and she was made whole from that very hour.

So very consistently we have this sense that the third party's faith, the minister's faith, and the recipient's faith all play a role and can be responsible, sometimes exclusively, for healing being effected. That means we don't have the room to play the blame game when it comes to faith for healing and say, "Oh, it's your fault," or it's anybody's fault. We all have to take the responsibility to minister for our good.

In fact, the reason why we see statements like in James where if anyone is sick they should call the elder is that they are expecting from the minister a greater degree of responsibility in terms of faith for healing. And that must be on our minds lest we start to throw blames at people rather than take responsibility both for their growth in faith and to minister healing to them.

## Faith as Reckoning and Consciousness

Now all that said, since we have seen that faith is what effects healing, the question has been, all right, how do we need to understand this faith that affects healing? And what we have seen is that the faith that affects healing is not merely faith as conviction, something we have studied way earlier on the podcast. It is rather faith as reckoning or faith as consciousness.

It's this faith that is measurable. Conviction is usually not measurable. Sometimes we interplay these words. You might say, "Oh, that guy's conviction is very strong," but you are probably not just talking about the conviction.

Well, it depends. It might be that on an issue he has more evidence and so on, so that might be valid. But conviction is usually not measurable. It is when we are speaking to reckoning or we are speaking to consciousness that we can be talking about the degrees of the same, how conscious or how much reckoning one has of a particular reality.

So we may all agree that Christ is in us and we may not be as conscious of it as the other. In fact, there are those who know, and maybe the very last time they really thought about it was a year ago. That shows you that we can vary in consciousness even when we are very similar in conviction.

And the faith that effects healing is faith as consciousness and not faith as conviction. At salvation we receive by faith as conviction. We see the truth of what Christ has done and we believe and we are saved. But when it comes to our walk, it's usually a matter of the state of our consciousness because that's how God designed us to walk.

## Faith, Freedom, and Staying Fixed

Right now, you might want to walk in love and the next minute you don't want to walk in love, and it's your choice. So faith is how we are existentially designed to walk. It is how we are existentially designed to receive things as well. Faith is not an order. We have had to say that over and again. It is just God's design because he wants us to be free agents.

That means we have free will to decide what we want and what we don't want. And faith as reckoning or faith as consciousness is what receives healing. It is also what we see at play in many miracles.

You remember we looked at the instance of Peter walking on water. One time he was in faith. Now of course walking on water is a discretionary miracle, unlike healing that is naturally available. So the Lord was walking on water. He wasn't walking on water all the time. This is a discretionary miracle.

Maybe some other time on the podcast we'll look at it, but there will be teachings on the pleros.org site that address that. I think in the Pleros Perfecting Course on the supernatural we look at gifts of the Spirit, discretionary, versus what is just readily available.

So the Lord was walking on water. Peter said, "Lord, if you're the one, call me on to walk on water as well." And he starts to walk on water and he's in faith. Then the winds get boisterous, everything gets tough, and Peter was afraid. That's what we are told. And the Lord said, "Oh you of little faith, why did you doubt?"

So clearly we can see that there's this spectrum of faith where one can be in faith and in the very same breath get into doubt. And this is a huge issue when it comes to people walking in all the realities of the newness of life, particularly healing. They can tell you, "But I believe that God can heal, so what's the problem?" But you could even hear some of their conversations and what they are saying, and you can tell that they misunderstand the place of staying firm in faith to the end, till they effect their healing.

## Examples of Wavering and Stability

And that's why it's important that we are rightly taught on this matter. Then we can have the similar faith to many of the folks we saw in scripture, but even more as new creations. For example, the woman who had the issue of blood, she wasn't saying different things. She was fixed on what she was saying: "If I see the Lord and I touch him, I'm going to be healed."

For the new creation we should be so fixed that, "I know the healing power of God works in my body and I am healed." We must get fixed. We shouldn't be oscillating. We shouldn't be speaking from both sides of our mouth or, as James says, we shouldn't be wavering in faith. We should be fixed in faith.

So we have looked at several examples of this where we saw that both faith and unbelief can be on a spectrum because we are not talking about conviction here, when someone rejects or accepts something. We are talking about this reckoning, this consciousness, this degree.

And we saw that in Hebrews 3 when unbelief was spoken of and it was said you can harden your heart into unbelief. That's the process. Even when unbelief was spoken of in Matthew 17, the Lord says they were perverse. That means they had turned away from that which was right to what was wrong.

I was showing us that there's a parallel use of perverse with repentance. Repentance, you turn from something negative to positive. Perversion, you turn from something positive to negative. And so this is something so important, that we are not changing our mind on what we have believed, on what we are fixed about.

If we can see these respectable, great men of God, like the apostles, being guilty of unbelief, this change of mind in the spectrum of their faith relative to a particular issue, we also must be guarded in our heart. Jairus's daughter was also tempted with the same.

## Faith Can Grow

So now I want to just go straight to the point. How are we going to be strong in faith and full of faith to receive or minister healing? How do we stay strong and full of faith to receive or minister healing? One thing is clear: faith can grow.

Faith grows, and there are several texts of scripture to the effect. Faith can be in degrees. There are also several texts of scripture to that effect. When Jesus speaks of "O you of little faith," you are already seeing the sense of faith in degrees, which is not the conviction changing but the reckoning or the consciousness changing.

Someone can become afraid who was initially full of faith, and Jesus will say, "Why did you doubt? You have little faith." It can be in degrees. So one can be weak in faith. We see an example of that with Abraham, that he was not weak in faith by not considering the natural state of things but by giving glory to God.

But he put his attention on God's power and then he was full of faith and he received. So faith can grow. The other text we can look at for that as well is 2 Thessalonians 1:3. Paul says he hears that their faith grows exceedingly.

Interestingly, in 1 Thessalonians 3:8, he said he wanted to see their faith so that he can perfect what is lacking in their faith. He wanted to see their faith so he can perfect what is lacking in their faith. You can read that yourself, 1 Thessalonians 3, all the way from verse 6 to verse 10.

In fact, after he said that, he said, "I still want to see you. I heard that you are doing well in your faith and I'm so glad, but I still want to see you to perfect what is lacking in your faith." So one can be excelling in one area in their faith and they can be lacking in another area.

So again, this is relative to our faith stand. Our faith stand can be strong or weak, can be full, or can be in unbelief, or you are in faith. You can be of little faith. So the degrees apply to this second kind of faith, if we may use that word, unlike the first kind of faith where we are just simply talking about conviction.

## Abraham and What We Consider

Romans chapter 4 and verse 19. What I like about this text is that he's also talking about healing. And I think it is of God's providence that we have Paul say this in the epistles to us.

So in Romans 4:19 Paul says, "And not being weak in faith," that's talking about Abraham, "he did not consider his body, already dead, since he was about 100 years old." So if you need healing, if you are going through a contrary situation, something has gone wrong, there is any form of death or sickness or disease, Abraham knows about that.

And you know Abraham was even natural. This wasn't a case of something being wrong. This is just his old age. His body was already dead, and also the deadness of Sarah's womb.

He did not consider that. So you have this natural situation going on. You are not disputing it, but you are not focusing on it. That word consider is katanoeo, which is to observe. In fact, the prefix means you are fully observing. So you are not putting your focus and attention on that.

You can note in every place where people get perverse in their faith, they change in faith into unbelief, they were putting attention on something else in the natural. For example, Peter had the boisterous wind, the tumultuous waters. Jairus, relative to his daughter, he was already in faith. He said when he lays hands on her she will live, that if she was sick then she would be healed and she would live.

But when he heard about her death, the Lord already suspected he was going to go into doubt and unbelief. And he says, "Fear not." Again, that's because of what he has observed. "Fear not, only believe."

So Abraham did not consider his body. So the first thing we see about being strong and full of faith for healing is what we are paying attention to because that's depositing on our heart. That's changing the state of our stand, the spectrum where we are in our faith. It can cause us to waver.
