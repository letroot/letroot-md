# Healing in the Newness of Life - Part 20

## Summary
This episode sharpens the distinction between simple belief that God can heal and the stronger, sustained [[Faith]] that actually effects healing. Pastor Akim leans on [[Romans 4]] and [[Hebrews 3]] to show that faith can be weak or strong, and that wavering can harden into [[Unbelief]]. He insists that Jesus Himself ministered healing by faith, so Christians must read the Gospels honestly and learn from both His works and His explanations.

## Key Insights
* **Faith Has Degrees**: Scripture speaks of being weak in faith, strong in faith, full of faith, or moving into unbelief. That means faith is not treated as a flat yes-or-no category.
* **Abraham as the Model**: [[Abraham]] did not ignore reality by pretending his body was young; rather, he refused to let visible weakness overturn God's promise.
* **Wavering Opens the Door to Unbelief**: Pastor Akim frames unbelief not merely as blatant rejection but as the direction one moves in when one keeps wavering.
* **The Heart Can Harden**: [[Hebrews 3]] is used to show that unbelief can become settled in the heart, not just momentary mental noise.
* **Jesus Was a Faith Minister**: The teaching stresses that Jesus did not heal by some hidden mechanism unrelated to faith; His own words present faith as central.
* **Read the Text Honestly**: If the Gospels repeatedly tie healing to faith, then Christian teaching should not explain faith away because of bad experiences or disappointment.

## Coach's Corner
**On Drift Becoming Identity:**
Daniel, most business failure does not begin with a dramatic collapse. It begins with drift. A missed week. A little discouragement. A subtle surrender of standards. A growing habit of interpreting every setback as final. This episode's warning about wavering hardening into unbelief maps directly onto founder psychology. If you repeatedly let disappointment redefine what is possible, that drift can become your operating mindset.

**On Protecting the Inner Core:**
A founder's deepest asset is not just skill. It is the inner posture from which he works. If your inner posture hardens into cynicism, fear, or learned helplessness, even good opportunities will start bouncing off you. You will under-ask, under-ship, under-follow-up, and under-recover. Guard the heart not in a vague religious sense, but in the practical sense of noticing what repeated disappointment is teaching you to believe.

**On Honesty Without Capitulation:**
The Abraham point is useful here. He did not deny the facts; he denied those facts the final word. That's mature leadership. You should look at the burn rate, the churn number, the weak launch, the code mess, and the low response rate clearly. But clarity is not capitulation. Mature founders stay sober about reality while remaining anchored to the next obedient move.

## Action Items
* [ ] **Name your drift pattern**: Write down the recurring thought pattern that makes you retreat when progress feels slow.
