Welcome to Pleros podcast. You are about to be blessed by the teaching ministry of Pastor Akim. It is going to be an enlightening time in God's word, renewing your mind and transforming your life to fulfill God's purpose. We pray for you that you are established in all the truths of God's word taught today. Let's get right into it. 

All right, welcome back to the Pleros podcast. This is your daily dose of God's word transforming you to fulfill God's purpose for your life. Very soon we are winding down this teaching on the Hidden Man. I believe this episode and maybe one more, and afterwards we will look at developing the Hidden Man, or the inner man, because we want to make clearer emphasis on some things than we did when we were looking at Faith Stand. But for now, I want to show us more text to strongly establish the reality of the hidden man and to help us turn our focus and attention to it to address it so that we can fully reflect all that God wants. God wants us to reflect; He wants us to come to the perfect man in our hidden man. He wants us to fully reflect the reality found in our spirit man, in our hidden man. That would be another way of saying it. 

## The Hidden Man as the Basis of Christian Conduct

I have seen that in the final analysis, what accounts for our work and our pursuits and fulfillment of God's purpose is the hidden man. It is not the spirit nature we have; it is not just the nature we have in Christ. We have to have that to be able to see the transformation of our inner man to walk as God wants us to, but that is not what accounts for our work. Whatever we see in a person's work is a product of their mind. And we are particularly talking about the spirit of their mind. 

We have seen that Peter is the one that described this part of our mind that we're talking about as the hidden man. He told the women who were married to husbands who did not believe the gospel to be mindful of their conduct so that they can attract them to the message for their salvation. In addressing how they can have that conduct he wants them to have, he told them: do not focus only on adorning or beautifying or cleaning your outward man, such as arranging your hair, wearing clothes, or your jewelry. Rather, he told them to pay attention to the hidden man of the heart and to adorn that part of them with the incorruptible beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit. 

## Adorning the Mind with Spiritual Realities

They are taking of their spirits and adorning on their mind the reality of their spirit. For example, there is love in their spirits, there is meekness and humility in their spirit, and they are doing the same on their mind. In their prayer life, their faith stand, their confession, their walk—anytime they just affirm, "No, I'm a new creation, I'm a love being in Christ"—what they are doing is adorning themselves, refreshing themselves. When we get to developing the inner man, that is where I want to take more time on exactly what we do there. I referenced it in Faith Stand, but I want us to look more directly at some text and also where we might be helped by the baptism of the Holy Ghost and the ability to pray in tongues in that regard. It might help you have clarity about why that is beneficial to you as well. 

Having said that, Peter said, "I want to adorn this part of you. I want you to adorn it with realities that are found in your spirit." And he's not talking about adorning something on your body because the outward man calls this one the hidden man of the heart. So there is an adorning that should happen on our mind of our spirit. 

## Strengthening the Inner Man for Christ's Dwelling

Paul also makes a reference to that in Ephesians 3:16. He wants us to be strengthened via our spirit, or via our spirit in union with the Spirit of God given to us; he wants us to strengthen that inner man. In fact, in Ephesians chapter 3, which is in verse 17, you see the result of that. It says, "I want you to be strengthened with might by the Spirit in the inner man, that Christ may dwell in your heart." Can you see that? You see the same issue: Christ dwells in your heart through faith. This cannot be salvific faith because, of course, Christ was already dwelling in their heart by faith. 

If you read the earlier portion of Ephesians, even a text like Ephesians 1:23 already says the body of Christ is the fullness of Christ dwelling all in all. That means He is dwelling in all His measure in every person—the fullness of Him that filleth all persons in all its measure. How do we know that? Because you'll find several texts, some in Ephesians like Ephesians 3:20 and one in Colossians, where we say you are filled with all the fullness of God. So you're already saying fullness, but use "all the fullness." It is like being emphatic and absolute. You entirely have what is of God's nature. 

## The Measure of the Stature of Fullness

He says the church is the fullness of Christ filling all persons in all its measures, literally, because the word fullness generally might just be seen as the nature, but putting "all the fullness" would then be clear to say like absolutely nothing is missing in the nature. Not just that you do have the nature; you completely do have the nature; you have all the fullness of God. 

So here, when he says that he wants us to be strengthened with might through the Spirit in our inner man that Christ will dwell in our hearts by faith, he's talking about the same thing he said in Ephesians 4:13, which is you come to the perfect man, the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ. This growth happens, this strengthening happens, this adorning happens so much so that you are visibly, simply walking and living Christ. You are a living, breathing Christ. This is premised on one, your salvation—your spirit has been changed—and two, your growth, first by knowledge, but we are seeing directly by faith stand, Christ will dwell in your heart through faith. 

I also hope that when we look through a number of these things, you realize that the bulk of the epistles are focused on this issue of our growth. They are really helping us to grow to become stronger, to walk as the Lord would have us do. They are not doing something different from the Old Testament text. Whilst we have different forms of literature at play, whenever you see the Old Testament text in its more expository form, you see the same type of emphasis, maybe like Joshua 1:8 or in Deuteronomy, that are speaking to the way our growth happens. 

## Defining the Soul and the Spirit of the Mind

In Ephesians 3, we have seen it mentioned as the inner man. In Ephesians 4, he talks about us being the perfect man by growth, by the work of the church. Of course, he is pointing to the hidden man or inner man becoming the perfect man, which is reflecting Christ fully, the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ. After this, he says, "I don't want you to walk like the rest of the Gentiles do," which is because of their mind. He says, "I want you to be renewed in the spirit of your mind." So another description of this hidden man or inner man is the spirit of your mind. 

As we were concluding the last episode, we talked about the hidden man or inner man being described as soul. And we saw that in Philippians chapter 2:19 downward. The hidden man or the inner man can be described as soul. Paul said Timothy is likeminded with me. He wasn't just saying Timothy shares the same knowledge with me; he actually used that word—Timothy is of "equal soul" with me. 

Soul is a very big word. If you remember when we studied on the mind of man, soul can refer to the entity of the human person which cuts across the inward and outward man. Both your spirit and body can be called your soul. One of the texts that makes this obvious is Hebrews chapter 4:12, when he talked about the word of God being living and powerful, sharper than any sword, piercing even unto dividing of soul and spirit, and of joints and marrow, and is a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart. 

The way that was framed, the writer was making the comparison between the whole and the part. Notice bone and marrow: there is the bone, but the marrow is inside the bone. Then when he says the thoughts and intents of the heart, it is in the plethora of our thoughts that the conclusion happens—the intent, your decision from your reasoning. Your thoughts, then your decision; your deliberation, then your decision; the intent of the heart. Then he used that same framework and said "soul and spirit." He was in that text making spirit a sub-part of the entire life that we have. 

## Working from the Soul in Natural Assignments

When we think of soul right now, many people think of soul automatically as mind, but within the scriptures it wasn't always that way. Soul is more of the entire person, and then both the body and the spirit are parts of a living person. He has a spirit, he has a body, and that makes up that person. For example, "three thousand souls were saved." That is not just three thousand spirits or three thousand minds; it's just three thousand persons—spirits and bodies—which includes the mind with the spirit and the brain with the body. They are saved persons. 

But there are times where the use of soul, when examined—like when he says Timothy is likeminded with me or of equal soul—means that the kind of life he's expressing is the same as mine. He is speaking precisely to something else as the soul. Let me get to the other text so it becomes clearer. The hidden man can also be described as soul because soul is a dynamic word. That's all I'm pointing out to us. 

Let's see Ephesians chapter 6 and Colossians 3. In Ephesians chapter 6, when Paul was encouraging the saints on their natural assignment, he was encouraging the ones that are like employees. In this case, he calls them bondservants. He also speaks to the masters because we are all under the lordship of Christ in our natural assignment. He told the bondservants to obey their masters according to the flesh with fear and trembling, in sincerity of heart as to Christ, not with eye service as men-pleasers, but as bondservants of Christ, doing the will of God from the heart. That last phrase, "from the heart," is actually the word for soul as well. So we are doing the will of God out of the soul. 

## Renewing the Spirit of the Mind for Effectiveness

It is talking about doing it from this part of your mind that has been so developed, adorned, and strengthened. You have built yourself to function like this: you no longer see your natural assignments or your endeavors in the natural as something ordinary; you see it as what God has given to you to do. So you are doing it out of your soul, out of that reckoning or consciousness that this is what the Lord has given to you. 

That exact thought is found in Colossians 3:22-23: "Bondservants, obey in all things your masters according to the flesh, not with eye service as men-pleasers, but in sincerity of heart, fearing God. And whatever you do, do it heartily"—there are actually two separate words there—"do it out of your soul as to the Lord and not to men." That means he's saying don't just do it because I'm instructing it and doing it grudgingly. Do it because you are convinced. Not just convinced, but you have built yourself to that; you have presented yourself that way so that you have grown to that measure to be able to so function and present yourself like that. 

It is so important that we recognize there is this reality and that it was not ignored by the apostles. They recognized there is this part of us that we need to work on. Don't walk like the Gentiles in the futility of their mind; their understanding is darkened. Something has happened to your own mind. Number one, your mind has become enlightened; you now know the truth of the word, so you have the conviction of truth on your mind. But number two, something should have happened to the spirit of your mind. You have learned from Christ not only the truth, but that you should put off the old man and be renewed in the spirit of your mind and put on the new. 

## Conclusion and Next Steps

You didn't learn from Christ just to be convinced of what He reveals and teaches. You learn from Christ to renew the spirit of your mind. If we don't work on this reckoning or consciousness part of our mind—which is what defines the faith that makes for our work, the second kind of faith, the first kind being the salvific faith—if we don't work on this part of us, we are going to be ineffective in our Christian lives. 

You might find believers and you're like, "Why is this fellow walking like this?" It is because he has a lot of garbage in that part of his mind, or he still does after salvation, and that needs to be addressed. He needs to work on that hidden man.
