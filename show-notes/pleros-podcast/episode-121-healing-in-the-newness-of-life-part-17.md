# Healing in the Newness of Life - Part 17

## Summary
In this episode, Pastor Akim continues exploring supernatural healing in the newness of life, focusing deeply on the relationship between faith, doubt, and unbelief. By examining the tension between faith and doubt in instances like Peter walking on water and Abraham’s faith for a child, he illustrates how faith is not a static state but a spectrum that must be actively held and exercised. Healing is made available through redemption, but it requires the responsibility of active, unwavering faith to be fully realized.

## Key Insights
*   **The Spectrum of Faith**: Faith is not an all-or-nothing binary but exists on a spectrum where doubt and unbelief can creep in. As seen in [[Romans 4]], [[Abraham]] was strengthened in faith by refusing to waver or doubt in unbelief regarding God's promise.
*   **Supernatural Healing as Defense**: While [[Natural Healing]] is valid and necessary, [[Supernatural Healing]] in the [[Newness of Life]] should be the believer’s primary defense, joint response to sickness, and sometimes the only solution.
*   **The Dynamics of Doubt**: Examining [[Peter Walking on Water]], it is evident that Peter stepped out in genuine faith. However, his consciousness shifted to his environment (the boisterous wind), producing fear and doubt, leading Jesus to address him as having "little faith."
*   **Faith vs. Unbelief**: Unbelief often signifies outright rejection of truth (as in Jesus' hometown), but in the context of believers failing to minister healing (like the disciples in [[Matthew 17]]), it represents a failure to stand firm in faith when challenged by difficult circumstances.
*   **Growing Faith**: Faith increases by being put to use—whether standing firm in the place of prayer or exercising it in active ministry.

## Coach's Corner
**On Focus & Execution:**
Daniel, the lesson on Peter walking on the water is exactly what it feels like to build a SaaS. You stepped out of the "boat" of your developer career to build your own company. That took genuine faith. But as you build, the "winds" will get boisterous—slow growth, technical debt, or customer churn. If you shift your consciousness from your goal to the noise around you, doubt creeps in and execution stalls. Stay laser-focused on the vision and the inputs you control.

**On the Spectrum of Belief:**
Your conviction in your product might be strong, but your daily consciousness can falter. You can't let your emotions dictate your momentum. Like Abraham, who "did not consider his body already dead," you have to sometimes ignore the immediate negative metrics and trust the long-term compounding of your efforts. Strengthen your faith by putting it to work daily through consistent shipping and talking to customers.

*   [ ] **Audit your inputs**: Identify what "boisterous winds" (distractions, competitor news, negative thoughts) are pulling your focus away from executing your core SaaS strategy.
*   [ ] **Exercise your faith**: Take one bold action today that requires you to step out of your comfort zone, such as reaching out to a high-ticket prospect or launching a feature you've been sitting on.
