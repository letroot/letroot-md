## Abraham: Moving from Weak to Strong Faith

Let's look at one more text. Romans chapter 4. Talking about Abraham. We saw Abraham move from a point where he was weak in faith to becoming strong and fully persuaded. Romans chapter 4 and verse 19: "And not being weak in faith, he did not consider his own body already dead."

So "not being weak in faith." If you read the account of Abraham, you get the sense in which he's speaking. He's talking about Abraham refusing to be weak in faith. He did not consider his body. You could read the account yourself and know that there was a time Abraham was considering his body. He said, "How shall this be when I'm already this age?" He doubted. He laughed. At a point, his wife also laughed. But he had to get into faith.

"He did not waver at the promise of God through unbelief, but was strengthened in faith, giving glory to God." You see, thanksgiving plays that role. He was strengthened in faith, giving glory to God. And being fully convinced that what He had promised, He was able to perform.

So, he moved from weak faith to strong faith to full faith, if we could describe it that way. And this is something we ought to do no matter where you are. If you agree that the power of God is available maybe for healing or you know that you're a new creation. No matter how weak you are—now we're talking about your consciousness is weak even though your conviction is right—then you can just stay there and move from that weak faith to stronger faith all the way to full faith and start walking in all that God wants you to do.
