# Every Level of Marketing ($0, $10k, $100k, $1m, $10m)

## Summary
Grant Owen interviews Oren (of Oren Meets World) on a comprehensive marketing playbook spanning five budget levels. From zero-budget organic content and $10/day Meta ad validation through $100K/month creative-and-paid flywheels, million-dollar influencer-at-scale strategies, and unlimited-budget monoculture campaigns. Oren argues that organic content is the greatest arbitrage in marketing history, that empathy trumps creativity, and that velocity and bias-for-action matter more than brand polish at every level.

## Key Insights

### Level 1: $0 to $10K
* **[[Organic Content as Arbitrage]]**: In the history of marketing, you used to have to pay for every impression. Now TikTok and Instagram give you 300-500 views from post one. This is an outsized opportunity for any brand regardless of budget.
* **[[Validate on Organic, Scale on Meta]]**: Use organic social to test what resonates, then take winners into Meta ads starting at $10/day. You can validate a product for $300. The test campaign tells you two things: does Meta give it spend (engagement), and does it convert (revenue)?
* **[[Empathy Over Creativity]]**: Creativity is not what makes content work -- empathy is. Understanding what your customer wants to hear, what problems they have, and creating for that. Some of the best content is just talking about real things on a walk.
* **[[Create a Dummy Feed]]**: Follow who your customer follows. Build a faux feed. See what they digest, what they want from content. Then create from that mind state.
* **[[It Does Not Have to Be the Founder]]**: The biggest fallacy is that the founder must be on camera. Get an expert in the niche -- a dermatologist for skincare, a nurse for wellness. Their credibility often exceeds the founder's.

### Level 2: $10K to $100K
* **[[The Organic-Paid Flywheel]]**: At this level, the formula is simple: make assets, see what works on organic, push winners into the ad account, double what works. It is a creative issue and a math issue. You do not need brand guides, positioning decks, or trade shows.
* **[[Content Market Fit First]]**: You do not need to use your brand account. Build a separate account that provides value to your demographic. Opel had a girl on a whiteboard doing mental health tutorials that plugged their product at the end -- on an unrelated handle. Solve content-market fit before product-market fit.
* **[[Brand Risk Is the Biggest Threat to Brand Success]]**: Worrying about brand risk is the fastest way to fall behind. The permanent bias-for-action era means if you spend 90 days planning, you are too late. Move fast, iterate, and fix problems as they come.
* **[[Pod Structure]]**: A modern marketing team is pods: one analytical/social manager + one creative/creator. Do not make the creative person go to meetings. Scale by adding pods (2 creators + 1 social manager + 1 editor = pod one).

### Level 3: $100K to $1M
* **[[Influencer Strategy Most Get Wrong]]**: Most companies give influencers a script and product and hope for the best. The right approach: find creators under 20K who are about to pop, bet early, and build long-term relationships. Brands that sponsored creators early (before they blew up) still pay fraction rates.
* **[[Affiliate Scale on TikTok Shop]]**: Brands scaling on TikTok Shop have 3,000-5,000 affiliates managed by a 3-person team. The scale of operation possible even at small teams is staggering if you lean into it.
* **[[Gray-Market First Mover Advantage]]**: What is happening in the gray market (streaming gambling ads, clipping culture from [[Andrew Tate]], AI companies breaking terms of service) is what will be in the white market in six months. Study the fringe.

### Level 4: $1M+ Per Month
* **[[[[Ramp]] as a Model]]**: Ramp does influencer marketing well -- they spotted that female creators making intellectual content converted for their TAM, scaled into that niche with hundreds of creators, and now dominate a space their competitors are not even in.
* **[[Scale What Works, Then Monoculture]]**: At this level, campaigns anchor to cultural moments (Super Bowl, Olympics, Coachella). But the real unlock is connecting monoculture moments back to a funnel. The gap at big brands is running huge awareness plays with no downstream funnel.
* **[[Funnel Attachment]]**: Salesforce runs Super Bowl ads with celebrity talent that go nowhere. If they drove to an ebook or free course, they would capture email value per lead. The gap between big-brand awareness and direct-response funnels is the biggest missed opportunity.

### Level 5: Unlimited Budget
* **[[[[Cloud]] as the Gold Standard]]**: Cloud did influencer marketing (found a niche in Instagram Girlies that competitors were not in), scaled into hundreds of creators, then layered monoculture campaigns (the "Keep Thinking" launch, a Super Bowl follow-up). That is the multi-pronged playbook.
* **[[Campaign-Centric, Not Ad-Centric]]**: At unlimited budget, individual ads do not matter. What matters is the overarching campaign that ties together influencer, social, TV, retail, and experiential into one permeating idea.
* **[[Viral Infrastructure]]**: Build an infrastructure to capitalize on organic viral moments. When an influencer goes viral with your product, have a ready-made response system that amplifies it 100x.

## Coach's Corner

**On the $0-$10K Lesson for SaaS:** Daniel, if you are not posting organic content about your SaaS space right now, you are leaving free attention on the table. Start with customer pain points, not product features. Create a dummy feed of who your users follow. Make 50 short-form pieces before you spend a dollar on ads. The ones that perform are your ad creative.

**On Meta Validation:** $10/day is not a risk -- it is a diagnostic tool. Run a test campaign for 2 weeks. You will learn whether your creative resonates (Meta gives it spend) and whether it converts (you get revenue). If neither, you have a messaging problem, not a budget problem.

**On Founder-on-Camera Fallacy:** You do not need to be the face. If you are uncomfortable on camera, find someone who is. A user who loves your product, a technical advisor, even a hired creator reading your scripts. The empathy in the message matters more than who delivers it.

**On Bias for Action:** The biggest risk right now is not moving. Every 90 days spent planning is 90 days a competitor is learning from real data. Ship content, read the metrics, adjust. The framework is: create, post, measure, iterate.

**On Connecting Awareness to Funnel:** If you ever run a big campaign, always attach it to a funnel. Even at scale, the brands that capture leads (Cloud's UTM links, Salesforce's missed ebook opportunity) will outperform pure awareness plays.

## Action Items
*   [ ] **Build a dummy feed:** Create a new social account. Follow exactly who your target customer follows. Scroll for 15 minutes daily. Note themes, formats, and pain points.
*   [ ] **Create 20 pieces of organic content this week:** Short-form, empathy-driven, focused on customer problems. No product pitches -- just value.
*   [ ] **Launch a $10/day Meta test campaign:** Take your top-performing organic piece and run it as an ad. Track spend vs. conversion for 14 days.
*   [ ] **Audit your funnel attachment:** If you are running any awareness play, is there a clear next step (email capture, ebook, demo booking)? If not, add one.
