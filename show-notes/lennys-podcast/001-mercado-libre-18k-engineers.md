# Mercado Libre: 18k engineers, 30k deploys a day, & their own fleet of planes

## Summary
Sebastian Barrios, SVP of Engineering at [[Roblox]] and former Head of Product/Engineering at [[Mercado Libre]], shares insights into scaling one of Latin America's most valuable companies. With 18,000 engineers and 30,000 daily deployments, Mercado Libre operates a unique model where engineering and product functions are deeply integrated, minimizing dedicated product managers. The discussion covers their culture of risk-taking, user-centricity, pragmatic approach to technology trends like [[AI]] and [[Crypto]], and Sebastian's unconventional personal philosophy shaped by his upbringing. He also recounts personal stories, including a call from [[Steve Jobs]] about his app and building a successful mobile app company.

## Key Insights
*   **Integrated Engineering & Product Model**: [[Mercado Libre]] operates with a highly integrated engineering and product function, where technical leaders and engineers often fulfill the product management role. With 18,000 developers and fewer than 1,000 product managers, this model thrives on engineers' deep technical understanding combined with business and user needs. The company's leadership is highly technical and hands-on, fostering a "founder mode" mentality that prioritizes user experience over immediate revenue metrics.
*   **Scaling through Decentralization and Autonomy**: Despite its massive scale, [[Mercado Libre]] empowers teams with significant independence. They avoid rigid [[OKRs]], instead providing high-level objectives and trusting teams to "do whatever you think is best." This freedom, coupled with rapid deployment cycles (30,000 per day), enables high-speed execution and experimentation, balanced by candid [[Design Reviews]] and a clear shared vision.
*   **Culture of Calculated Risk-Taking**: The company fosters a culture where teams are encouraged to take risks and "make mistakes," provided these are not due to bad quality or system outages. Failures related to market readiness or initial product ideas are tolerated and even seen as opportunities for growth, reinforced by leadership's actions and promotion decisions.
*   **User Observation over Direct Feedback**: While many companies emphasize talking to users, [[Mercado Libre]] prioritizes observing users' actual behavior. This approach helps uncover unspoken needs and identifies where product assumptions clash with real-world usage, leading to more intuitive and effective solutions.
*   **Pragmatism and Deep Research in Tech Adoption**: [[Sebastian Barrios]] highlights a cautious approach to technology hype cycles (e.g., [[Blockchain]], [[Generative AI]]). Instead of blindly adopting trends, the focus is on deep research into fundamental technology, understanding its practical application, and leveraging it to solve concrete user problems, exemplified by their internal [[AI platform]] `Verie` which enables agents to combine existing microservices for new features.
*   **The [[Flywheel Effect]] of Market Leadership**: Being the top company in a region like Latin America creates a powerful [[Talent Flywheel]], attracting and retaining top talent who want to work on complex, impactful challenges. This allows the company to set its own path and build innovative solutions unique to its market.
*   **Importance of Personal [[Agency]] and Curiosity**: Sebastian's personal journey, marked by early entrepreneurial success and unconventional upbringing (his mother's "spy training"), underscores the value of cultivating [[Curiosity]] and [[Agency]]. These traits drive problem-solving, independent thinking, and a natural inclination to explore and build, aligning personal passion with professional impact.
*   **Intentional Information Diet**: Sebastian practices an intentional detachment from mainstream news, social media, and even common stimulants like coffee or music. This disciplined approach minimizes distractions and filters out negativity, allowing for focused thought and a clear mind, promoting deep work and curiosity-driven learning.

## Coach's Corner
Young man, building a [[SaaS business]] is a marathon, not a sprint, and Sebastian's journey offers profound lessons that extend beyond the technical. Let's distill these into actionable insights for your trajectory:

*   **Own Your Product, Engineer First**: When you're small, you *are* the product and engineering team. Embrace this. Sebastian's success at [[Mercado Libre]] with an engineer-led product model isn't just about scale; it's about deeply understanding the *art of the possible* with technology. Don't delegate your core product vision or technical feasibility studies too early. The closer you are to the code, the better you'll understand what's genuinely impactful and innovative for your users. This means being in the details, asking the "why is this pixel here?" questions, and ensuring your technical choices directly serve the user experience. Your SaaS will be stronger if you, as the founder, remain deeply technical and product-minded.

*   **Cultivate a Culture of Experimentation (Not Recklessness)**: Sebastian emphasizes empowering teams to make mistakes. As a young [[SaaS founder]], this means creating a psychological safe space for your early employees to try new features, test hypotheses, and even "fail fast." Distinguish between failures due to poor execution or quality (unacceptable) and failures due to market misalignment or an unproven idea (learning opportunities). Document these learnings. Your tiny team needs to be nimble, and fear of failure will stifle that agility. Encourage radical ideas, but always tie them back to clear, high-level objectives for your product's direction.

*   **Observe, Don't Just Ask: The True Voice of the User**: Everyone says "talk to your users." Sebastian goes deeper: *observe* them. This is critical for your SaaS. Set up screen recordings, user testing sessions, or even just watch over their shoulder. Users often can't articulate what they truly need, or they might describe a solution rather than a problem. Watching them interact with your product will reveal unspoken pain points, unexpected workflows, and areas of confusion far more effectively than any survey or interview. This direct observation will be your secret weapon for building intuitive features that truly resonate.

*   **Strategic Pragmatism in the Face of Hype**: The tech world is a constant barrage of new frameworks, AI trends, and marketing channels. Sebastian's approach to [[Crypto]] and [[AI]] is a masterclass in pragmatism. Before you pivot your entire SaaS to integrate the latest shiny object, ask: What is the fundamental problem this technology solves *for my specific users*? What are the true costs and benefits? Do deep research, understand the mechanics, and don't be swayed by the noise. Your resources are limited; allocate them to what genuinely provides value, not just what's popular.

*   **Harness Your Personal [[Agency]] & [[Curiosity]]**: Sebastian's upbringing instilled a profound sense of agency – the ability to solve problems and "just get things done." This is the entrepreneur's superpower. Don't wait for permission. See a problem, investigate, and build a solution. Combine this with genuine curiosity, not just in technology, but in your market, your users, and even adjacent industries. Your unique blend of curiosity will lead you down paths others overlook, helping you discover [[Founder-Market Fit]] and innovative solutions for your SaaS.

*   **Master the Information Diet**: In a world of infinite information, Sebastian's intentional detachment from news and social media is a powerful example. As a founder, your focus is your most valuable asset. Be ruthless about what enters your mental space. Curate your information sources to be directly relevant to your business, personal growth, and well-being. Avoiding the "bad news" cycle isn't ignorance; it's self-preservation and allows you to channel your energy into creation rather than consumption of negativity.

*   **The Power of the Weekly "State of Me"**: Adopt Sebastian's practice (or [[Lenny Rachitsky]]'s "State of Me" email). This isn't just for your manager; it's a vital self-reflection and communication tool for you as a founder. Every week, document:
    1.  **What you accomplished:** This builds momentum and a sense of progress.
    2.  **Your next priorities:** This forces clarity and strategic alignment.
    3.  **Blockers:** Crucially, this identifies where you need help from co-founders, advisors, or your team.
    This structured reflection can save countless hours and help you course-correct proactively.

## Action Items
*   [ ] **Implement weekly "State of the Team" updates**: Structure a recurring email or internal post for your team (or even just for yourself if solo) to share what they got done, next priorities, and blockers, mirroring Sebastian's practice.
*   [ ] **Conduct direct user observation sessions**: Schedule at least three 30-minute sessions this month to *watch* users interact with your [[SaaS product]] without intervention, noting any points of confusion or unexpected behavior.
*   [ ] **Research a current tech "hype" deeply**: Choose one trending technology (e.g., a specific [[AI framework]], a new marketing channel) and spend 4-8 hours researching its core fundamentals, practical applications for your [[SaaS]], and potential pitfalls, before committing any significant resources.
*   [ ] **Define your SaaS product's "high-level objectives"**: Articulate the 3-5 core strategic goals for your product for the next 6-12 months. These should provide direction without prescribing specific features, allowing your team (or yourself) flexibility.
*   [ ] **Evaluate your approach to product ownership**: Consider how you can empower your engineers (or yourself, if solo) with more product ownership, blurring the lines between engineering and product management, inspired by [[Mercado Libre]]'s model.
*   [ ] **Audit your information consumption**: Identify one area (e.g., a news source, a social media feed) that consistently drains your energy or distracts you, and intentionally reduce or eliminate its influence for a week.
