# Are You Even Trying

## Summary

This short clip hammers one thesis: most people dramatically underestimate the volume of effort required to see results. The speaker shares a personal story about flyer marketing -- he put out 300 flyers and got one call (about a dinged Mercedes); his mentor tested with 5,000 per batch and then scaled to 5,000 per day (150,000 per month). The gap between 300 and 150,000 is the gap between failure and success. The same principle applies to content creation, sales, and every other domain: the winners are not doing 2x or 3x more -- they are doing 300x to 10,000x more. And the biggest delay for beginners is the time wasted searching for an easier way that does not exist.

## Key Insights

- **Volume is the Variable**: [[Volume]]. The difference between success and failure is rarely talent or strategy -- it is the raw number of actions taken per unit of time. People talk about doing; winners do. The discrepancy in volume between average performers and top performers is not 2-3x, it is 300x-10,000x.
- **The Flyer Story -- Test Size Matters**: [[Test Size]]. When the speaker put out 300 flyers, it was a statistically meaningless test. His mentor tested with 5,000 per batch, then scaled to 5,000 per day (150,000/month). You cannot determine whether something works from a sample size of 300.
- **Content Volume as Compound Advantage**: [[Content Volume]]. The speaker's team produces 450 pieces of content per week. Someone proud of 90 days of daily posting (90 posts) has produced what he does in two days. The gap is not incremental; it is orders of magnitude.
- **Sales Rep Volume Discrepancy**: [[Sales Volume]]. A beginner who has taken 400 live calls over 90 days compares themselves to a closer who has closed 4,000 deals (not calls -- deals). At a 30% close rate, that closer has taken roughly 12,000 calls -- 30x the beginner's volume. That is why they are better.
- **The Hard Way is the Only Way**: [[No Easy Way]]. The biggest delay for beginners is the time spent searching for an easier method. People discover what to do, then spend a long time trying to find a shortcut, and only start the real work -- the hard way -- after giving up on the shortcut that never existed.

## Coach's Corner

**On Volume and SaaS Growth:** Daniel, this is the uncomfortable truth for early-stage SaaS. You might be writing great code, crafting thoughtful landing pages, and sending a handful of outbound messages -- but are you doing enough volume to even know if the channel works? If you are running 5 user interviews, that is 300 flyers. You need 5,000 before you can honestly say the approach does not work.

**On the Search for Easy:** The trap of "is there a shortcut?" is the single biggest time-waster for founders with technical backgrounds. You already know how to build. The temptation is to find a clever growth hack instead of just doing 10x the outreach. The hard way -- relentless, repetitive volume -- is the only way that compounds.

**On Content as Compounding Volume:** If you are not producing content at volume, you are invisible. 450 pieces per week sounds insane, but that is the benchmark of someone who has built a $100M+ business on the back of content. Even aiming for 10-20% of that output would put you ahead of 99% of SaaS founders.
