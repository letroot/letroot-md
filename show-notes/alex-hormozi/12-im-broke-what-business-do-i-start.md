# I'm Broke, What Business Do I Start?

## Summary

Alex Hormozi presents his complete five-step framework for going from zero to first customers. The system covers: (1) picking your "What" via the Three P's (Pain, Profession, Passion), (2) narrowing your "Who" into a hyper-specific avatar, (3) articulating the "How" through the Value Equation (good stuff vs bad stuff), (4) combining everything into a one-sentence offer, and (5) adding a Unique Mechanism for differentiation. He also provides a dead-simple outreach system for landing your first five customers.

## Key Insights

* **The Three P's for Picking a Business**:
  * **Pain**: Something you personally overcame (e.g., a mom of nine kids creating a lunch-packing system).
  * **Profession**: A skill from your day job turned into consulting or teaching (e.g., a hospital dietitian teaching other dietitians how to bill insurance, earning nearly $1M/year with only 5,800 Instagram followers).
  * **Passion**: What you obsessively read, watch, or listen to in your free time (e.g., Hormozi reading T-Nation every night until his coworkers offered to fund his gym).

* **The "Best Bad Idea" Philosophy**: [[Iteration]] beats perfect selection. You won't pick right on the first try. Pick anything, get feedback, and make it "less wrong" over time until it becomes a good idea.

* **The Five-Frame Avatar**: Narrow down using at least three of: age, gender, profession, problems/pains, interests/passions. Example: "35-year-old male accountants who are bored at their jobs" is far more powerful than "people like me."

* **Specificity = Pricing Power**: The narrower your niche, the more premium your price. Time management PDF = $19. Time management for salespeople = $199. Time management for outbound sales reps in garden and power tools = $10,000. [[Niche Selection]] creates blue oceans.

* **The Value Equation (How Part 1 - The Good Stuff)**: [[Value Equation]]
  * How much **easier** their life becomes
  * How **guaranteed** the outcome is
  * How much **faster** they get results
  * How it feels to experience their **dream scenario**
  * Example: "Average gym makes $30K in 30 days using Gym Launch. Average gym owner makes $36K/year take-home. We help them fill their gym easier, faster, guaranteed."

* **The Pain Framework (How Part 2 - The Bad Stuff)**: You motivate people by either giving them more good stuff or taking away bad stuff. The more specific you describe their pain, the more it bypasses their ad blindness.
  * **Risk avoidance**: What negative outcomes do they fear?
  * **Speed contrast**: How slow is their current path vs. your fast path?
  * **Effort/sacrifice reversal**: What do they hate doing that they can stop? What do they love that they can keep?
  * Example: "Stop feeling your thighs chafing in the sun" beats "lose weight fast" because it describes a specific, visceral pain they can't ignore.

* **The Unique Mechanism**: [[Unique Mechanism]] — your proprietary process that differentiates you. It's the "vehicle" from their current pain to their dream outcome. Examples: P90X's "muscle confusion," Weight Watchers' "point system." Name it with alliteration or rhyme for memorability.

* **The One-Sentence Offer**: `I help [WHO] get [GOOD STUFF] without [BAD STUFF] through [UNIQUE MECHANISM].`
  * Basic: "I help 45-year-old women who just had kids get back into their high school jeans without giving up time with their family."
  * Advanced: "I help non-fiction business authors get on the Wall Street Journal bestseller list without looking cringe through the Author Authority System."

* **First Five Customers System**: Greet → Specific compliment → Insert your one-sentence offer → Ask if they know anyone who could benefit. Do this for 4 hours/day or until you reach 100 people.

## Coach's Corner

**On Niche Selection for Your SaaS:**

Daniel, Hormozi's framework is deceptively simple but brutally effective. The mistake most technical founders make is building for "everyone" because they fear narrowing too much. Hormozi's dietitian example proves you can earn $1M/year with 5,800 followers solving one hyper-specific problem. Your SaaS doesn't need a massive TAM initially — it needs a tiny, desperate market that lights up when they hear your offer.

**On Crafting Copy and Offers for Conversions:**

This is where Hormozi delivers gold. The [[Value Equation]] and [[Pain Framework]] are the foundation of all high-converting copy. Here's how to apply them:

* **Describe the pain with microscopic specificity**: Generic pain points ("save time," "make more money") get ignored. Specific pain points ("stop manually exporting CSVs every Friday at 6 PM when you'd rather be home") bypass psychological defenses. Interview 10 potential customers and ask: "What makes it painful specifically?" "Can you describe the experience when it's not right?" Their exact words become your copy.

* **Flip the Value Equation into your headlines**: Your landing page should scream the dream outcome and the speed/guarantee. Not "Project Management Software" but "Ship Features 3x Faster Without Late-Night Deployments." The good stuff (speed, certainty, ease, dream outcome) goes in headlines and above-the-fold. The bad stuff (risks avoided, sacrifices removed) goes in objection-handling sections and FAQs.

* **Use the "Keep It / Stop It" framework in your messaging**: What do they hate doing that your product eliminates? (Stop: "Stop writing the same boilerplate code."). What do they love doing that your product preserves? (Keep: "Keep your flexible work schedule without missing deadlines."). This directly addresses the effort/sacrifice side of the Value Equation.

* **Create your Unique Mechanism even if you're early**: You don't need a decade of IP. Your mechanism can be a 4-step onboarding sequence, a proprietary template library, or a unique integration workflow. Name it. "The [[Zero-to-Deploy]] Framework" or "The [[Customer-First]] Onboarding Method." It doesn't need to be revolutionary — it needs to be *named* and *framed* as the missing piece they need.

* **The One-Sentence Offer Test**: If you can't explain what you do in one sentence using Hormozi's formula, your positioning is too weak. Test variations on Twitter, in Slack communities, in cold DMs. The version that gets "Wait, that's exactly my problem" is your winner.

**On Getting Your First Five Customers:**

Don't over-engineer launch. Hormozi's outreach system (greet → compliment → offer → ask for referrals) works because it's human, not automated. As a technical founder, your instinct is to build a funnel. Don't. Spend 4 hours/day doing warm outreach in communities where your specific avatar hangs out. The feedback loop from real conversations will improve your product and copy faster than any A/B test.

**On Pricing Psychology:**

The narrower your who, the higher your price. If you're selling "project management for teams," you're competing with Notion and Asana at $10/month. If you're selling "project management for fractional CTOs managing 4+ client codebases," you can charge $200/month because the specificity signals expertise and the pain is acute.

## Action Items

* [ ] **Pick Your Three P's**: Write down one idea from each category (Pain, Profession, Passion). Which one do you know most intimately?
* [ ] **Define Your Avatar**: Use the five-frame method (age, gender, profession, pain, interest). Get to at least three descriptors. Fill a room in your mind.
* [ ] **Interview 5 Potential Customers**: Ask Hormozi's exact questions: "What are you struggling with? How long? What makes it painful? Describe the ideal experience. What do you hate doing? What do you love that you'd hate to give up?"
* [ ] **Write How Part 1 and How Part 2**: List all the good stuff (easier, guaranteed, faster, dream scenario) and all the bad stuff (risks, slowness, effort, sacrifices) for your specific avatar.
* [ ] **Draft Your One-Sentence Offer**: `I help [WHO] get [GOOD STUFF] without [BAD STUFF].` Test 5 variations in real conversations or Twitter posts.
* [ ] **Name Your Unique Mechanism**: Even if simple, give your process a proprietary name with alliteration or rhyme.
* [ ] **Do Warm Outreach**: Greet → Compliment → Offer → Referral ask. 4 hours/day or 100 people. Track responses.
* [ ] **Read** [$100M Offers](https://acquisition.com) by Alex Hormozi — the full expansion of the Value Equation and Unique Mechanism frameworks.
