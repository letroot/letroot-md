# Why You’re Always Busy but Never Productive

## Summary
A guest post by Sahil Bloom outlining a timeboxing framework that splits professional time into four distinct domains: Management, Creation, Consumption, and Ideation. Most professionals drown in Management Time, leading to burnout and superficial busyness. By utilizing timeboxing, one can contain Management Time and protect the other three categories to drive true progress and creativity.

## Key Insights
*   **Management Time**: Reactive work like emails, meetings, and calls. Highly productive if constrained, but defaults to a massive time-suck that values "movement over progress."
*   **Creation Time**: Focused work producing new ideas, solutions, and outputs (writing, coding, building). This is the engine of meaningful progress.
*   **Consumption Time (Forgotten Category #1)**: Absorbing information (reading, podcasts, studying) to fuel new ideas. Quality upstream input ensures quality downstream output.
*   **Ideation Time (Forgotten Category #2)**: Time for stillness, brainstorming, and reflection. Yields asymmetric opportunities via nonlinear, creative thinking.

## Action Points
*   [ ] **Audit Your Time**: Color-code your calendar for a week into Management, Creation, Consumption, and Ideation to see where your time actually goes.
*   [ ] **Constrain Management Time**: Put strict limits on emails and meetings (e.g., schedule 1-3 blocks per day) utilizing Parkinson's Law so they don't bleed into your whole day.
*   [ ] **Schedule-Sync**: Share your timeboxed calendar with managers and colleagues to protect your Creation Time and set boundaries on your availability.
*   [ ] **Start Small with the "Forgotten" Times**: Schedule at least one short block (e.g., 30 minutes) per week dedicated solely to Consumption and Ideation.
