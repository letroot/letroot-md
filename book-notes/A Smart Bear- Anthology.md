# A Smart Bear: Anthology

**Author:** Jason Cohen

## Key Highlights

### Positioning Around Value, Not Efficiency

> So the next time you want to formulate your product as a way to "save time" or "save money" or "be more efficient" … don't. Instead, figure out how your product creates value in the way your customer already measures value, and position your product as a way to accomplish that.

This is a critical insight for product positioning. Rather than framing a product in terms of abstract savings (time, money, efficiency), the better approach is to understand how your customer already measures value in their own domain — and position your product as a direct contribution to *that* metric. People don't buy efficiency; they buy outcomes they already care about.
