# Aggregation Theory

A business framework developed by Ben Thompson (Stratechery) that explains the dynamics of internet-era monopolies (like Google, Facebook, Amazon, Netflix).

## Core Principles
- **Zero Marginal Costs**: The cost of serving an additional user is practically zero.
- **Zero Distribution Costs**: The internet makes it free to distribute digital goods globally.
- **Owning Demand**: Because supply is unconstrained and distribution is free, the companies that create the best user experiences attract the most consumers. By controlling consumer demand, these "Aggregators" gain leverage over suppliers, creating a virtuous cycle where more users attract more suppliers, which in turn attracts even more users.

## Evolution in the AI Era
With the rise of generative AI, Aggregation Theory is being challenged by the return of marginal costs (and specifically, opportunity costs of compute). AI inference requires significant compute per query, meaning supply is no longer functionally infinite or free, forcing hyperscalers to make tradeoffs on who gets access to compute. However, the core principle of *owning demand* remains highly relevant—companies with the strongest consumer touchpoints can monetize effectively to fund the massive compute investments needed.
