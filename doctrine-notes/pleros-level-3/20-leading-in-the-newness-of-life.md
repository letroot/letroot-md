# Leading in the Newness of Life

Leading in the newness of life is built on the reality of God's purpose. If there is such a thing as God's purpose for our lives, then there is such a thing as fulfilling God's purpose. But we must also be honest enough to say that there is such a thing as not fulfilling God's purpose. In Scripture, we see individuals who fulfilled God's purpose, and we see those who did not. So the issue is not only that there is a purpose, but that there is also the question of knowing the leading of the Spirit and following the leading of the Spirit.

The young prophet who was sent to rebuke a king is an example of someone who knew God's leading but did not follow through with it. Balaam is another example that warns us that a person may even point to a supernatural experience and still be out of touch because of hardness of heart. So the subject of God's leading is not something we should ignore or handle carelessly. It matters because God's purpose matters.

## God's Purpose and Man's Freedom at the Beginning

The best place to understand the leading of God is at the beginning. In Genesis 2, when God put man in the garden and told him what he may eat and what he must not eat, we see the first clear instance of God's leading. What stands out immediately is that when God leads a man, that man still has the freedom to accept or reject that leading.

God said, in effect, that man may freely eat of the trees of the garden, but not of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil. That means God's leading did not remove man's liberty of choice. God's purpose was already in view. God made man for a reason, placed him in the garden for a reason, and wanted him to partake of life. Yet man still had to choose whether to walk in that purpose.

So when it comes to leading in the newness of life, two realities are always present. There is what God wants, and there is man's freedom to choose. God's purpose is good, and his leading is worth following. But following his leading involves two things: knowing what God is leading us to do, and choosing to do it.

## God Must Reveal What He Wants Us to Do

In Genesis, man would not have known what to do unless God told him. That shows us something very important: it is God's responsibility to reveal what he wants us to do. If God wants us to follow him, then he must make known his will, his purpose, and his leading.

Ephesians 1 says God has made known to us the mystery of his will. If God does not reveal his will, it remains a mystery. So the issue of leading is a matter of revelation. God must reveal what he wants, and he is faithful to do so. If he wants us to walk in his purpose and will judge us in relation to it, then he cannot be withholding the leading we need. God is faithful by nature. He will always reveal what he wants us to do.

That shifts the question. The main question is not whether God is revealing. The question is whether we are receiving what he has revealed and is revealing.

## God's Leading Is Always Truth

When we look through Scripture, we see a fundamental principle: God's leading is always truth. If God is not in truth, then there is no God at all. And if God is truly God, then whatever he leads anyone to do will necessarily be the truth.

Ephesians 1:13 calls the gospel the word of truth. Ephesians 4 shows that the message by which the saints are taught in the church is also the truth. Ephesians 4:24 speaks of the new man as created after God in righteousness and holiness of truth. So what God spoke at the beginning was truth. What he spoke in the gospel for salvation is truth. What the church is to keep teaching is truth. God's own nature is aligned with truth.

That means whatever God will lead anybody to do will be the truth. This is one of the greatest safeguards in the subject of leading. People often want the mechanics of how to sense or hear, but the deeper anchor is this: God's leading is always truth. That is what guards us from error.

Ephesians 5 says the fruit of the light is in all goodness, righteousness, and truth, finding out what is acceptable to the Lord. So discernment is not detached from truth. We find out what is acceptable to the Lord by finding out what aligns with goodness, righteousness, and truth.

## Leading Does Not Cancel Free Will

The reality of God's leading does not exclude the independence and freedom of our will. That has been the case from the beginning. God showed man what to do, and man was free to choose. Man did not choose what God wanted, and that is not a new problem.

Cain is an example. He was warned and still did otherwise. Adam at the start also missed God's purpose. The point is that the existence of God's leading does not remove the liberty to reject it. So the issue is not merely whether God is leading, but whether man will heed that leading.

There are also examples that warn us against self-will dressed up as spirituality. Sometimes people may want a particular outcome so badly that they become willing to accept anything that suits what they want. That is part of what we see in examples like Ahab and the four hundred prophets. It is possible to be misled. It is possible to have people around you saying, "Go ahead," and yet that not be the word of the Lord.

## The First Way God Reveals His Truth: Creation

Romans 1 shows us the first way God reveals his truth. What may be known of God is manifest in man because God has shown it to him. Paul says that since the creation of the world, God's invisible attributes are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made.

So the first way God communicates his truth is by creation, or the natural world. Creation is not incidental to this discussion. It is one of God's ordained means of making known the truth.

By creation we can know the reality of God. We can know the nature of God. We can know that something is wrong in the world, that there is contradiction, evil, or sin. We can also know that there is a need for redemption that only God can accomplish.

There are many other truths people can learn by observation and reason from creation, but in this context those four are primary. Creation tells us there is God, what his nature is like, that something is wrong, and that only God can ultimately provide the answer.

## The Second Way God Reveals His Truth: Biblical Revelation

There are truths that go beyond what can be known from creation alone. If redemption is something only God can do, then God must reveal it. That is where biblical revelation comes in.

First Corinthians 15 shows that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, was buried, and rose again the third day according to the Scriptures. So God had already been revealing redemption beforehand, and that revelation was fulfilled in the person of Christ. It was supernaturally validated in the resurrection and historically credible through witnesses.

Before there was a compiled Bible, there was already revelation concerning redemption. But now that revelation has become biblical revelation. God orchestrated that the Bible would be the authoritative document for his truth and revelation. It is from the Bible that we find out not only that there is God and that redemption was needed and accomplished, but also why he made us and what his purpose is.

So the second way God leads by truth is biblical revelation. By the Bible, we learn many things God wants us to know about his purpose, his plan, and our walk.

## Most of God's Leading Is Already Revealed

Much of the leading a believer needs in life has already been given by creation and by Scripture. In that sense, a great deal of God's leading is already available before we even begin to talk about supernatural leading in the individual sense.

God has already revealed his purpose for us. He has revealed sonship, fellowship with him, walking in his character, and the reality of inheritance. For many matters of life, he has already spoken in principle. If someone asks, "What should I do in my natural assignment?" there are often many things that have already been revealed: start from where you are, use the opportunities before you, and pursue lawful desires within the circumstances and means God has provided.

This helps us avoid unnecessary complication. There are matters where God's leading is that you choose responsibly within the boundaries he has already given.

## Truth Sets Boundaries Against Strange Leadings

God has already led us by his truth in creation and in the Bible, and that sets boundaries against error. There are things you should already know God cannot be saying.

God cannot tell you to do something that is plainly injurious to life. He cannot tell you to eat poison. He cannot tell a married man to commit adultery. There are things that are clearly outside goodness, righteousness, and truth. So we must not be expecting strange leadings that contradict what God has already revealed.

The more a person reasons properly on what God has already said in creation and in Scripture, the more accurately that person can walk with God. Poor reasoning on Scripture has cost many people right leading in life. Sometimes people call irresponsibility faith. They wait for God to say again what he has already said plainly. They want supernatural direction where discipline, honesty, diligence, or sound reasoning is what is already required.

That is not piety. It is irresponsibility. You cannot ignore what God has already said and then expect to go far in his purpose by waiting for another leading.

## Knowledge and Conviction of Truth Must Come First

If we are going to follow God's leading, the first thing we need is the knowledge and conviction of God's truth in nature. The second thing we need is the knowledge and conviction of God's truth in Scripture.

It is not enough merely to know these things. They must become conviction. In some cases they must become a settled consciousness and faith-stand. This foundational conviction becomes a boundary against error in supernatural leading.

Many believers want to make much of the ten percent that concerns supernatural details while neglecting the ninety percent that has already been revealed. But leading in the newness of life is not only about supernatural leading. We cannot ignore most of what God has already said and then wait for a small remaining portion. If we neglect what has already been revealed, we weaken our ability to receive accurately when supernatural direction is needed.

Paul in Acts 16 is an important example. He was not passive. He was already moving in obedience to what God had generally revealed: go into all the world and preach the gospel. While he was moving in that already-revealed direction, the Holy Spirit stopped him in a specific instance. So urgency in obeying revealed truth creates the context in which more specific leading can be properly received.

## Your Mind Must Be Trained to Receive God's Leading

Supernatural leading is built on the foundation of conviction in God's truth in nature and in Scripture. That foundation becomes a defense and a boundary against error. There is a kind of mind that is not strong enough to properly discern God's leading.

If your mind is not trained in God's word, it may not be able to discern what is acceptable to the Lord. Your mind is the receiver. Romans 1 shows that people can suppress the truth. The issue was not that God failed to reveal. The issue was that their minds were resisting what was revealed.

So part of being led by the Spirit is training your mind to be sincere, honest, and receptive to truth. A person can hear the word and already be fighting it inwardly. That is a kind of suppression of truth. Over time, that can produce a hardened or debased mind, a mind that is no longer in a healthy state to receive from God.

This is why you must guard your mind. Avoid things that harden the heart. Avoid gossip, corrosive criticism, and patterns that make you defensive against truth. Keep your heart pure. The equipping of the saints includes work on the mind, not only so that we may walk in the Spirit, but so that we may receive from God rightly.

If your mind is permissive, biased, or insincere, then what you call supernatural leading may only be your own distortion. But when your mind is trained by God's truth, you can often already judge a matter in line with truth before any extra revelatory detail comes. You can say, "Something is off here," or, "This is how this matter should be handled," because your mind has been formed by the truth.

You must have a righteous mind, a Bible mind, a truth mind. Train your mind to be receptive to God's leading.

## Supernatural Leading and the Inward Witness

After the foundation of truth in creation and Scripture has been laid, we can speak about supernatural leading. In the individual sense, God also reveals supernaturally what he wants a person to do. But even here, the concern is not only the experience itself, but whether the mind receiving it is properly trained.

There are different kinds of supernatural experiences. One important category is perception, not merely perception as information, but perception as a witness that approves or disapproves something. This is what many people call the inward witness.

That kind of witness does not necessarily give a speech or a vision. It gives a sense of approval or restraint. It says, in effect, "This is okay," or, "This is not okay." In that sense it is strong, because voices and visions may still require interpretation, but the inward witness often functions as a direct restraint or permission in the heart.

Acts 16 gives an example. Paul and his companions were forbidden by the Holy Spirit to preach the word in Asia. There was a real restraint, a forbidding witness. That kind of inward witness is a serious part of supernatural leading. But even there, the major concern remains this: the mind and heart must be trained by God's truth so that what is received is received accurately.
