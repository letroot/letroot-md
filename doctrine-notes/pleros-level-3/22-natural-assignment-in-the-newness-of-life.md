# Natural Assignment in the Newness of Life

One of the most important questions we need answered as human beings is the question, why are we here? We have answered that question many times as the church, and through the Pleros Perfecting Course. But that question can be more accurately described as: what is God's purpose? Once we have answered that there is God, and that he is the creator, then the reason we are here is determined by him. If there is a self-existent creator God, then the question becomes: what did he make this world for? The only way to know God's purpose is for him to tell us.

In Ephesians 1, God makes known to us the mystery of his will according to his good pleasure which he purposed in himself. That means we can find out what God's purpose is by finding out what his will is, what he wants, and what his good pleasure is, what he likes. Ephesians 1:5 tells us that he predestined us to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ to himself according to the good pleasure of his will. So what God predestined us to must be the good pleasure of his will. Adoption as sons there is sonship. Therefore, God's purpose is sonship.

## God's Purpose Is Sonship

Romans 8 explains sonship. We have received the Spirit of sonship whereby we cry, "Abba, Father." That means the moment you believe the gospel, you enter into God's purpose because he gives you the very Spirit of his purpose, the Spirit of sonship. Fulfilling it is another matter, and knowing it is another matter, but at the instance of faith in Christ for salvation, you have entered into God's purpose.

The Spirit bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God, and if children, then heirs of God and joint-heirs with Christ. So two realities make up sonship. First, we are children of God. Second, we are heirs of God.

To be a child of God means we have been reproduced by him. We have his nature. The implication is that we walk in fellowship with God, we walk in his nature and character, and we fellowship with one another as members of his family. God's purpose is relational at its core. He wants a family of his own. He wants us to interact with him, to fellowship with him, and to have friendship with him.

There is supposed to be vulnerability in that fellowship. We are to cry, "Abba, Father," with the familiarity of children. But we must not relate with an idol we made in our own image. If you go to God for real, he can console you, but he will still tell you the truth. We do not go to God merely to hear what we want to hear. We go to God for him to tell us the truth.

## Sonship Includes Being Heirs of God

If children, then heirs. God did not make us only to be his children, but also to be his heirs. All believers are made joint-heirs of everything God owns, but as individuals God also gives each person a portion, an assignment, an allotment, a responsibility.

There are three ways the Bible speaks of inheritance. First, God gives us himself, his Spirit. That is inheritance. Second, God gives us creation, the cosmos, what he has made. The meek inherit the earth, and Abraham is called heir of the world. Third, God gives us our individual assignment in his created world. In this third sense, inheritance is a portion, an allotment.

There are two assignments in that inheritance: a natural assignment and a spiritual assignment. The spiritual assignment concerns the mission of the church, the work of ministry, the preaching and teaching of the gospel, evangelism, and discipleship. But there is also a natural assignment, and God gives a natural assignment to every believer.

## Natural Assignment Is Part of God's Purpose

Colossians 3 is one of the clearest texts on this. Paul speaks to bondservants about obeying their masters according to the flesh, not with eye-service as men-pleasers, but in sincerity of heart, fearing God. Whatever you do, do it heartily, as to the Lord and not to men, knowing that from the Lord you will receive the reward of the inheritance, for you serve the Lord Christ.

This is not merely saying that natural work should resemble service to the Lord. It is saying that the believer's natural work is actually service to the Lord. That is why Paul calls it the reward of the inheritance. The portion of work you are doing is an allotment to you, and you receive the reward of that allotment from the Lord. Whether you are an employee or an employer makes no difference. When you are found serving somewhere, it is for the Lord.

That means the believer must have a purpose mindset in natural work. If you serve only according to the salary your boss pays you, you are not serving the Lord Christ and you have already capped yourself. The issue is faithfulness. You are to be maximally productive with what you have at your disposal, knowing that you serve the Lord Christ.

Colossians 4:1 also addresses masters: give your bondservants what is just and fair, knowing that you also have a Master in heaven. No one is exempt from the lordship of Christ in natural assignment. When it comes to our natural assignment, we are all under the lordship of Christ.

By natural assignment, we are talking about everything we do in the natural: academics, career, business, research, and other natural pursuits. We are not talking there about the mission of the church, but about the natural sphere of life. Whatever you are doing in the natural, you are serving the Lord Christ.

## Natural Assignment Is the Will of God

Ephesians 6 makes the same point. Bondservants are told to obey their masters according to the flesh, not with eye-service as men-pleasers, but as bondservants of Christ, doing the will of God from the heart, with goodwill doing service as to the Lord and not to men, knowing that whatever good anyone does, he will receive the same from the Lord, whether slave or free.

So natural assignment is called the will of God, and the person doing it is called a bondservant of Christ. There is no way to read the apostles rightly and conclude that natural assignment is not part of God's purpose. It is part and parcel of God's purpose. Spiritual assignment is higher in value and priority, and our natural assignment should ultimately serve spiritual assignment, but that does not remove the fact that natural assignment remains an integral part of God's purpose.

Most of our time in this world will be spent in natural assignment. We should not imagine that this time is wasted. We are serving the Lord while doing it. How faithfully we do it is another discussion, but we are not merely serving self.

## How to Know Your Natural Assignment

First Corinthians 7 says, "As God has distributed to each one, as the Lord has called each one, so let him walk." The language is the language of portioning or distributing an inheritance. Whatever God has parted to you is what he has called you to. So the first way to know your natural assignment is to look at what has been parted to you and start from there.

### Start From Where You Are

Paul says, "Let each one remain in the same calling in which he was called." In context, he uses examples from natural status, including slavery. The point is that wherever you first find yourself is the starting point of your walk with God. Our walk in God's purpose starts from where we are. God already knows and plans your walk in his purpose from there.

There is nobody who does not have something to do from where they are. The problem is often that people are not doing what they can do from where they are. Start from where you are. Even when there is no obvious outward gain, it is not a waste. Discipline is being formed, skills are being built, experience is being gathered, and progress is being made.

Joseph is an example of this. Even when he was a slave and later in prison, he served excellently. He was not self-serving. He was serving God there. That is why he could refuse Potiphar's wife's offer and call it a sin against God. He was functioning as God's servant in the place where he found himself.

### Take Advantage of Opportunities That Come to You

Paul says, "Were you called while a slave? Do not be concerned about it. But if you can be made free, rather use it." So you start from where you are, but if opportunities come to you to be better, use them. Freedom is better than slavery. In the same way, when better opportunities come in your natural assignment, take advantage of them.

That means the second phase of walking in natural assignment is using the opportunities that come to you. Start from where you are, but when an opportunity comes that improves your situation, use it.

### Use the World Without Misusing It

Later in 1 Corinthians 7, Paul speaks about those who use this world as not misusing it. This refers to natural assignment. It is the use of the cosmos, the harnessing of the world God made, the physics, chemistry, biology, manufacturing, producing, trading, and innovating that belong to life in this world.

There are places where God gives you the liberty of your will. Natural assignment is one of them. You can use this world, but do not misuse it. Do not do it wrongly. Do not exploit people. Do not produce what is harmful. There are many ways to use the world without misusing it.

### Be Subject to the Leading of the Spirit

The leading of the Spirit is not the primary way we carry out natural assignment, but we must always be discerning. God can lead people in natural assignment, and we must maintain a devotional life that leaves us open to his direction. As we take initiative in what we do, we must still remain subject to the leading of the Spirit.

### Be Maximally Productive

This is a core instruction on natural assignment. Since we are doing it as unto the Lord, and the Lord always sees, we are to be maximally productive. The issue is not merely appearing diligent before men. The issue is being faithful before God with all the intelligence, resources, exposure, and opportunity that have been given to you.

Someone else may do less than you and yet be more faithful, because faithfulness is measured according to what has been given to each person. But for each of us, the charge is the same: be maximally productive.

## Exhortations on Natural Assignment

### Find One Thing to Do

You can have hobbies and side interests, but you must know the one primary thing you are doing. You only have one life, limited time, and limited strength. Pour your energy in the direction of one main thing so that you can be effective at it. Otherwise, you may end up being busy without being productive.

If you are a student, for example, your academics should be the one primary thing in that season. There may be other things around it, but you must know what is primary.

### Be the Best at It

Ecclesiastes 10 says that if the axe is dull and one does not sharpen the edge, then he must use more strength, but wisdom brings success. The exhortation is to sharpen the edge of your tool, your skill, your craft. Be the best at what you do. Sharpen the edge.

You can be diligent and still not be productive if you are spreading yourself thin or refusing to master anything. The labor of fools wearies them. So do not be blunt across the board. Be good at something. Be valuable at something. If you are doing it as unto the Lord, do not do it in a careless way. Whatever is done for the Lord should be done as best.

### Count the Cost

Luke 14 speaks of a man intending to build a tower who first sits down to count the cost, whether he has enough to finish it. Though that text is often used directly for spiritual assignment and consecration, there is a lesson in it for natural assignment as well. Do not dabble into things you have not planned for. Sit down first and count the cost.

When you count the cost, you are thinking in terms of your capacity and your time. Those are two major things you can assess. There are things in life you cannot control, but there are things you can and should evaluate. You should decide what you are going to do, and decide to do it well and finish it.

In all of this, the Lord must still be first. A disciple prefers the Lord above all else and is willing to sacrifice to see God's purpose done. That applies supremely to spiritual assignment, but it also teaches us how to think about our natural assignment. We should enter it soberly, intentionally, and with the resolve to be faithful.
