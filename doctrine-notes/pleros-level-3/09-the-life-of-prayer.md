The word prayer itself in the Greek is the word `proseuche`, whilst the verb to pray is the word `proseuchomai`. I will spell it. The word prayer is `proseuche`, `P R O S E U C H E`. The word to pray, the verb to pray, is `proseuchomai`. That is why you see that in almost all languages you hear statements like, I pray thee, O king. It might not even be God. You can use the word pray for a human being. It can even be that I am praying anybody here, like I pray thee, please let me do this. It is like I beseech you.

But `proseuchomai` is from two words. We have the word `pros`, which means towards. So prayer has to do with engaging towards another person. You cannot be praying to yourself. Prayer is not an engagement that is with one individual. You cannot I pray myself to do this. Prayer is towards another entity.

The second word there just means to wish or to desire. So the word prayer in its mildest form is to desire towards. It can be to long towards. There are times where it is not about asking for anything, so it is not about beseeching for something. It is just that you wish or you desire towards. You are just fellowshipping with the individual in question. You wish to, you desire towards, you long towards.

It can be stronger, like you are beseeching, you are asking, you are supplicating. But ordinarily that word means you wish or desire towards another. Fellowship, communion, interaction. So prayer in the simplest form is fellowship, communion, interaction, desiring, longing, wishing towards another, interacting with another.
