# Believer's Authority

We are looking at the believer's authority, or the authority of the believer. This is an important teaching because it comes on the back of what we studied on spiritual warfare. We are in a world where there is war. There is nothing we can do about the fact that there is war, but believers need to recognize their authority in this ongoing warfare.

2 Corinthians 10:3 says that though we walk in the flesh, we do not war according to the flesh. So we are at war. That is clear. Our focus here is on the fact that there is such a thing as the believer's authority.

The first text to see is Luke 10:19. The Lord Jesus said, "Behold, I give you authority." If you are using the KJV, it says "power," but the word there is `exousia`, the right for something to be. He says, "I give you authority to trample on serpents and scorpions, and over all the power". That word "power" is `dunamis`. So he gives authority over all the power of the enemy, and nothing shall by any means hurt you.

The second text is Matthew 28:18. Jesus says, "All authority is given to me in heaven and on earth." Not some, not many, not most. All authority. So authority is a big deal in God's word. It is not a side issue or a by-the-way issue.

## Authority and Power

We must be aware that there is a difference between authority and power. Matthew 10:1 says Jesus gave his disciples authority over unclean spirits, to cast them out and to heal all kinds of sickness and disease. Luke 9:1 says he gave them power and authority. So there is such a thing as power, and there is such a thing as authority.

People often use the illustration of a traffic warden. He has the authority of the state behind him, and he also has the power of the state backing that authority. When he says stop, people stop because of his authority and the power behind it. If someone refuses to obey the authority, the power behind that authority enforces what has been said.

So when we think about the believer's authority, we must understand that authority is effective because it is backed by power. That does not mean it is backed by the personal power of the one bearing the authority. It is backed by the power behind the one who owns the authority. That dynamic between authority and power matters, and we must be precise about it.

## Authority Given to Man at the Beginning

This issue of authority takes us all the way back to origin. Authority was originally given to man. In Genesis 2:15-17, the Lord put man in the garden and commanded him concerning the trees. Something is clear there: man had authority to determine whether he would choose life or death, and whatever he chose would affect everything else.

So when we talk about the believer's authority, we must not ignore that there is human authority divinely given: the right to choose and determine the direction of the world. Romans 5:12 says through one man sin entered the world, and death by sin. Man had the authority to decide for himself and for the world.

Genesis 1 shows something else. God said, "Let us make man in our image, after our likeness, and let them have dominion." The immediate statement made about man was dominion. In one word, God was saying, let man have dominion over the cosmos and everything in it. Man was designed to have authority in this world. A case can even be made that man was designed to have authority in heaven and on earth.

So from the beginning, man had two kinds of authority. First, authority to decide for himself and for the world. Second, authority to reign in the world. Dominion is about kingdom. It is your domain, and you direct how things should be in that domain.

## How Man Exercises Authority

What did man do with the authority he had to decide? He chose against God's purpose and chose sin and death for himself and for the world. That is Romans 5:12 again. But after deciding that direction for the world, man still continues to reign in it.

The way man exercises authority is by words. We have seen before that partaking of the fruit of the tree happens by speaking. Proverbs 18:20 says a man's stomach shall be satisfied from the fruit of his mouth, and from the produce of his lips he shall be filled. Nobody literally eats from the fruit of his mouth, so the point is spiritual. By words you are affected.

Verse 21 says, "Death and life are in the power of the tongue." The word translated "power" there has the idea of hand or control. So death and life are in the control of the tongue. Those who love it will eat its fruit.

So at inception, man was given authority to decide for himself and the world, and authority to continue to reign in the world, and he does both by words. He decides by words, and he continues to reign by words.

When people speak death, darkness, and evil, they are reigning, but they are reigning in death. Sometimes people speak terrible words over their children and over their own lives. That is reigning in death over a trajectory. A believer should not be found causing the reign of death in his own life by his words.

## Man's Reality Under Sin

After seeing man's authority at origin, we have to see man's reality under sin. The moment man chose against God's purpose, the reign of sin and death kicked in. That is the cause of the war we now have, because the tyrannical reign of sin and death is opposed by God. So we are not just under tyranny; we are in an opposed reign. That means we are in war. Man's reality under sin is spiritual warfare.

We are living in a world under attack, under the attack of sin, death, and darkness. In the teaching on spiritual warfare, we examined that war on different levels.

The first level is the natural effect of death on body and matter: mortality and corruption. 1 Corinthians 15 says Christ must reign till he has put all enemies under his feet, and the last enemy that will be destroyed is death. In that context, the death being spoken of is mortality and corruption. That one will finally be enforced by the Lord at his coming.

The second level is deception.

The third level is lust or influence, not just deception as information, but deception affecting people's consciousness.

The fourth level is demonization, or demon attachment.

The fifth level is supernatural effects on body, matter, and events. This is also where the operations of practitioners of darkness are seen. Believers, on the side of light, also walk in supernatural effects on body, matter, and events. Life in God should have that supernatural quality to it.

So there is real war at play: mortality and corruption, deception, lust or influence, demonization, and supernatural effects on events, body, and matter. If that is what is going on, we cannot afford to relax under it. The attack is ongoing. The whole world lies under the wicked one.

## Why Redemption Must Give a Response

That raises a necessary question: what is available to us in redemption? If redemption is God's response to the reign of sin, what has it armed us with? What weapons have we been given? How are we supposed to address this problem? That is what brings us to the believer's authority.

We began with man's authority because there is already the right to decide and to reign at the human level, but what does redemption now give us? Redemption gives us a legitimate response to what is going on. It is not mere rebellion. It is a legitimately earned response.

## Keys, Gates, and Ecclesial Authority

Matthew 16 begins to introduce this. Peter receives the revelation that Jesus is the Christ, and Jesus says, "Upon this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades shall not prevail against it." Gates speak of access, and words of access can also speak of authority. The gates of Hades can be read as the authority of death. The authority of death shall not prevail against the church.

That is not merely a prayer point. It is a fact. Those who are part of the church are those over whom the second death has no power. Death has no hold on them anymore.

Then Jesus says, "I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven." Keys also speak of access and therefore authority. Kingdom is not primarily about a place; it is about reign. So he is speaking of the authority of the reign of heaven, the authority of the reign of God exercised on earth.

In Matthew 16, that text is primarily about what can be called ecclesial authority, the authority held by church leadership for the ordering of God's kingdom on earth. The first mention is with Peter, and the second is with the apostles in Matthew 18.

The challenge is that some people then assume every reference to authority is only for church leaders. That is why people can act as though only specially authorized persons can cast out demons. But every believer has been authorized to cast out demons. That is a sign that follows those who believe.

## Binding and Loosing

Jesus says, "Whatever you bind on earth shall be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven." Binding is to tie down, to restrain, to restrict. Loosing is to untie, to free, to allow. So authority has to do with restraining some things and allowing others.

There are things you restrict from your environment. "This will not happen here." Binding does not mean annihilating something; it means restraining it. There are also things you loose, things you allow.

In ecclesial authority, leaders sometimes say, "No, this is not right. This is not how things are done among us." That is part of ordering the church in discipleship. Matthew 18 shows this in the context of dealing with sin among brethren. The authority there is primarily ecclesial.

But the text also shows that the authority Christ won is not limited to ecclesial use. "If two of you shall agree on earth concerning anything they ask, it shall be done for them by my Father in heaven." There you see the authority devolving into personal issues beyond purely ecclesial issues.

So the authority Christ won is for church leadership in ordering the church, but it is not only for church leadership. It extends to believers.

## The Authority of the Risen Christ

Matthew 28 is where what Christ was promising becomes fulfilled. "All authority has been given to me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore and make disciples of all nations." The immediate instruction is ecclesial, but the authority Christ won is not just for church leadership. It is for all believers.

Ephesians 1 says Paul was praying that believers would know what belongs to them in Christ, including "the exceeding greatness of his power toward us who believe, according to the working of his mighty power which he worked in Christ when he raised him from the dead and seated him at his right hand in the heavenly places." The same power that raised Christ from the dead is the power now at work in us.

But notice that the work of that power was not only resurrection. It also seated him at God's right hand, far above all principality, power, might, and dominion, and every name that is named, not only in this age but in that which is to come. All things are put under his feet, and he is given as head over all things to the church.

So the believer's authority is the authority that Jesus has. We share in the same authority he has. When Jesus says all authority is given to me in heaven and on earth, the Bible is clear that this authority is for the benefit of the church, which is his body.

Ephesians 2 says God made us alive together with Christ, raised us up together, and made us sit together in heavenly places in Christ Jesus. The same Spirit that raised him is the one that enthroned him, and that same Spirit is in us. So the authority of Jesus at the resurrection is our authority right now.

Philippians 2 says God highly exalted him and gave him the name above every name, that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those in heaven, on earth, and under the earth. There is no debate about the extent of this authority. It is full authority. Christ remains the head of the body, but the body now shares in that exalted authority.

## The Believer's Authority in Redemption

What redemption has given us is the believer's authority: the legitimate right to rule in heaven and on earth by the Spirit. The same ground upon which we speak of power is also the ground upon which we speak of authority. The Spirit by whom Christ was raised and enthroned is the Spirit by whom we partake of his authority.

Did people partake of this authority before Christ completed redemption? Yes. They partook of it ahead of time on the grounds of what Christ would accomplish. Moses confronting the magicians of Egypt is an example. All of them had power, but one side had the highest authority. When Jesus told the disciples, "I give you authority," he was giving ahead of time what would legitimately belong to them through redemption.

So we have all authority. The believer's authority is not a lesser version of authority in existence. It is the highest authority in existence. It covers all the domains of existence.

## Give No Place to the Devil

Ephesians says, "Neither give place to the devil." There are things believers should not allow. Rage is sin. One may be displeased and yet not sin, but even displeasure should not be allowed to stay too long, because it can turn into bitterness, malice, anger, strife, division, envy, and all kinds of evil. Guard your heart, because you can allow the devil to take a foothold there.

Paul even warned the Corinthians after they had withdrawn fellowship from the sinning brother, because the devil can consume a person in condemnation. We are not ignorant of the devices of the devil.

## Resist the Devil

James 4:7 says, "Submit to God. Resist the devil and he will flee from you." The word carries the sense of standing against. That is the believer's disposition. We stand against him.

That defense requires the armor of God, and the armor is bound up with the knowledge of God's word. Once I know who I am and stand on that basis, that is enough for defense. The devil does not negotiate. If you resist the devil, he flees.

This is personal. When it comes to defense, authority is enough. There may be a stronger dynamic of power involved in offensive or counterattacking ministry, and there is a place for growth in power in the believer's walk, but when it comes to personal defense against the enemy, authority is enough.

You do not need to say, "Maybe I have not done enough to defend myself this time." No. Authority is enough. Nothing shall by any means hurt you. To know who you are and stand based on who you are is enough to stop the workings of the enemy.

If someone does not want a particular activity on them and you pray for them, that is enough. There is no negotiation there. If someone is trying to work a supernatural effect against you, as long as you are standing on God's word, they have no right there.

In that defensive sense, even the weakest faith is enough, as long as it is faith for defense. No is no. The believer does not have to negotiate with darkness. The Israelites could still be unaffected by sorcery done against them, even while being very unserious in other ways. Yet they could choose by themselves to go and walk in death.

When it comes to defense against the attacks of darkness and the enemy, the weakest faith is enough.
