# Supernatural in the Newness of Life

We are looking at supernatural in the newness of life. There are two parts to that subject. We are looking at the supernatural itself, but we are giving it a particular context: the supernatural that is available, that functions, and that works in the newness of life. That means we first have to reexamine the concept of the newness of life before we come back to see the place and role of the supernatural in it.

For a while now in level three, the newness of life has been an anchor subject matter. We have examined love, righteousness, healing, the believer's authority, and spiritual warfare from that standpoint. That matters because many of these subject matters can be taught from a standpoint that is not exactly how God wants us to see them.

For example, people can talk about love or righteousness as something being demanded of you, so that you now try to do them as a matter of self-righteousness. But if we speak about love or righteousness from the newness of life, we are saying that love and righteousness are already your nature. You are not being performative. You are expressing what is already yours. That is an entirely different system.

## The Newness of Life Is a Change of Nature and a Change of System

When we speak of the newness of life, we must take seriously what it means. The newness of life is contrasted not only with the oldness of our former nature, but also with the oldness of a system.

It is possible for a church to say it is preaching the new creation, growth in Christ, the walk in the Spirit, love, and righteousness, while the actual system operating in that church is still the system of the law. That shows up in attitude, tone, and culture. A person can be told to grow, while the environment around that person is still demanding compliance instead of producing transformation.

Real spiritual growth is not the same thing as behavioral modification. If the goal of correction is only to force outward compliance, that is not the newness of life. In the newness of life, people are strengthened to grow. So when we speak about the supernatural in the newness of life, we must understand that this new life is not only a change in nature. It must also be seen in a change of system.

## The Emphasis of Church Doctrine Is the New Man

Ephesians 4 shows that the work of the church is to perfect the saints for the fulfillment of God's purpose. That means when we gather in church, we are being taught the truth of Scripture so that we can fulfill God's purpose for our lives.

Paul says that because of this, we should no longer walk as the Gentiles walk. Our walk is supposed to change, not only because something has changed in our spirit, but because something is changing in our mind. So at salvation, your spirit has changed, but through the work of the church, your mind is meant to change, and as your mind changes, your walk is supposed to change.

What then are we to be taught in church? Paul says in Ephesians 4 that the truth we have learned in Christ comes to this: put off the old man, be renewed in the spirit of your mind, and put on the new man created according to God in true righteousness and holiness. That means the consistent emphasis of the church is the new man, the new creation, the newness of life. We cannot overemphasize this.

## Walking in Newness of Life

Romans 6:4 says that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life. There is a contrast between death and life. We have come out of death and are now in life.

This means, first, that the old man has been crucified with Christ. The believer does not have a dual nature. The old man has been destroyed. Second, Romans 7:6 says we are to serve in newness of spirit and not in oldness of letter. So the contrast is not only between the old nature and the new nature, but also between the old system and the new system.

The content of the law is not the issue there. The issue is the system of the law. The old system says, "Comply." The newness of life works by growth and faith. If all you are trying to do is comply with demands, you inevitably end up with self-righteousness or rebellion. What we have been called to is true righteousness.

So when we speak of the supernatural in the newness of life, we have already established that there is such a thing as the new life, that we are in it, and that we are meant to grow and walk in it.

## Life Is Present in Us and Future Before Us

This new life is spoken of in the New Testament both as a present reality and as a future reality. Life is in us, but life is also something we enter into.

The Lord Jesus spoke about entering into life. Titus speaks of the hope of eternal life promised before time began. Titus 3 says that having been justified by his grace, we have become heirs according to the hope of eternal life. First John 3 says we have passed from death to life, and then also says eternal life abides in us. So there are both external and internal conditions of this life.

God offered man life at the beginning, and after sin that life became the hope of humanity. It is in us now, and it is also still ahead of us in its full effect.

## Christ Has Brought Life and Immortality to Light

Second Timothy 1 says that Christ has abolished death and has brought life and immortality to light through the gospel. Yet we still live in a world where death is visible. That is because death in Scripture is an entire dominion or system. Physical death is part of it, but so are spiritual death, sin, sickness, disease, and the rest of the reign of death.

Christ has won the victory over death, but not everything in that victory has yet been effected in present experience. Some things are available to be walked in now, while some things will only be effected by the Lord at the end.

First Corinthians 15 says that in Christ all shall be made alive, but each in his own order: Christ the firstfruits, and afterward those who are Christ's at his coming. Later in the same chapter, Paul says that flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God in its full fruition, and that corruption does not inherit incorruption. We shall all be changed. This mortal shall put on immortality, and this corruptible shall put on incorruption.

So in this newness of life there is going to be a change from mortality into immortality, and a change from corruption into incorruption. Both our bodies and the cosmos will be changed.

## The New Life Operates by the Spirit

The real question is how immortality and incorruption will be effected. Scripture's answer is clear: by the Spirit.

Second Corinthians 5 says that we groan in this tent, not because we want to be unclothed, but further clothed, that mortality may be swallowed up by life. God has prepared us for this very thing and has given us the Spirit as guarantee. Romans 8:11 says that if the Spirit of him who raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, he who raised Christ from the dead will give life to your mortal bodies through his Spirit who dwells in you.

That text is not primarily about healing. Its immediate context is glorification and immortality. The body is still mortal because of sin, even though the spirit is life because of righteousness. But the point is this: the newness of life operates by the Spirit. The Spirit is how immortality will be effected, how incorruption will be effected, and, in general, how the new life works.

That is almost the general rule of the new life. If something belongs to this life, it operates by the Spirit.

## The Spirit Makes the Kingdom Present Now

Matthew 12:28 says, "If I cast out demons by the Spirit of God, surely the kingdom of God has come to you." So the kingdom is not only later. The kingdom is also now. The reign of life has come.

If casting out demons happens by the Spirit, and healing happens by the Spirit, then the Spirit is the way the new life or kingdom of God operates. The Spirit of life drives out the things of death and the works of the enemy.

Matthew 10 shows the same pattern. Jesus gave the twelve authority over unclean spirits to cast them out and to heal all kinds of sickness and disease. Then, as he sent them out, he told them that when they were delivered before rulers, they should not worry beforehand about what to say, because it would be given to them in that hour. He says, "It is not you who speak, but the Spirit of your Father who speaks in you."

That means the Spirit does more than power. The Spirit gives revelation, and the Spirit gives utterance. Something is given to you, and then the speaking itself is supernatural.

So from the teaching of Jesus, we can say that in the newness of life the Spirit operates supernatural power, supernatural revelation, and supernatural utterance.

## Jesus Ministered by the Spirit and by Faith

Luke 4:14 says that Jesus returned in the power of the Spirit to Galilee. Acts 10:38 says that God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with power, and that he went about doing good and healing all who were oppressed by the devil.

There are things Jesus did that particularly showed his divinity, but it is not biblical to say that the bulk of what he did, he did simply because he was God become man. Scripture repeatedly explains his ministry by the Spirit.

The Gospels also show that Jesus was a faith teacher. He said, "If you can believe," and, "According to your faith be it unto you." He taught the power of the word received by faith. That does not justify harsh or abusive uses of faith teaching, but it does mean we cannot deny that Jesus ministered and taught in this way.

So when the apostles explain what they saw in Jesus, they say he was anointed with the Holy Spirit and with power. The same Spirit has now been given to us.

## The Supernatural Is Available in the Newness of Life

John 14 says, "He who believes in me, the works that I do he will do also; and greater works than these he will do, because I go to my Father." In the later verses, Jesus explains that he is going to the Father so that the Holy Spirit may be sent.

So when we talk about the supernatural in the newness of life, we are talking about the miraculous workings of the Spirit in the life. In one sense, everything in the new life is supernatural, even our character, but here we are talking particularly about miraculous workings, miraculous faculties, and miraculous expressions.

If the new life gives immortality, it is by the Spirit. If anything is received in the new life, it is by the Spirit. If we walk in the new nature, it is by the Spirit. If supernatural power, revelation, or utterance operate, it is by the Spirit. If incorruption happens to the cosmos, it is by the Spirit.

So yes, there is the supernatural in the newness of life.

## A Taxonomy of the Supernatural in 1 Corinthians 12

One of the clearest places where we see a categorization of the supernatural is 1 Corinthians 12. Paul says there are diversities of gifts, but the same Spirit; differences of ministries, but the same Lord; and diversities of activities, but the same God who works all in all. The manifestation of the Spirit is given to each one for profit, for advantage, for help.

If you run life on the natural alone, you are missing out. The manifestation of the Spirit is given to make us better equipped, to bring advantage, to join with what is natural and help.

Paul then lists the gifts: word of wisdom, word of knowledge, faith, gifts of healings, workings of miracles, prophecy, discerning of spirits, different kinds of tongues, and interpretation of tongues. All these are worked by one and the same Spirit.

Those nine gifts are commonly grouped into three categories: utterance gifts, revelation gifts, and power gifts. Tongues, interpretation, and prophecy are utterance gifts. Discerning of spirits, word of knowledge, and word of wisdom are revelation gifts. Faith, workings of miracles, and gifts of healings are power gifts.

The point is that all of these are available by the Spirit, and therefore the supernatural is available in the newness of life.

## Naturally Available and Discretionarily Available Operations

Not all supernatural faculties are available in exactly the same way. Some are naturally available, and some are discretionarily available. That distinction matters.

Healing, as far as God's word is concerned, is naturally available. Preservation is naturally available. Tongues, as prayer and fellowship with God, are naturally available. Prophecy also has a naturally available dimension in the sense that there is always something God may use to strengthen people.

But word of knowledge and word of wisdom are not at our command in that same way. You cannot tell God which word of knowledge to give you or demand that he show you whatever you choose. Those things are discretionarily available. What you can do is optimize for them. You can be discerning, sensitive, and available.

So while we are able to walk in all the gifts, we must understand their nature. Some operations are ours to use directly in faith, and some require sensitivity to the Spirit's own discretion.

## The Reason for the Supernatural

There are two reasons for the supernatural. There is the ordinary reason, and there is the circumstantial reason.

The ordinary reason for the supernatural is that we may engage the spiritual or metaphysical realm so as to fulfill God's purpose. Prayer is part of this. Receiving God's counsel and direction is part of this. The supernatural enables life with God and participation in God's purpose.

The circumstantial reason for the supernatural is to limit or reverse the effects of sin and death. Healing is an example. Preservation is another. In such cases, the supernatural addresses the reign of sin and death and restricts its effects.

So we can say the supernatural serves both our ordinary life in God and our response to the present reign of sin and death.

## Growing in the Supernatural

Like everything else in the life, we can grow in the supernatural. First Corinthians 1 speaks of not coming behind in any gift. First Corinthians 12 speaks of not being ignorant concerning spirituals. Second Corinthians 8 speaks of abounding in faith, speech, and knowledge. Acts 6 speaks of men full of the Holy Spirit and wisdom.

All of that shows that people can be weak or strong in the operation of the supernatural. There is such a thing as being fuller of the Spirit, more knowledgeable, and more effective.

How do we grow in the supernatural? We grow in the supernatural by faith stand, especially in prayer. In Acts, the initial mention of people being filled with the Spirit is tied to speaking. Ephesians says, "Be filled with the Spirit," speaking to yourselves. First Corinthians 14:4 says that he who speaks in a tongue edifies himself, builds himself up.

So by praying in the Spirit, by prayer generally, and by staying in faith, we grow in the supernatural. Growth does not force discretionary gifts, but it makes us best available for them and more effective in the ones that are naturally available.

## Walking in the Supernatural by Faith Initiative

We grow in the supernatural by faith stand, but we walk in the supernatural by faith initiative.

Sometimes people act as though the supernatural is something that simply happens to you. But walking in the supernatural is like walking in love. You take faith initiative. Jesus said believers will lay hands on the sick and they will recover. He did not say the Holy Spirit will move their hands without their participation. He said they will cast out demons. There is initiative involved.

So you take initiative to minister healing. You take initiative to pray in the Spirit. You take initiative to give psalms, prophecies, and utterance. You take initiative to be sensitive for words of knowledge, words of wisdom, and discerning of spirits. The spirit of the prophets is subject to the prophets. Things do not just happen on their own.

At the same time, you do not turn discretionary operations into guaranteed rights where Scripture has not done so. Peter walked on water because the Lord was already operating there and told him to come. There is no text that guarantees walking on water as a standing right. The same applies to other discretionary operations. Apart from things like healing and preservation, many of the power gifts remain discretionary in their expression.

So we take faith initiative for what is naturally available, and we take faith initiative to remain sensitive for what is discretionarily available.

## Personal Walk and Ministry to Others

There are two broad ways to speak about walking in the supernatural. There is our personal walk in the supernatural, and there is our ministry of the supernatural.

Our personal walk in the supernatural includes healing, preservation, and favor. There is such a thing as favor in the newness of life, a kind of supernatural orchestration. It can happen in matters of welfare, provision, and the ordering of life.

Our ministry of the supernatural includes utterance, revelation, and power for others.

So the conclusion is clear. We are in a new life, and that life operates by the Spirit. Because it operates by the Spirit, the supernatural is available in it. The Spirit works power, revelation, and utterance. Some expressions are naturally available, some are discretionarily available, and we are to grow in this by faith and walk in it by faith initiative.
