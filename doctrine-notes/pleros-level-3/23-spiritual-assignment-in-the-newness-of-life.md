# Spiritual Assignment in the Newness of Life

We want to start looking at spiritual assignment in God's purpose. We have looked at natural assignment in God's purpose, and we began with that important question: why are we here? Once we answer the truth of existence, that there is a self-existent Creator who made the world, then the question of why becomes a matter of God's purpose. What is God's purpose?

The clearest answer is in Ephesians 1. God has made known the mystery of His will according to His good pleasure which He purposed in Himself. Ephesians 1:5 says that He predestined us to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the good pleasure of His will. So whatever He predestined us to in redemption must be the good pleasure of His will. We can answer definitively that God's purpose is sonship.

## Sonship Means Child and Heir

That brings us to the next question: what does sonship mean? Romans 8:15 says we have not received the spirit of bondage again to fear, but the Spirit of sonship whereby we cry, "Abba, Father." At salvation, the Spirit we receive is the Spirit of God's purpose. The Spirit bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God.

Then Paul says, if children, then heirs of God and joint-heirs with Christ. So two realities make up sonship. First, you are a child. Second, you are an heir.

Galatians 4 helps explain this. The heir, as long as he is a child, differs nothing from a servant, though he is lord of all, but is under guardians and stewards until the time appointed by the father. Paul is showing that sonship is not only about being a child of God. It includes functioning in inheritance. In the fullness of time, redemption came so that we would receive sonship, and therefore no longer be slaves but sons, and if sons, then heirs of God through Christ.

So when we say God's purpose is sonship, we are not only saying God called us to fellowship and worship. We cannot take away the assignment part from God's purpose. By design, there is pursuit and fulfillment in God's purpose. That is why Barnabas could exhort believers in Acts 11:23 that with purpose of heart they should continue with the Lord. Purpose is for pursuit and fulfillment.

## Spiritual Assignment Has Real Priority

You cannot wish away from God's word that spiritual assignment is treated as a priority in God's purpose. Paul, who also taught the place of natural assignment, spoke in Acts 20:24 about finishing his race with joy and the ministry he received from the Lord Jesus, to testify to the gospel of the grace of God. In 2 Timothy 4:7 he said, "I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith."

No doubt, natural assignment is real and important. Paul himself said he coveted no man's silver or gold or apparel, and that with his own hands he labored to meet his needs and the needs of those with him. But it is not easy to carry both natural assignment and spiritual assignment. If someone functions in primary spiritual leadership, spiritual assignment alone can already be full-time work. That is just the honest truth.

I still encourage people, especially at earlier stages, to give themselves to natural assignment while doing spiritual assignment. But the danger is that someone can pursue natural assignment so ambitiously that it affects spiritual assignment and the person never gets to the point where reducing the natural becomes necessary for the spiritual. These are realities we should face honestly.

## Spiritual Assignment Is Also an Inheritance

As children of God, we have an inheritance. We have seen natural inheritance, but there is also spiritual inheritance. In 1 Corinthians 7:17, Paul says, "As God has distributed to each one, as the Lord has called each one, so let him walk." That shows the principle of starting from where God has apportioned you.

But there are also texts that use the language of lot, part, or inheritance for spiritual assignment. In Acts 1:17, speaking about Judas, it says he "obtained a part in this ministry." Again in Acts 1:24-25 they prayed, "You, Lord, who know the hearts of all, show which of these two You have chosen to take part in this ministry and apostleship from which Judas by transgression fell." There is the idea of part, lot, and allotment when it comes to ministry.

So there is such a thing as spiritual assignment, or ministerial assignment. There is natural inheritance, and there is spiritual inheritance.

When it comes to spiritual assignment, God chooses in a way that is different from natural assignment. In natural assignment, there is broad liberty for righteous pursuit. But in spiritual assignment, there is also choosing, election, limitation, and allotment. We must not ignore what Scripture says because of the many patterns people now see around them. God chooses.

At the same time, that choosing is not a restriction on pursuit itself. It is more a restriction on where the pursuit happens. The one not chosen in Acts 1 would still pursue God's work diligently. So spiritual assignment is not an excuse for passivity. It is an allotment within pursuit.

## Every Believer Is a Part With a Measure

Ephesians 4 develops this further. Verse 7 says, "To each one of us grace was given according to the measure of Christ's gift." God gives grace according to measure. Then verse 16 says the whole body grows according to the effective working by which every part does its share.

That means each believer is a part, and each part has a measure. Grace is given for that measure. Every individual believer is a part in the body of Christ with a measure and with grace for that measure.

1 Corinthians 12:27 says, "Now you are the body of Christ, and members individually." Each individual is a distinct part. You are a singular part in the body of Christ. So God's distributing does not only apply to the circumstances of natural assignment. It also applies to spiritual assignment. He gives grace for our measure as parts in the body.

## Spiritual Assignment Is the Work of Ministry

What is the point of being a part with a measure? Ephesians 4 answers it. Christ gave apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers for the equipping of the saints for the work of ministry. So spiritual assignment is ministry.

Acts 1 spoke about obtaining a part in this ministry. Ephesians 4 shows that every believer has a part in the work of ministry. Leaders equip the saints so that the saints do the work of ministry and grow into leading in their own measure.

That is why everybody born again has been given ministry. 2 Corinthians 5:17-18 says that if any man is in Christ, he is a new creation, and that God has reconciled us to Himself through Jesus Christ and has given us the ministry of reconciliation. It is the new creation that is given this ministry.

So every believer has a spiritual assignment. Every believer has been called to the work of ministry.

## Ministry Is Serving the Message

This ministry is not merely about helping with natural church operations. Those things have their place, but the ministry being discussed here is about a message. 2 Corinthians 5:19 says God has committed to us the word of reconciliation.

Your spiritual assignment is to serve the message. Ministry means you are serving something. You are serving the gospel to the world. You are serving the redemptive message. If people have not believed, you preach reconciliation. If they have believed, you teach them to walk in redemption and in the purpose redemption has brought them into. You teach them to walk in victory over the flesh, in healing and health, and especially in God's purpose.

So spiritual assignment is the work of ministry, and the work of ministry is the service of the message.

## The Kingdom Is Worth Our Whole Life

Because of its value, spiritual assignment has highest priority. Matthew 13 says the kingdom of heaven is like treasure hidden in a field, which a man found and, for joy over it, sold all he had and bought that field. Again, it is like a merchant seeking beautiful pearls, who, when he found one pearl of great price, sold all he had and bought it.

That means we should live all our lives for the fulfillment of the mission of the church. Even what we do in the natural should be done with that kingdom priority in view.

That also shows up in giving. We should not merely talk about grand gestures. The first rule in giving is commitment. Then there is generosity. Then there is sacrifice. Commitment means consistent giving. Generosity means willing giving. Sacrifice means giving in a way that genuinely costs you. But the kingdom should shape all of it.

## How We Are Called Into Spiritual Assignment

When people ask, "What is my spiritual assignment? How do I start?" the first answer is this: we are generally called into God's purpose, including our spiritual assignment, by the salvific message.

2 Thessalonians 2:13-14 says God chose us for salvation through sanctification by the Spirit and belief in the truth, "to which He called you by our gospel." So when the gospel is preached, God is calling people into what He has planned. The call of salvation is already a call into God's purpose, including ministry.

The second thing is that we are specifically called into our spiritual assignment, or the work of ministry, by the discipleship message. Someone may preach salvation to you without understanding God's purpose fully, and yet that preaching truly brings you into salvation. But discipleship preaching specifically calls you to walk in God's purpose and in the work of ministry.

That is why Ephesians 4 says leaders equip the saints for the work of ministry. The discipleship message is a specific call toward that end.

It is possible for someone to be saved and yet not understand God's purpose. But when someone calls you by discipleship into God's purpose, you are meant to respond.

## Commit to the Local Church That Calls You by the Message

The third thing is commitment. We are to commit to the local church that calls us by the discipleship message so that we can be made effective for our spiritual assignment.

People are not only meant to call you to salvation, but also to discipleship. If someone helps you to see God's purpose and your spiritual assignment in it, then you should commit there. That is the principle of God's word. You do not keep saying you are waiting for God to lead you when the truth of His word has already led you.

Of course, there are other things to consider in a church. If there are issues like exploitation or abuse, that is serious. But as a principle, a church that calls you by the message into God's purpose is a church to commit to.

## Start From Where You Are

The fourth thing is to start evangelism and discipleship from where you are. As the Lord has apportioned to each one, and as He has called each one, so let him walk. Start from where you are.

If God will call you for something, He will equip you for it, and that equipping includes the orchestration of your life. There is no need to force pioneering work or imitate what is not your lot. You can carry out your spiritual assignment, your part and your measure, faithfully where God has placed you.

This is where character and qualification matter. There are some things a person should not force. If someone is not built for a certain kind of pioneering work, that person may be better served by submitting within an existing system where there is accountability and help.

You can do great things for God without carrying burdens that may not be yours. It should not be by force.

## Unfold Naturally Into More Phases of Ministry

The fifth thing is to unfold naturally by divine arrangement and orchestration into more phases of ministry. This is the ordinary order of the word.

1 Timothy 3 and Acts 6 show a natural unfolding. A person starts as a member, learning. Then as a worker, serving both in-house and in outreach. Then there is further unfolding into forms of leadership and responsibility. You do not need some special pioneering call just to begin carrying out your spiritual assignment. Start where you are, pursue it faithfully, and let the church's order and correction help you grow.

If a good church corrects you in character or conduct, that correction is part of God's will for your growth. You take the correction and unfold further so that you can lead others effectively.

## Be Discerning and Be Faithful

The sixth thing is to be discerning of the leading of the Spirit within the local church to specific assignments. God orchestrates our steps, but we must participate faithfully in that orchestration.

There should be conviction, commitment, and consistency. 1 Corinthians 4:1-2 says, "Let a man so consider us, as servants of Christ and stewards of the mysteries of God. Moreover it is required in stewards that one be found faithful."

When you are convinced of the truth, commit to it and be consistent in it. If there were no times set for gathering, many people would never gather. Commitment channels faithfulness. That is part of how the Spirit works in us.

Faithfulness is that grace by which, when we see the truth, we commit to it and remain constant in it, even when it is inconvenient.

## The Shape of Our Spiritual Assignment

So we have a spiritual assignment in God's purpose, and it is the work of ministry. That work of ministry is to preach the message. It is to preach the gospel for salvation. It is to preach discipleship unto establishment in God's purpose. It is to call men into salvation and to call men into the church for discipleship.

Then, in time, within the local church, people are trained so that they can fulfill God's purpose and serve others in their own measure.

You have a spiritual assignment in God's purpose. It is evangelism and discipleship. It is to preach men into salvation and to preach men into the church for discipleship. In your part in ministry, you have a measure, and you have grace for that measure.

Commit yourself out of conviction to God's purpose and to your spiritual assignment in God's purpose, and be consistent with it by the Spirit of God.
