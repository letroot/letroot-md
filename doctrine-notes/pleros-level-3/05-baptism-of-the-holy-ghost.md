# Baptism of the Holy Ghost

This is still level three, or we could say the baptism of the Holy Ghost. This is a subject matter that is so important because people have a lot of questions. People have a lot of concerns. People really desire certainty on this subject matter.

You must understand that when we are dealing with revelatory truths, you can only have certainty to the degree of what is revealed. It means you have to do analytical reasoning on what is revealed to arrive at the conclusions you are to arrive at. Sometimes people grandstand beyond what is available, beyond what is actually revealed.

Baptism of the Holy Ghost is a subject matter you cannot talk about without talking about tongues. Sometimes people can start grandstanding on tongues beyond what is available. There are texts that talk about speaking with tongues, and they give us, by very good analytical reasoning, the conclusions we can afford to make. Beyond that, we are going beyond the limits and bounds of that which can be done with the revealed text.

You have to be faithful to what is available to make your conclusions. As we desire certainty on this matter, we must trust the revelation of God. The subject matter of the Holy Ghost is not a subject matter you can understand by observation and reason in the natural at all. We are fully in the domain of revelation. As we go into that domain of revelation, for those of us who have believed in Christ Jesus as truth, we are limited to the Bible. That is what we have.

## The Reality of the Baptism of the Holy Ghost in Scripture

Our first contact with this idea is John the Baptist in the four Gospels: John indeed baptized with water, but there is one coming who will baptize with the Holy Ghost. The first thing I want you to observe is this: it means there is a reality called the baptism of the Holy Ghost, and it is a reality specifically associated with the work of the Lord Jesus.

I do not want us to assume what it means yet. I want us to study the text faithfully.

Our next contact with this reality is Acts 1:5: "For John truly baptized with water, but you shall be baptized with the Holy Ghost not many days from now." If at all we are wondering what this reality of the baptism of the Holy Ghost is, the Lord Jesus is breaking it out to us. Whatever this thing means, he says, you are going to be baptized with the Holy Ghost not many days from now. So he is making it clear that this reality John spoke about is about to become functional, about to take off, not many days from now.

In Acts 1:8 he says, "You shall receive power after that the Holy Ghost has come upon you." You can see the idea I mentioned earlier. Baptism of the Holy Ghost is not, "I am dipping you in." It is more like, "I am pouring out, and you are drenched in it." The Holy Ghost will come upon you, and then you shall be witnesses.

Our next contact with the idea is Acts 11. Peter says, "Then remembered I the word of the Lord, how that he said, John indeed baptized with water; but ye shall be baptized with the Holy Ghost." What made him remember? He had just said, "As I began to speak, the Holy Ghost fell on them, as on us at the beginning." Again, the idea is not that the Holy Ghost is there and you are put inside him. It is that the Holy Ghost is poured on you. He fell upon them as upon us at the beginning.

Then in Acts 19 Paul asks a striking question: "Did you receive the Holy Ghost when you believed?" They said they had not so much as heard whether there be any Holy Ghost. Then he asked, "Into what then were you baptized?" So the idea of receiving the Holy Ghost was a baptism experience they should have had. He expected that something should have accompanied their faith, and when it was lacking, he questioned them about it.

When Paul laid hands on them, the Holy Ghost came upon them, and they spoke with tongues and prophesied.

So the first thing I want you to know is that there is no doubt within the word of God that there is a reality called the baptism of the Holy Ghost. If anyone is denying that there is such a reality from God’s word, the person is not being faithful to the text. What it means is a separate issue, but there is the reality called the baptism of the Holy Ghost.

## Different Descriptions of the Same Reality

The second thing to see is that there are different descriptions of that same reality.

One description is the Holy Ghost coming upon someone. Acts 1:8 and Acts 19:6 show that clearly.

Another description is the Holy Ghost falling upon someone. Acts 11 says, "The Holy Ghost fell on them, as on us at the beginning," and Peter immediately connects that to the words of the Lord Jesus about the baptism of the Holy Ghost.

Another description is receiving the Holy Ghost. Paul said, "Did you receive the Holy Ghost when you believed?" Peter also gives us the same thought when he says God gave them the same gift he gave us when we believed on the Lord Jesus Christ. That means this reality is something given, and something received.

The last description we will take note of is being filled with the Holy Ghost. This one is more general, because being filled with the Holy Ghost can describe someone acting under the influence of the Spirit even outside the specific discussion of the baptism of the Holy Ghost. For example, Zacharias was said to be filled with the Holy Ghost and prophesied. So being filled with the Holy Ghost is a general description of someone acting under the influence of the Spirit. Still, for this experience of the baptism of the Holy Ghost, that term can also be used.

But I want to be careful. The first two expressions, the Holy Ghost coming upon and the Holy Ghost falling upon, show the baptismal idea more directly. The receiving language shows your part in it. Being filled is a broader term that can still describe this experience, especially after the resurrection of Jesus, but it is not limited to this experience alone.

## What the Baptism of the Holy Ghost Means

What exactly does it mean to be baptized with the Holy Ghost? I do not want to define it in a way that boxes things in too rigidly, but I will describe it this way: it is the state of full expression of the Holy Ghost, specifically the external expression of the Holy Ghost.

When I say the full expression of the Holy Ghost, I mean there is an internal expression of the Holy Ghost and there is an external expression of the Holy Ghost. It is called the baptism of the Holy Ghost because we are witnessing the full expression that goes beyond the internal expression. We are now seeing the Holy Ghost in external expression.

Why is this vital? Every time we see them talk about this experience called the baptism of the Holy Ghost, it is premised on the fact that the person has believed on the Lord Jesus. Acts 19:2 says, "Did you receive the Holy Ghost when you believed?" Acts 11:17 says, "If God gave them the same gift as he gave us when we believed on the Lord Jesus Christ." So faith in Christ is the ground upon which you can have this reality.

Now let me clarify something. Can you lack this reality despite believing? Analytically, the answer is yes. Paul’s question implies that someone may have believed and yet be lacking this experience. That does not mean the believer does not have the Holy Ghost at all. That would be wrong thinking.

There is the internal reality or expression of the Holy Ghost, and then there is the external reality. The baptism of the Holy Ghost refers to a man who, having the internal one, now also has the external one. That man has the full expression, experience, or state of the Holy Ghost.

## The Internal Reality of the Spirit at Salvation

Paul is clear in Ephesians 1:13 that there is an automatic indwelling of the Spirit at the point of faith. You were sealed with the Spirit. It is immediate.

Romans 8 also makes it clear: if any man does not have the Spirit of Christ, he is none of his. At the instance of faith, the Spirit of God is in you. John 14 likewise points to the Spirit being with them at one point, and then being in them in the permanent sense tied to redemption.

So when we talk about the baptism of the Holy Ghost, we are not saying it is the beginning of the Spirit’s presence in the believer. We are talking about the full expression of the Spirit, where we can speak about the internal reality and then the external reality.

The internal expression speaks primarily to things like our character in Christ. Because the Spirit is in us, we bear the fruit of the Spirit: love, joy, peace, longsuffering, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, meekness, temperance or self-control. There is an inward transformation of character. Something has happened on the inside of you.

## The External Expression of the Spirit

The things that pertain to the baptism of the Holy Ghost are about external expression. Even though you have the Spirit internally, the baptism of the Holy Ghost deals with external expression. If we see someone prophesy and say that person is filled with the Holy Ghost, it is because there is an outward expression taking place.

A man may have the Spirit of God, may be born again, saved, in God’s family, and yet we may still say he has not been baptized in the Holy Ghost in this sense, because he has not entered into the external expression. He may have a fantastic expression of the internal reality of the Spirit, walking in love, joy, peace, longsuffering, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, meekness, and temperance, and still not have stepped into the external expression.

There are people sincerely like that. If the Lord gives us something and we do not partake of it, there is no doubt that we are having a limited experience of all that should have been possible through us.

So why is there such a special thing called the baptism of the Holy Ghost if people had external expressions before the Lord came? It is because, at the instance of the new creation, which had never been before, the way the new creation steps into the external expression of the Spirit of God is by that reality called the baptism of the Holy Ghost. This is the order in which God expects the new creation, the son of God, not just to be found with the internal reality of the Spirit and walking in it, but also to partake of the external reality of the Spirit.

## Empowerment for Witness and Purpose

Why does God want the believer to have this external reality as well? Acts 1:8 is clear: "You shall receive power after that the Holy Ghost has come upon you, and you shall be witnesses unto me." That means there is a value the baptism of the Holy Ghost has for our walk in God’s purpose, and a particularly critical value for our spiritual assignment.

A believer who is not walking in this external reality is going to miss certain things that should come to his help and aid in his walk in God’s purpose. That is why 1 Corinthians 12 says the manifestation of the Spirit is given for profit, for benefit, for assistance. The gifts of the Spirit are supposed to bear the load of God’s purpose with us, whether in the broad sense of our lives or specifically in the work of ministry.

That is why the Lord said they should tarry until they were endued with power from on high. Again, that language of being clothed has the idea of something poured out upon you so that you are covered with it. The baptism of the Holy Ghost is empowerment for purpose.

## Speaking With Tongues as the Primary Mark

When we choose to receive the baptism of the Holy Ghost, what we see in Scripture to be the primary mark of receiving it, without fail, is speaking with tongues. Speaking with new tongues, speaking with other tongues, speaking with tongues is what we consistently see as the mark of entering into this external expression of the Holy Ghost.

The reason is not far-fetched. By the time we read 1 Corinthians 12 to 14, this particular gift is the leading gift into other expressions of the Holy Ghost. That does not mean every time any expression of the Holy Ghost happens, a man must speak in tongues before anything else can happen. But by divine order for the new creation, it is the leading gift into other gifts.

So when the Lord said, "You shall receive power after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you," what we saw happen was that they spoke with tongues. When Peter said they received the same gift as us, what happened was that they spoke with tongues. In Acts 19, when Paul referenced receiving the Holy Ghost as a baptism reality, what happened was that they spoke with tongues.

Acts 2 says they were all filled with the Holy Ghost and began to speak with other tongues as the Spirit gave them utterance. Acts 10 says the Holy Ghost fell on those who heard the word, and the Jewish believers knew the gift of the Holy Ghost had been poured out on the Gentiles because they heard them speak with tongues and magnify God. Acts 19 says the Holy Ghost came upon them, and they spoke with tongues and prophesied.

Clearly, in all the instances where we see the baptism of the Holy Ghost being received, speaking in tongues is the primary essential initial mark.

## What Tongues Is

What is this act of speaking in tongues? There are many ideas and conceptions about it. Some are of the opinion that tongues is a human language, and understandably so because of Acts 2 and the way it is rendered there. There is no debate that Acts 2 includes supernatural speaking in human languages. If one is being honest with the text, that happened.

But as far as the text of Scripture goes, tongues is a non-human language. First Corinthians 14:2 says, "He that speaketh in an unknown tongue speaketh not unto men, but unto God: for no man understandeth him; howbeit in the spirit he speaketh mysteries." That is very definitive. When you speak in tongues, you are not speaking to men but to God. No man understands. In the Spirit, you speak mysteries.

Those statements emphasize the non-participation of the human mind, not just the mind of the speaker but also the mind of the hearer. So I would say that tongues is a non-human language supernaturally granted to us by the Holy Ghost.

I would be careful not to be overly emphatic in saying tongues can never have any connection to human languages, because some people want to accommodate Acts 2 that way. But I would rather take it that when people speak human languages supernaturally, that is not necessarily the ordinary thing being discussed in 1 Corinthians 14. What is clear from Paul’s explanation is that tongues, in the regular sense being discussed there, is not speech directed to men in intelligible human language.

## Who Can Speak in Tongues, and When

Who can speak with tongues? Every believer can. Mark 16 says these signs shall follow them that believe. People may debate textual questions around Mark 16, but even apart from that, the scriptural pattern still shows that this is open to believers generally.

In Acts 2 they all spoke with tongues. In Cornelius’s house, it was not presented as selective. In Acts 19, they all spoke with tongues. First Corinthians 14 does not give the impression that this is for only a few special believers. It presents an open, corporate participation in spiritual utterance.

When can a believer speak in tongues? Any time. Where can a believer speak in tongues? Anywhere. Peter said, "The promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off, even as many as the Lord our God shall call." Since Jesus has poured out the Spirit, every believer can receive and speak.

## Why Tongues Matters

Why tongues? There is one main answer: it takes off the limits of natural human language in our engagement with God and in the supernatural.

Imagine trying to fellowship with God only in your natural language. At some point, you run short of words. You want fellowship, but you are constrained by your vocabulary. Tongues removes that limitation.

It also takes off the limits of natural language in self-edification. It takes off the limits of natural language in supplication when you do not know the details you ought to pray about. The essential reason for tongues is to take off the limit of natural human language in the supernatural, in our fellowship with God, in our edification, and in prayer.

## How the Baptism of the Holy Ghost Is Received

How does the baptism of the Holy Ghost happen? We receive it. It has already been given. Acts 2:33 says he poured it out. Acts 10 speaks of the gift of the Holy Ghost being poured out on the Gentiles. That outpouring speaks of what Jesus accomplished by his resurrection and ascension.

Titus 3 also speaks of the Holy Ghost, "which he shed on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Saviour." The outpouring of the Spirit from the resurrection of Jesus causes the Spirit to indwell us, and from indwelling us, it is also available in this outward sense.

So the faith of salvation receives the Holy Ghost internally, and faith for the baptism of the Holy Ghost receives the Holy Ghost for external expression. When you receive the baptism of the Holy Ghost, you are intentionally receiving that outpouring for external expression.

That means the faith involved is not merely, "I believe God can do it," or, "I believe it exists." It is, "I receive the baptism of the Holy Ghost for external expression now." It is faith to do the external expression. Primarily, it is faith to speak with tongues.

If I say I have received the baptism of the Holy Ghost, then I am saying I have received the external expression. It cannot stop at a vague internal expectation that something may happen later. I have received to speak. I have received to do.

## Acting on the Word and Speaking Forth

That leads to the matter of acting on God’s word. Many people make the mistake of thinking something will happen on its own accord without their participation, but Acts 2:4 tells us very clearly what happened: "They were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance."

The Spirit gives utterance. They began to speak. If you do not begin to speak, nothing will be spoken. They began to speak as the Spirit gave them utterance.

You may ask, "But this is a non-human language. How will I know what to say?" None of us knows what to say beforehand. We speak as it is given. Some people sense what they are about to say and refuse it because it does not sound how they expected. But you are not the one giving the utterance. You are the one doing the speaking. As long as you are in faith, you can never be wrong.

No believer who genuinely wants to speak with tongues can be speaking some so-called devil’s tongues. That idea is false. The Holy Ghost is the one making utterance available. The believer’s role is to speak forth what the Spirit gives.

They began to speak. They began to speak. They began to speak as the Spirit gave them utterance.

## Receiving the Empowerment of the Spirit

The baptism of the Holy Ghost is empowerment for purpose. You shall receive power after the Holy Ghost has come upon you. You shall be witnesses unto me. There is a need for empowerment for purpose.

God has given us his Spirit. That is wonderful. But this life is also about external expressions. Our spiritual assignment requires this empowerment, and even our broader work as children of God benefits from it.

Please do not set a limit on what God can do through you by refusing or neglecting the baptism of the Holy Ghost.

Jesus died and rose again and poured out the Spirit not just for our regeneration internally, but for the external expressions of the Holy Ghost. The believer can accept that reality now, receive the baptism of the Holy Ghost, and step into the external expressions of the Holy Ghost by speaking with tongues and by the other gifts of the Spirit.
