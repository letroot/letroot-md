# Spiritual Warfare

We are looking at spiritual warfare. It is a very important teaching.

Imagine being in a war and not knowing it. What is more dangerous than being at war is not knowing that you are at war. If you do not know that you are at war, you will not install defenses, and you will not launch the attacks that are necessary. You become highly susceptible to the enemy.

That is where I want to begin: there is a real warfare. There is the reality of spiritual warfare.

One challenge with this teaching is that some people have an exaggerated view of the war. If you hear them talk about spiritual warfare, they can leave you terrified and confused. Others have a very low view of it. Some believers even assume that because of what Christ has done, we are no longer at war at all. That is not correct.

This teaching has to be handled delicately. If indeed we are at war and we are made unaware of it, then we become more susceptible to its implications. If you know you are at war, your defenses and your guard will be up. If you do not know, they will be down.

So we need to understand the war we are in, the attacks that are coming, the defenses that are needed, and the attacks that are to be made if indeed we are at war.

## The Reality of Spiritual Warfare

I am making the claim that there is spiritual warfare. That is a biblical claim, and we can know it in two ways. We can know it by observation and reason, and we can know it by the revelation of the word.

By observation and reason, we already see contradiction in the world. There is life, yet there is death. There is light, yet there is darkness. There is good, yet there is evil. There is health, yet there is sickness. That contradiction gives us the sense that there is a battle going on.

But for us as believers, what matters most is the revelation of the word.

In 2 Corinthians 10:3 Paul says, "Though we walk in the flesh, we do not war according to the flesh." We live in the natural world and we are in contact with natural things, but our warfare is not according to the flesh.

Then in verse 4 he says, "The weapons of our warfare are not carnal, but mighty in God for pulling down strongholds." Warfare requires weapons, and weapons are for defense and attack. So Paul is saying there is a warfare going on, it is not natural in character, and it has spiritual weapons for defense and attack.

In 1 Timothy 1:18 he tells Timothy to "wage a good warfare." So there is a war we are in whether we like it or not, and we are meant to engage in that war.

James 4:1 speaks of wars and fights that arise from lusts. First Peter 2:11 tells us to abstain from fleshly lusts that war against the soul. So we are to attack and we are also to defend. We are to cast down imaginations and everything that exalts itself against the knowledge of God, and we are to abstain from fleshly lusts.

Ephesians 6 makes the same point very clearly: "Put on the whole armor of God, that you may be able to stand against the wiles of the devil." We do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against principalities, powers, rulers of the darkness of this age, and spiritual hosts of wickedness in heavenly places.

Luke 11 also uses warfare imagery in connection with demons. The Lord speaks of a strong man fully armed guarding his palace until a stronger one comes, overcomes him, takes away his armor, and divides the spoil. That is warfare language.

Hebrews 2:14 also shows a confrontation: through death Christ destroyed him who had the power of death, that is, the devil. So by the revelation of God there is no doubt about it. There is war. There is spiritual warfare.

## The Warfare Must Not Produce Fear

The fact that we are at war is no reason for fear.

Sometimes people teach spiritual warfare in a way that leaves people terrified, grasping for hope, as though the enemy is the final authority. That is not what God’s word does. Ordinarily, war may produce fear, but the work of Christ was done so that those who through fear of death were all their lifetime subject to bondage would be released.

If people are left in fear by the teaching of spiritual warfare, then we have not taught the biblical truth of that warfare wholesomely.

## The Origin of Spiritual Warfare

What led to this warfare?

God’s purpose by design made war a possibility. God’s purpose did not create war automatically, but it made war possible.

If God’s purpose is that man should be in his family, be his son, have his life, and do so by free response, then there is the possibility that man can choose against that purpose. If man chooses against God’s purpose, he creates an opposing reign to the reign God wants him to have.

That in itself is not yet war. But if God intends to oppose that contrary reign and ensure that his purpose is still fulfilled, then war becomes a possibility.

So God’s purpose creates the possibility of war. Man’s rejection of God’s purpose creates the background of the war. God’s determination for redemption is the reason the war begins.

If God had simply allowed the reign of sin and death to continue unopposed, what we would have had is tyranny. But God was not willing to leave things under the tyrannical reign of sin and death. He chose to oppose it.

That is why Genesis 3 is important. After the fall, God says to the serpent, "I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her seed." That is warfare. God is saying, "I will not sit still with what has happened. I will oppose this."

So the flow is this:

God’s purpose creates the possibility of war.

Man’s rejection of God’s purpose creates the background to the war.

God’s decision to redeem launches the war.

## Sin and Death Entered Through Man

It is very important to see that sin and death entered through man.

Romans 5:12 says, "Through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin." So sin and death entered the cosmos through man. That means there was no basis for speaking of sin and death as already present in the cosmos before man’s rejection of God’s purpose.

This is why I say that the angel who became the ruling angel in darkness and death was not created that way. He became that way. Just as man was not created in sin and death but became subject to sin and death, so no angel was created in that state either.

The devil and his cohort are the primary beneficiaries, managers, and stewards of the reign of sin and death, though they are also eventual victims. Man and the cosmos are the immediate major victims of that reign.

So the background to this war is that man rejected God’s purpose and brought in the reign of sin, death, and darkness.

Certain angels also sided with that reign. Others remained on the side of light. That is why Scripture can speak of holy or elect angels. They remained on the side of God’s purpose.

## The Devil and His Cohort as Beneficiaries of the Reign of Death

Once sin and death entered through man, the devil and his cohort became the primary beneficiaries, managers, and stewards of that reign.

There is a kind of cosmic intelligence for evil just as there is a cosmic intelligence for good. Sometimes people can look back at their lives and see how events were arranged in a way that brought help, guidance, or preservation. In the same way, some people can look back and see destructive orchestration: being in the wrong place at the right time for corruption, bondage, or ruin.

That is part of what it means to say that the devil and his cohort are managers and stewards of death and darkness. They work within that reign.

Still, God’s opposition is the major reason there is now war. That is not a bad thing. War in this case is better than tyranny. It is better that God opposes the reign of sin and death than simply leave creation under it.

## The Nature of the Warfare

Once we have seen that there is war and we have seen its origin, we need to understand its nature.

Since this war begins with the reign of sin and death, we must ask what sin and death are doing against people, under the management of the devil and his cohort, that God is now opposing for the good of people.

The first thing to say is that this warfare is first seen in the natural effect of death on matter and the body.

That includes the corruption of matter, the mortality of the human body, and susceptibility to sickness. Matter in its corrupt state can produce disaster. The mortal human body is susceptible to sick conditions. In this case nothing supernatural has to be directly imposed. These are the natural effects of death in a fallen world.

The second thing is deception, and this is the primary one by which the devil is directly described.

Deception means falsehood affecting consideration and conviction. It is the churning out of false information to affect the way people think and what they become convinced of.

The third thing is influence, temptation, and lust. This is not merely falsehood at the level of reasoning. It is pressure brought toward the consciousness, emotions, and desires.

The fourth thing is demonization or demon attachment.

The fifth thing is supernatural effect on matter, body, and events.

Usually people jump immediately to the fourth and fifth levels when they hear "spiritual warfare," while ignoring the first and second levels, which are foundational.

## The First Level: Natural Effects of Death

The first level of this warfare is the natural effect of death on matter and the body.

Here we are talking about corruption, mortality, and the susceptibility that comes with them. Disasters can happen in a corrupt world. Sickness can happen in a mortal body. These things belong to the reign of death.

So when someone says a sickness is an attack of the enemy, that is not necessarily a strange statement, even where there is no direct supernatural activity involved. The enemy works through the reign of death and darkness.

## The Second Level: Deception

The primary work of the devil in this warfare is deception.

The devil is a deceiver. The Greek idea behind the word is to cause someone to stray or roam from the right place, whether that right place is truth, safety, goodness, or virtue.

That is why the Lord repeatedly warned, "Take heed that no one deceives you." False christs and false prophets would arise to deceive many. Paul says, "Do not be deceived." He says evil men will grow worse and worse, deceiving and being deceived.

This is the operational model of the devil. He deceives, and those he deceives go on to deceive others.

So in this war, as the devil seeks to deceive, God is opposing deception. That is why truth matters so much. That is why teaching matters so much.

The devil is called the one who deceives the whole world in Revelation 12:9. If falsehood is reigning, the devil is at work there.

Thinking wrongly about the devil is deception. Thinking there is no devil is deception. Thinking the devil is more powerful than he is is deception. Underrating the devil is also deception. What we need is truth and accuracy.

## The Third Level: Influence, Temptation, and Lust

The third level of spiritual warfare is influence, temptation, and lust.

This is the war of desires brought against the consciousness. First Peter 2:11 says fleshly lusts war against the soul. Romans 6 warns, "Do not let sin reign in your mortal body, that you should obey it in its lusts."

So there is a warfare in the realm of desires. The enemy brings influence, temptation, and lust. If someone keeps yielding to these things, the person becomes more susceptible to further bondage.

This is not yet the same as demonization. Many issues are simply the flesh and must be resisted as the flesh. A believer may be struggling, may be growing, and may be resisting lusts without being demonized.

But if there is persistent yielding and hardness, then susceptibility increases.

## The Fourth Level: Demonization and Demon Attachment

There is a point at which someone can become demonized or demon attached. I am trying to make clear that this is not as simple or as casual as people often make it sound.

Many issues are the flesh. They require resistance, repentance, growth, and the exercise of the believer’s authority. It is not true that every serious struggle means a person has a demon.

At the same time, there is a kind of unrepentance or hardness of heart that can open a person to deeper demonic attachment. Judas is an example. He kept yielding until there came a point where Satan entered him.

Deception can also help lead someone to this point. A believer who is taught and is growing, even if still struggling, is less likely to become demonized than one who is deeply open to deception and wrong beliefs. If someone is deceived into thinking that certain darkness is acceptable, the person can more easily get into trouble.

## The Fifth Level: Supernatural Effects on Matter, Body, and Events

The fifth level is supernatural effect on matter, body, and events.

Here we are talking about things deliberately done by the enemy in a supernatural way. There can be supernatural effects in people’s bodies, in events, and in material situations. There can be orchestrations that are not merely natural happenings.

This is real, but it is not the whole picture of spiritual warfare. People often focus only here and ignore the broader and more foundational operations of death, deception, and lust.

## Human Participation in the Reign of Darkness

When we speak of enemies in this warfare, we are not talking about human beings in a simplistic sense. Death is an enemy. Darkness is an enemy. The devil and his cohort are enemies.

At the same time, there are human beings who make themselves managers of death and darkness. In that sense, they are participating in the enemy’s work.

Acts 13 is an example. Paul rebukes Elymas and calls him "son of the devil" and "enemy of all righteousness." So yes, there are human beings who give themselves to stewarding darkness.

We need care and discernment here. Not every person in error is a steward of death and darkness in the same sense. Some are simply wrong. Some are deceived. Some are functioning according to destructive traditions or practices without fully understanding what they are doing. But the devil can still have a field day through such conduct.

So when people become practitioners of death and darkness, whether intentionally or by deeply ingrained participation, they are siding with the enemy’s work and must be resisted.

## The Primary Battle Is in the Teaching of the Word

One reason I am keen to teach spiritual warfare carefully is that many people think only in terms of dramatic deliverance acts. They hear spiritual warfare and imagine that the only real battle is shouting, commanding, and casting out demons.

But the real battle is first in the teaching of the word.

Jesus came to preach deliverance to the captives. People can lose demons simply by hearing the word, having their convictions changed, and having their consciousness corrected, without even realizing what happened in dramatic terms.

If preachers understand that preaching is itself a vital part of deliverance ministry, there would be much more confidence in sitting people under truth before getting to moments where direct ministry may be needed.

The devil does much of his work through deception, and the preaching of the word is one of God’s primary answers to that deception.

## Redemption Is the Primary Engagement in This War

How then do we engage spiritual warfare?

The primary engagement in this war is redemption.

Any engagement of spiritual warfare before redemption was in view of redemption or based on the redemption that would be done. The primary engagement from God’s side, from the side of life and light and faith, is redemption.

Why is redemption primary? Because what the devil got was legal in the sense that man truly brought sin and death into the world. That reign was evil, but it was not imaginary. So if the enemy was going to be defeated, it had to be by a legitimate work.

That legitimate work is redemption.

In Luke 4 the devil says the kingdoms of the world had been delivered to him. People sometimes respond by saying the devil always lies. We should be more careful than that. The devil’s whole agenda is to lead into falsehood, but that does not mean every sentence he ever speaks is false in isolation. He can speak truth in order to lead into a lie.

So when he says authority had been delivered to him, that fits the logic of man’s fall and the reign of sin and death.

Christ therefore had to do a redemptive work, bearing the consequence of sin and death legitimately, and then rise from the dead as proof of that redemption.

Hebrews 2:14 says that through death he destroyed him who had the power of death, that is, the devil. That destruction means the devil has lost his right, his governing authority, not that his operative power has vanished from the present age.

Colossians 2:15 says Christ disarmed principalities and powers and made a public spectacle of them. That is, he stripped them of authority.

So redemption is the primary act of resistance and opposition to the reign of the devil. It has obtained for us the legal right of victory.

## The Devil Is Defeated but Still Active as a Rebel

The devil has been defeated, but he is still active as a rebel.

This is where many people make mistakes. Redemption has taken away his authority, but it has not removed his power from the present age. Nor is our engagement in this age about erasing his power absolutely. Our engagement is about restraining the use of that power.

Because Christ has stripped him of authority, we can always resist, reject, and restrain his activity by the right and power of redemption.

That means deception can be restrained from gaining ground in your mind. The influence of temptations and lust can be restrained from gaining ground in your heart. Demons can be restrained from activity around you, in you, or toward you. Supernatural effects of darkness can be restrained.

## Personal and Ministerial Engagement

So how do we engage the war now?

First, redemption has already engaged the enemy and taken away his authority.

Second, there is personal engagement.

Third, there is ministerial engagement.

That ministerial engagement includes the word, prayer, and the ministry of the Spirit. By preaching the word, we cast down imaginations. By prayer, we set up events and circumstances against what the enemy wants to do. By the ministry of the Spirit, including commands in the name of Jesus, we restrain the enemy’s activity.

All of this belongs under the believer’s authority, which would need fuller treatment elsewhere.

## The End of the War

When will spiritual warfare end?

The war will end when God’s purpose is consummated. The gospel of the kingdom will be preached, and the end will come. The final end of the war is tied to the coming of the Lord and the final defeat of the enemy.

Revelation 20 gives a clear picture of this. The devil who deceived the nations is cast into the lake of fire. Death and Hades are cast into the lake of fire. The beast, the false prophet, the devil and his cohort, and all who remain outside the book of life end in the second death.

So the war ends when God’s purpose is consummated and the final judgment is executed.

## Our Present Duty

Until then, we have an occupying duty.

We are to understand the war we are in. We are to recognize the ongoing attack of falsehood, the ongoing attack of lust and darkness, the enemy’s attempt to harden people through deception or willful resistance, and the enemy’s attempt to produce supernatural effects of darkness.

And we are to restrain all of that by enforcing what redemption has accomplished, both personally and in ministry.

So for now, spiritual warfare should be clear. We are at war. There is an ongoing attack whether we like it or not. But we can engage that war and restrain the enemy by the victory of Christ.

That is what we are supposed to do in spiritual warfare.
