# Your Place in the Local Church

We are looking at a very important teaching: your place in the local church, on discipleship. And the long and short of this teaching, as I am starting, is to make it clear that the place where discipleship happens is the local church. Of course, that came up in our study on discipleship, where the third thing we saw on discipleship is that we commit to the local church. But we want to show your place in the local church because this one has another gist to it. It is like being in a family.

When you are in your family, there is a place you have, and your place changes in your family over time. When you were first born, everybody was attending to you. Everybody put their attention on you. Like when there is a new child, one person says, "That is my baby for now," but after a while the person's baby era will expire. But it is just normal. You are giving the person the required attention. When you have a new child, you give the required attention, and that is the place that child has in your family. But there is a point where the place of that child is changing. The person is now a participant. In fact, there is a time when the child is expected to be contributing to the family, or even when they are gone they are supposed to be sending things back, and those types of things. You can see that in a family your place changes.

So we are not looking at the work of the church to you as much in this particular teaching, even though that will come up somewhere. We are seeing the entire scope of your own journey in the church, your place in the local church. It is so important, and I want you to get it.

## The Church as Spiritual Reality and Natural Gathering

The first thing I want to do is quickly reestablish the idea of what we mean by the local church. Let us go to Matthew 16 as well as Matthew 18. Or you know what, let us go to Matthew 28 first, so we make a point that you would also have seen, I hope, in discipleship, the path of growth.

Now in Matthew 28 and in verse 18 the Lord Jesus said, "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore..." I told us that in this material this concept of heaven and on earth is a church concept. Every time the Lord used that concept it was relative to the authority that the church was to have, that He was giving to the church. So when He says all authority is given to Me in heaven and on earth, the way He uses heaven and earth there is relative to the church.

Let us see the first time He used it in Matthew 16 and verse 18. So He was talking to Peter about His church. He said, "You are Peter, and upon this rock I will build My church, and the gates of Hades will not prevail against it."

I do not want us to delve into issues about why Peter is not the rock that the church is built on. Even though Peter is no doubt the initial leader of the church, there is abundance of evidence to the fact that Peter had a place of primary leadership. Now the nature of how that primary leadership passed on is another debate entirely. That seems like it is beyond the scope of the Scriptures, and whether it was more of a functional passing on that was supposed to be figured out functionally rather than an ontological passing on. If we are going to be honest, we do not have evidence that it was resolved by the apostles.

People have tried to claim from historical evidence that it was resolved by them, but none of that historical evidence leans as strongly as they say. And so they have to go and claim another divine authority to validate itself, which is funny, because that divine authority is themselves validating themselves. But let us just leave that and not get into it.

Now Peter had primary leadership, of course. Generally, as a church, we take the position that from the primary leadership of Peter and the other apostles it was meant to unfold into functional leadership. So the church was meant to, which is what the church literally did, organize itself functionally in leadership going forward.

But that said, when Jesus was speaking and said, "Upon this rock I will build My church," it was not about Peter's primary leadership. It was rather about Peter's identification with Him who was the Rock upon which the church is built, something that Peter would also teach us about ourselves: that we have come to Jesus, 1 Peter 2:4, as to a living stone. So he calls Jesus a stone, rejected indeed by men, but chosen by God and precious. He now says, "You also, as living stones..." So it is something he learned from the Lord, that the Lord taught that we would be part of the building He is trying to make.

"I will build My church upon a rock," which is the Lord Himself, and you are part of it. That is why I am calling you a stone, and you are the primary leader in what I am building. So you are the person I am using as an example of it, but you are not the one that the church is built on. The evidence in this regard is overwhelming. People admit that there is no doubt it is clear in God's word that the church is built on Jesus, but then they say that in this particular case it was to show that the earthly church will be built on Peter. No. Peter being the primary leader is not the same thing as something being built on him. That concept of something being built on Peter is so strange to Scripture. But that Peter is primary leader is well evidenced in Scripture.

Now, when the Lord is talking about building His church and the gates of Hades not prevailing against it, it was about what will happen in the redemptive work He was going to do, that is, in His death and resurrection. In the death and resurrection of the Lord Jesus, death could not prevail against Him, something Peter himself taught in Acts 2:24, that it was not possible for Him to be held by it. So He was raised.

Out of this, the church is undefeatable by death. And that is not something we need to pray about, confess, or even hope for or expect in the future. It is something that is settled in the past. When you are saved, death has no hold on you and Hades has no hold on you. You have no business with death and Hades anymore. It is not about saying it will never happen that somebody dies physically. That is not the issue. Once you are part of the church, you are out of the domain of death, and it is permanent. You are now in the domain of life.

Now, I do believe that there is a guarantee in God's word as well of the church on earth never being defeated until the coming of Christ. I think there are other Scriptures for that, like this gospel of the kingdom being preached in all the world and then the end will come, meaning that the church will be active until the end. But this particular text is not really intended for that. We can only try to apply it that way, and it is fine that since we have defeated death in this manner, it will never be that death prevails against us even in our walk on earth. But what does it mean in itself? It is about the victory we have over death by what Christ has done once we are in Him.

But to show you that issue of heaven and earth relative to the church, what was the next statement? Verse 19: "I will give to you the keys of the kingdom of heaven, and whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven." Now interestingly here everything was in the singular, again showing Peter as having primary authority, which is why somebody wants to claim that it is ontological.

But by the time we get to Matthew 18 the Lord Jesus will use the church in another way. So at this juncture in Matthew 16, the church is being used as a spiritual reality, without any mindset to it being a natural reality. I will do a work in the spiritual, and then the church will defeat Hades and death permanently. So church here is a spiritual reality. Sometimes people call it the universal church, the church spiritual as a whole.

But in Matthew 18 the Lord will use the church in another way. In this instance, the church will be used as a natural reality, that is, a reality that subsists in the natural, not just one that exists in the spiritual.

Matthew 18 and verse 15: He was talking about how we resolve conflict, interpersonal conflict within the church. "If your brother sins against you, go and tell him his fault between you and him alone." Do not do silent treatment. Go and tell him his fault between you and him alone. "If he hears you, you have gained your brother. But if he will not hear you, take with you one or two more, that by the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established. And if he refuses to hear them, tell it to the church. But if he refuses even to hear the church, let him be to you like a heathen and a tax collector."

That is where the idea of withdrawing fellowship could even possibly come from. Now look at verse 18: "Assuredly I say to you, whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven." Do you see that the issue of binding and loosing comes up again, and the issue of heaven and earth comes up as well?

In this case, it was plural. You might not notice because everything is just "you" in English, but if you go and check it in your interlinear and so on, you will be able to see that the "you" in chapter 16 was singular to Peter, but the same authority was now plural here, showing that it was a matter of church leadership as a whole having that authority, Peter being the primary leader, the other apostles having that same authority that would now devolve to people functionally from there.

So there is ecclesial authority. That is the authority that the church, particularly leadership, can wield. The leadership in partnership with the congregation, yes, but the leadership usually decisively can wield it. Why am I saying that? Some people like to talk about a congregationalist model. As nice as it sounds, if you really want to square it up with Scripture, it is not like that.

For example, when Paul instructed them in 1 Corinthians to withdraw fellowship and good pleasure from the man that took his father's wife, not everybody obeyed. In the second letter he said, "I wanted to test your obedience," and he said a number of people obeyed, but there was an insinuation underneath that some people did not obey. Yet the leadership took the initiative and went on. So when He says, "Tell it to the church," in this case it is very likely the episcopal authority that says, looking at this matter, this person has been warned already, you have not changed, so the church withdraws fellowship from the person. This can only happen if the person is a committed member, to correct that wrong.

Now look at verse 19 again: "Again I say to you that if two of you agree on earth concerning anything that they ask, it will be done for them by My Father in heaven. For where two or three are gathered together in My name, there I am in the midst of them." Clearly it is about a natural gathering, where two or three are gathered together.

So there is church as this spiritual reality brought about by the work that Christ has done. But there is church as the gathering, the natural gathering of people that are of that spiritual reality. There is church as that spiritual reality. There is church as that natural reality or gathering. And what we often call the local church is that assembly, that gathering. So when we talk about your place in the local church, we are talking about that community of believers that you belong to.

## Discipleship Is the Basis of the Natural Gathering

So the first thing I wanted to establish again for us is that there is the reality of the local church, the natural gathering of people that belong to the spiritual church. What is the basis of that natural gathering? Matthew 28. Discipleship is the basis of that natural gathering.

Matthew 28 is where the Lord gave the instruction that was going to be the basis of the church, that natural gathering, the local church. It was discipleship that led up to it. Matthew 28 says, "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth." Do what? "Go therefore and make disciples." Do not forget this authority in heaven and on earth is church authority according to Matthew 16 and Matthew 18. So by the time He was saying, "I have that authority," we can say cosmological authority that should be handed over to the church as ecclesial authority. Therefore, in giving that cosmological and ecclesial authority, He says, "Go and make disciples."

So discipleship is what births the natural gathering. Very important, very important to state that Jesus never said, "Go and start church." He said, "Go and make disciples." But the natural result is church, people gathered together to be discipled.

Is discipleship the only thing we are supposed to do? No. Now if discipleship means we are supposed to observe all that He commanded, it means everything that comes out of discipleship, we are also supposed to do. For example, one of the things that comes out of discipleship is fellowshipping with one another in love. Do you know one of the most emphatic instructions in God's word is, "Greet one another with a holy kiss"? Do you know? It is almost everywhere. Romans, 1 Corinthians 16, toward the end of Romans 16, almost everywhere: greet one another with a holy kiss.

For them, you know, that was their culture. But it was showing you the kind of welcome believers are supposed to have. There is a way you can just welcome someone from afar, and there is a way you can welcome someone very warmly. So that means when we finish church, as a closure, whenever you start to see those complimentary closings, greet one another with a holy kiss, it is supposed to be part of church that we have a warm welcome for one another. You welcome one another warmly, and you do not give any excuse.

That means, of course, as we grow larger there is a limit, but you should always try. At least you look for a circle. Maybe the church is now so large, imagine one of the branches and it is like one thousand people. If you say greet one another with a holy kiss or with a warm welcome, you can look for a circle, maybe your row, maybe this section. You try to greet at least maybe five or ten people warmly. It is biblical instruction. You greet them warmly. "How are you doing? How are you really doing? What has been going on? Are you okay? Hope you are fine." There is that spirit. That means the church has that phileo spirit, friendliness. He is so emphatic.

So discipleship is what led to the church. Jesus says go and make disciples.

## The Church at the Core Is the Family of God

You know one thing we can also say: why does He want us to make disciples? Because of God's purpose. So we can say the church is really the home of God's purpose. God wanted a family. That is what the church is. That would mean that at the core the church is the family of God.

That is why I started out using the family idea to describe your place in the local church. He said He sets the solitary in families. What is the church at the core? God's family. Why? Every individual there is in God's purpose and in His sonship, which means they are children of God. If we "What is the church in the most definitive manner?" the church is the family of God.

Why then did He give instruction on discipleship? Because that family of God needs to be trained to fulfill God's purpose, to be able to function as children and as heirs. Do not forget that being a child of God and being an heir of God, they are all family issues. Do you know even our natural assignment is a family issue? Our spiritual assignment is a family issue. Everything about our life is a family issue. As members of God's family, we have work to do. It is all a family issue. Imagine that is how you treat your life: this is all a family issue. I am just in the family of God. We are all just doing family business, family business, things that pertain to the family.

So when Jesus gave that instruction on discipleship, it is because discipleship is how the family purpose that God has, family purpose including functioning as children and as heirs, will be fulfilled. So that is what makes discipleship the core of the local church, because the local church at its core is about family.

I know sometimes we "Oh, it is more of the mission and training. It is not really about family, even though we are still family." The mission is a family affair. The training is a family affair. Training was a thing in their families in the Greco-Roman world. You take your child through a definite training. You might even have a particular person you send him to, like Paul was sent to Gamaliel. You would decide the kind of pedagogy that you want him to go through. The church is a place of discipleship for the sake of family.

Family is the most defining definition of the church. If I can just get that into you and make you appreciate discipleship from the standpoint of family, then I think we have gotten the major thing done. So when we talk about your place in the local church, we are talking about your place in your most important family, your core family.

Even your natural family should reflect the church. Imagine we function in our homes like it is also church, where fellowship activities go on. Family is a church subset. Every family automatically is a church unit. Automatically it should be like that, where they are now also pastoring the children, and in that pastoring that is going on they might now also be pastoring other families as well, and those families themselves are now also learning to pastor their own families, and they can now be multiplying on and on. If we appreciate the family within God's purpose well, it will help with the furtherance of the mission greatly. The church should reflect that family.

## Your Third Place: Worker

Third place in the local church is worker. The funny thing is that all these realities are already resident in you from salvation, even to the last thing we are going to see in your place in the local church. All of those things are already realities resident in you from salvation, yet you are supposed to grow and unfold into them.

The third place is that of a worker. In 2 Corinthians 5 it is clear that when we get saved and become a new creation, we are meant to be workers. We are meant to serve. But it is also clear that believers can be weak and may not yet be functioning in their role. So we are showing how to unfold.

Do you know what is interesting? Some people can unfold speedily. Someone can get saved and join and even start serving in one day. It is possible. And yet for another person it can be a journey. And what do we do in both cases? You just make available everything to help them grow. You know what happens sometimes? Because we are seeing someone have this speedy growth, you start to put pressure on the person that is not like that, and someone that could have also turned out strong now collapses under the weight of pressure. That is not the best way to go.

We are called to be workers. We are already designed as workers from the point of salvation, which, as we will see, means you are already designed as a leader from the point of salvation too. But let us start with worker. The third place in the local church is the place of a worker.

I want you to see 2 Corinthians 5:17. At the point of salvation you are already stepping into your spiritual assignment, your responsibility in your spiritual assignment.

There is the argument from Acts 6 that those people were deacons, even though there is no clear-cut statement to that effect. But they were called to come and serve tables. That is why we have that idea. And then when we say the word deacons, those who serve, then we take it into Acts 6, that the people who were called to serve tables are deacons. And it is okay. I believe it is correct because they were assisting those who were doing the primary duty of ministering the message on that oversight level, and they were assisting them by serving tables, by serving people.

You hear of Elisha: he poured water on Elijah's hands. He was serving Elijah. You hear of Joshua: he served Moses. So the idea of deacon is that you are serving a person doing a work. You are serving the person. You are assisting the person. Therefore, deacon: you are a servant helping that person.

See Acts 6. See what the apostles said when the number of disciples was multiplying. They did not have the structure to carry the demand that was coming on them, so people were getting neglected. This is why structure is very important, and that structure involves people. As you can see, they had to choose people.

Look at verse 2: "Then the twelve summoned the multitude of the disciples and said, 'It is not desirable that we should leave the word of God and serve tables.'" That means this dispersion of attention can distract us. That is the reality. "Therefore, brethren, seek out from among you seven men of good reputation, full of the Holy Spirit and wisdom, whom we may appoint over this business." Something we have been doing, let them serve us in doing it. Deacons. Let them help us in doing it. "But we will give ourselves continually to prayer and to the ministry of the word."

So there is the role of deacons, which is actually a training ground for unfolding into your leadership. So the fourth place in a local church is that of a deacon.

Go to 1 Timothy 3. It is a training ground for unfolding into the leadership that God has for you. And I will show you that it is already a design for you to end up as a leader in God's purpose. Let me say it unequivocally. Even though people might want to feel this or that, if you do not end up as a leader in the local church, it is likely you did not fully fulfill God's plan. It is likely, because other conditions can affect things, so let us say it that way. There might be exceptions. And being the managing director somewhere does not stop you from being that.

Not for anything, but so that you can arrange yourself as you are making progress little by little toward a clear destination that is biblical. Natural assignment is part of God's will for your life, a very important part, and that will be judged. But this other one, spiritual assignment, if you do not end up as a leader, check it. It is very likely that you are not fulfilling God's purpose.

First Timothy 3:8: "Likewise deacons must be reverent..." So it just means servant, but you can tell this is a leadership position as a servant. So it is a deacon responsibility.

And how does serving as a deacon work? Once you are in the church, you are already a worker. You are doing your spiritual assignment. You are helping in natural affairs in the church, basic things, music, arranging chairs, all of that. You are available. Availability is the key issue. When someone is available, you just tell that person, "Can you help us with this?" But many people are not available. You are always weary. Should we tell them to help us with this? No.

You are available. You are growing. You are doing all of these things. When leadership has more burdens, they will pass on responsibility to you. You are now a deacon.

"Likewise deacons must be reverent, not double-tongued, not given to much wine, not greedy for money, holding the mystery of the faith with a pure conscience. But let these also first be tested..." That means there is a point where they were just workers before you gave them that leadership role as deacons. So that word deacon, servant, is a leadership role. There is even the test to be a deacon. But being a deacon is also a test to become something else.

"Let these first be tested, then let them serve as deacons, being found blameless." Likewise their wives, or the women, must be reverent, not slanderers, temperate, faithful in all things. "Let deacons be the husbands of one wife," one-woman man, as the case may be. "Ruling their children and their own houses well." For those who have served well as deacons, what happens to them? "They obtain for themselves a good standing and great boldness in the faith which is in Christ Jesus."

That means they now have the right to be promoted to what comes next.

## Your Fifth Place: Bishop, Leader, Pastor, Elder

The fifth place in the local church: bishops, leaders, pastors, elders. That is the fifth place in the local church. And that is how we summarize it for now. We will keep it as five.

You start as a saint. You become a member. You become a worker, a deacon, then a leader, a bishop. You are leading a unit, or even you might now be leading a church. Anyone that does not function in leadership, I am not sure that you have fulfilled God's purpose. I will be honest with you. It depends because of exceptions, because of circumstances and time, but the general trajectory of our spiritual assignment and our participation in the church ought to terminate in leadership.

Before we come back to 1 Timothy 3, let us see Ephesians 4:7, that it is already designed by God for that to happen to you. Then we will come back and see the part about bishops.

Ephesians 4:7 says, "But to each one of us grace was given according to the measure of Christ's gift." How many people did He say grace was given to? Each one of us. That means there is nobody here that does not have grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ. Nobody.

And what is the gift of Christ? Verse 8 says that when He ascended on high, He led captivity captive and gave gifts to men. The gift of Christ is the gift that Christ gives to humanity. So there is a gift Christ is giving humanity, and He has given us grace according to that gift. The Lord has given you as a gift to humanity. "I am a gift to humanity."

If each of us have grace according to the gift, and that gift is gift to humanity, that means each of us are gifts to humanity. We have grace for that. "I am a gift to humanity for their salvation, establishment in, and fulfillment of God's purpose." That is it. That means there are people whose salvation into God's purpose depends on you. Their establishment in God's purpose depends on you. The fulfillment of God's purpose depends on you. You are a gift Christ has given, with grace to back it up.

And in fact He now explains that gift better in verse 11: apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachers. He gave you as people He sends to speak His word, to evangelize it, and then to oversee those who believe it. Christ gave you as the gift of apostle, prophet, evangelist, pastor, teacher to humanity, and He gave you grace for that, for your own measure in that.

I tell you the truth, that is what is there in the revelation of God in His word. If the authoritative document for God's truth and revelation says so, I accept it. You are God's gift to humanity in your own measure, and you have the grace for that measure. What are you doing about it?

Your place in the local church cannot just remain, "It is fine, I am just attending, I am hearing the word, wonderful." You must unfold. You must move from saint, that is an attendee, to member. You must move from member to worker. You must move from worker to deacon, and from deacon to bishop or elder or pastor, where you are now serving and leading people.

To show you that leadership is open to all believers, look at what Paul told Timothy. Because it is in the natural trajectory of the doctrine that all believers have that grace to end up as leaders, he told him that it is not strange for anyone to desire it. It is okay. If there was an exemption, he would have said so. And if the caveat was that you cannot desire it but some are specially called to it, he would have said so. He said it as something that can happen to anybody if they are so qualified by the unfolding and by their character.

As desirable as it is, if you do not have the character for it, it is not right to be dabbling into it. You should first fix the character so that you can now engage in it. Those are things people do not want to accept. If you really care for mission and for souls, why, when you know that you are still going through a lot, would you not deal with the character?

1 Timothy 3:1: "This is a faithful saying: If a man desires the position of a bishop, he desires a good work." Very clear, very open. If he desires bishopric, the office of a bishop, the reality of a bishop, the position of a bishop, he desires a good work.

"A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife, temperate, sober-minded, of good behavior, hospitable, able to teach..." He keeps giving all the qualifications. He must have controlled sexual appetite, restricted to one woman, or as the case may be. When someone has unbridled sexual appetite, particularly of the kind that seeks several partners, it is one of the earliest things mentioned. In fact, it is the first thing talked about after blamelessness. That word blameless is a big word for every other thing, because these are the things that always affect the church, aside from financial exploitation, which is also mentioned as early as possible because greed is also an issue. He goes on and gives us all the qualifications, warns against pride as well. He says he must have a good testimony among those who are outside, lest he fall into reproach and the snare of the devil.

This is your place in the local church. So you are in the family of God. What is your place? It is like that person that is born into a house. All of us are giving the person attention. Then over time the person grows. Then the person is now serving until, at a time, the person is now taking care of responsibilities because they have come of age, can take responsibility, are helping the family, and so on. In that regard, at one point your parents were the ones taking care of you. You may grow to a point where you have to be the one taking care of them. Your place in the local church begins with the place of a saint, then member, worker, deacon, and then leader, bishop, and so on. Deacon is assistant leader. Bishop is leader.

## What Determines How You Unfold

And so it is my desire for you that you unfold unto that end, that you grow until you start to function in that place in the local church. You must be able to know how that happens. There are two things, or maybe I should say three, that determine that happening, how you unfold.

Number one is your own growth, your own personal journey of growth. Number two is your ministry. You cannot qualify for leadership without the requisite knowledge, growth, stamina, and capacity to bear it. Your growth is important because in leadership you have to teach. If you do not know what to teach, your growth in knowledge and conduct is very key. Capacity is so key.

Number two is your ministry, because your ministry is what will bring people into your hands. But because of the way ministry is these days, ministry these days is a partnership. Sometimes it will be those you directly disciple. Sometimes it will be those who trickle in by the joint work of ministry that we are doing, and they are put under your oversight because of your capacity.

Number three is orchestrations and leadings. God can orchestrate that you are supposed to go somewhere, and that is why your work will be done there. God can lead you that you now have to go and do something in a particular place, and that is why your work is to be done there. Number three is orchestrations and leadings. They determine how the whole unfolding process happens and how you therefore fulfill your spiritual assignment.

You have two things in your control: your growth and your service. Plug in. If you cannot do service because you are weak, start with your growth. There is nobody that cannot do growth. There is no kind of weakness that means, "I cannot do growth." That would be like saying, "I cannot eat." That one is now a sickness. That one is a problem.

Once you are growing, you will get to the point where you can now serve. And once that is happening, God is always faithful to His orchestration and His leading. Then you will now be able to do your specific roles in the body of Christ.

Sometimes there will be no dramatic leading. It is just normal orchestration, that everything is being arranged because God wants you to lead some people, and it just happens over time. We just have these people and "Ah, brother so-and-so should lead this particular team, maybe this unit, maybe even a virtual unit." You just see events unfold that way. Lead them. With that, you are leading them. Before you know it, you are becoming way stronger.

Before you know it, God can orchestrate that you are supposed to go to another place, and in that place maybe there are already people there, or you go and start a work there. And there may be a direct leading: "I want you to actually go to this particular place and do something there."

That is how it works in your place in the local church. From just being an attendee, you should grow into a functional leader that can now even be specifically led on different things to do. Timothy grew to the point where he was now being led, "Stay in Ephesus, Timothy." That is how we should think about our life in God's purpose.

We have spiritual assignment and natural assignment to fulfill. And there is so much ahead of us to do in God's purpose.
