# Your Place in the Local Church

We are looking at a very important teaching: your place in the local church, on discipleship. The long and short of this teaching is to make it clear that the place where discipleship happens is the local church. Of course, that came up in our study on discipleship, where the third thing we saw is that we commit to the local church. But here we want to show your place in the local church, because this has another gist to it. It is like being in a family.

When you are in your family, there is a place you have, and your place changes over time. When you were first born, everybody was attending to you. Everybody put their attention on you. In a more common way, when there is a new child, one person says, "That is my baby for now," but after a while the person's baby era will expire. That is normal. You give the required attention to a new child, and that is the place that child has in the family. But there is a point where the place of that child changes. The person becomes a participant. In fact, there is a time when the child is expected to be contributing to the family, and there are times when, after they are gone, they are supposed to be sending things back.

You can see that in a family your place changes. So we are not looking at the work of the church to you as much in this teaching, even though that will come up. We are seeing the entire scope of your own journey in the church, your place in the local church. It is so important, and I want you to get it.

## The Church as Spiritual Reality and Natural Gathering

The first thing I want to do is quickly reestablish what we mean by the local church. Let us go to Matthew 16, Matthew 18, and Matthew 28, to see that when the Lord spoke about the church, there is a path of growth to it.

In Matthew 28 and verse 18, the Lord Jesus said, "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth." I told us that in this material, this concept of heaven and on earth is a church concept. Every time the Lord used that concept, it was relative to the authority that the church was to have, that He was giving to the church.

Let us see the first time He used it, in Matthew 16 and verse 18. He was talking to Peter about His church. He said, "You are Peter, and upon this rock I will build My church, and the gates of Hades will not prevail against it."

I do not want us to delve into issues about why Peter is not the rock that the church is built on. Even though Peter is no doubt the initial leader of the church, and there is abundance of evidence that Peter had a place of primary leadership, the nature of how that primary leadership passed on is another debate entirely. If we are going to be honest, we do not have evidence that it was resolved by the apostles the way some claim.

But let us leave that and not get into it. When Jesus was speaking, "upon this rock I will build My church," it was not about Peter's primary leadership. It was rather about Peter's identification with Him who was the Rock upon which the church is built. Peter himself teaches us this in 1 Peter 2:4, where he speaks of Jesus as a living stone, rejected indeed by men, but chosen by God and precious. Then he says, "You also, as living stones..." So Peter learned from the Lord that we are part of the building He is making.

"I will build My church upon a rock," which is the Lord Himself, and you guys are part of it. That is why I am calling you a stone, and you are the primary leader in what I am building. So you are the person I am using as an example of it, but you are not the one the church is built on. The evidence in this regard is overwhelming.

Now, when the Lord was talking about building His church and the gates of Hades not prevailing against it, it was about what would happen in the redemptive work He was going to do, in His death and resurrection. In the death and resurrection of the Lord Jesus, death could not hold Him. Peter himself taught this in Acts 2:24, that it was not possible for Him to be held by it. So He was raised.

Out of this, the church is undefeatable by death. This is not something we need to pray about, confess, or hope for in the future. It is something that is settled in the past. When you are saved, death has no hold on you and Hades has no hold on you. You have no business with death and Hades anymore. Once you are part of the church, you are out of the domain of death permanently. You are now in the domain of life.

I do believe there is a guarantee in God's word as well that the church on earth will never be defeated until the coming of Christ. There are other Scriptures for that. But this particular text is not really intended for that first. What it means in itself is about the victory we have over death by what Christ has done, once we are in Him.

Then in verse 19 He says, "I will give to you the keys of the kingdom of heaven, and whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven." Interestingly, everything was singular there, again showing Peter as having primary authority.

But by the time we get to Matthew 18, the Lord Jesus uses the church in another way. In Matthew 16, the church is being used as a spiritual reality, without any immediate mindset to it being a natural reality. But in Matthew 18, the church is used as a natural reality, a reality that subsists in the natural, not just one that exists in the spiritual.

In Matthew 18:15, He was talking about how we resolve interpersonal conflict within the church. "If your brother sins against you, go and tell him his fault between you and him alone." Do not do silent treatment. Go and tell him his fault between you and him alone. If he hears you, you have gained your brother. If he will not hear you, take with you one or two more, that by the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established. If he refuses to hear them, tell it to the church. If he refuses to hear the church, let him be to you like a heathen or a tax collector.

That is where the idea of withdrawing fellowship could even possibly come from. Then in verse 18, He says again, "Assuredly I say to you, whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven." Do you see that the issue of binding and loosing comes up again, and the issue of heaven and earth comes up as well?

In this case it is plural. The same authority that was singular in Matthew 16 to Peter is now plural here, showing that it was a matter of church leadership as a whole having that authority. Peter being the primary leader, the other apostles having that same authority, and that would now devolve functionally from there. So there is ecclesial authority, the authority that the church, particularly leadership, can wield.

Then look at verse 19: "Again I say to you that if two of you agree on earth concerning anything that they ask, it will be done for them by My Father in heaven. For where two or three are gathered together in My name, there I am in the midst of them." Clearly, this is about a natural gathering, where two or three are gathered together.

So there is church as this spiritual reality brought about by the work Christ has done, and there is church as the natural gathering of people who are of that spiritual reality. What we often call the local church is that assembly, that gathering. So when we talk about your place in the local church, we are talking about that community of believers that you belong to.

## God Sets the Solitary in Families

What is that community of believers you belong to? I like the way God's word says it in Psalm 68. God wants all of us to belong to a community of believers. He puts us in families.

Verse 5 says, "A father of the fatherless, a defender of widows, is God in His holy habitation." Then verse 6 says, "God sets the solitary in families; He brings out those who are bound into prosperity; but the rebellious dwell in a dry land."

If you are isolated, it is not normal. It is not normal for you to be alone. No matter where you are in your discipleship phase, even if you are still seeking God's word on things, go to church. If you do not agree, eventually you will not commit, and if you now see somewhere else that you better agree with, you go there. But it is not God's idea that you just stay alone, isolated, solitary. He sets the solitary in families.

The local church is something God wants for every believer once you have believed the gospel. If you are not doing that, then of course you are working in the flesh, because He calls it rebellion. It means you are disobeying an instruction in God's word.

I am definitely of the persuasion that people should join or commit to a local church that matches their persuasion. That is basic, because it is impossible to obey God's word and not be a hypocrite if you are committed to a local church that does not match your persuasion. Also, if you have more light in God's word, it must be God's orchestration to you. It cannot be the devil giving you more light in God's word.

That means it may be of God that you join a church that matches your knowledge and persuasion in God's word. I believe this is a basic Luke 12:47-48 principle. He that knows his master's will and does not prepare himself nor do according to his will shall be beaten with many stripes. Preparation will often be in the local church. But he that does not know, yet does things deserving of stripes, shall be beaten with few. So God is aware that some people will not have as much light as others. But there are some who have more light and do not match their commitment with the light they have. That is wrong.

I am not saying people should leave a local church in a dishonorable manner. Please, no. Sometimes even having more light may not automatically mean leaving a local church. There might be ways you can share what you know in that local church, and if they accept it, it might transform that church to the degree of what you now know. But there are ways you will be able to discern, either before telling it or after you have told it, that it will not work. Then the most honorable thing for you to do is to leave honorably.

Never do anything in an unruly manner. If you want to do something by the Spirit, why do it by the flesh? It is of the Spirit that you now know, and it is of the Spirit that you are supposed to make that decision. So do it by the Spirit: honorably, lovingly, peaceably. As much as it is up to you, be at peace with all men. On your own part, do everything you should do, giving it the time that it requires before you can rightly transition.

There is an honorable way of doing it. If you have responsibility somewhere, you do not just drop everything. You hand it over properly. We are talking about people's lives. You do it in a way that is rightly handed over.

## Discipleship Is the Basis of the Local Church

The first thing I wanted to establish again is that there is the reality of the local church, the natural gathering of people who belong to the spiritual church. What is the basis of that natural gathering? Matthew 28. Discipleship is the basis of that natural gathering.

Matthew 28 says, "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore and make disciples." Do not forget that this authority in heaven and on earth is church authority according to Matthew 16 and Matthew 18. So by the time He was saying, "I have that authority," we can say that cosmological authority would be handed over to the church as ecclesial authority. Therefore He says, "Go and make disciples."

Discipleship is what births the natural gathering. It is very important to state that Jesus never said, "Go and start church." He said, "Go and make disciples." But the natural result is church: people gathered together to be discipled.

Is discipleship the only thing we are supposed to do? No. But if discipleship means we are to observe all that He commanded, then everything that comes out of discipleship, we are also supposed to do. For example, fellowshipping with one another in love is part of it. One of the most emphatic instructions in God's word is, "Greet one another with a holy kiss." For them, that was their culture, but it was showing you the kind of warm welcome believers are supposed to have for one another.

So when we finish church, it should be part of church life that we welcome one another warmly. As churches grow larger, there is a limit, but you should still try. You can greet a circle, your row, five or ten people, warmly and genuinely. "How are you doing? How are you really doing? What has been going on? Are you okay?" The church should have that phileo spirit, that friendliness.

Why does He want us to make disciples? Because of God's purpose. The church is really the home of God's purpose. God wanted a family. That is what the church is. At the core, the church is the family of God.

That is why I started out using the family idea to describe your place in the local church. He sets the solitary in families. What is the church at the core? God's family. Why? Every individual there is in God's purpose and in His sonship, which means they are children of God. If we ask in the most definitive manner what the church is, the church is the family of God.

Then why discipleship? Because that family of God needs to be trained to fulfill God's purpose, to function as children and as heirs. Everything about our life is a family issue. Our spiritual assignment is a family issue. Even our natural assignment is a family issue. As members of God's family, we have work to do. It is all family business.

So when Jesus gave the instruction on discipleship, it was because discipleship is how the family purpose of God will be fulfilled. That is what makes discipleship the core of the local church, because the local church at its core is about family.

When we talk about your place in the local church, we are talking about your place in your most important family, your core family. Even your natural family should reflect the church. Every family should be a church unit, where there is fellowship and pastoring going on, and from there other families can be helped as well.

## Your First Place: A Saint, Believer, and Learner

So what is your place in this family, in the local church? The very first place you have is the place of a saint, a believer, or a learner. That means you are saved, you are a believer, and you are a learner.

You see this very commonly in the epistles. They speak to believers as saints. Saints just means sanctified. In 1 Corinthians 1:2 Paul writes, "To the church of God which is at Corinth, to those who are sanctified in Christ Jesus, called saints." The very least you can be in the church is a saint. Except we are talking about your place as an unbeliever, and at that point you are not yet really in the church.

The first and primary place of any person in the local church is the place of a saint. That is why in Ephesians 4:11 the leaders of the church are given for the perfecting of the saints. It does not matter how far back you are. You are a saint, and you are there to be perfected. It does not matter what your struggles are. You are a saint, and you will be perfected.

This is why our understanding of saint matters as well. There is a concept of saint that can be misleading, as though being a saint is an accomplishment. No. Being a saint is Christ's accomplishment for you, on your behalf. He has sanctified you. A saint is not an accomplishment of your deeds.

Every believer must fully recognize themselves as a saint. The church is a community of saints. That means you must be embracing of saints in their different stages. The church is not a community of committed people first. The church is not even a community of people who look like they are going to be strong. The church is a community of saints, believers.

Acts 5 says believers were increasingly added to the Lord, multitudes both of men and women. Acts 2:47 says, "The Lord added to the church daily those who were being saved." Not those who had already changed everything externally, but those who were being saved. Verse 41 says, "Those who gladly received his word were baptized." That is, they believed.

So your first place is being a saint, a believer, saved, a learner.

## Your Second Place: A Member of a Local Church

Your second place in the church is a member. You are saved, you are coming to church, and at that point you are an attendee. You are learning. But the second place you should have in the local church is that of a member.

Member means you have now joined the church. The church may have different ways of measuring that, but there is such a thing as joining a church. You have been around, you are a saint, you have been coming around, but you have now joined.

We will see that there is an idea of joining church in the Bible. People think it is strange, but you can join a church. Acts 5:13 says, "Yet none of the rest dared join them." So you can join the church. There is such a thing as commitment: "I have joined this church."

Acts 17:34 says, "However, some men joined him and believed," among them Dionysius the Areopagite and a woman named Damaris. So there is joining. Here you are accepting this as your church family. The Lord sets the solitary in families. You are accepting that church family, and you are also submitting to spiritual authority. That now makes you available for further training.

## Your Third Place: A Worker

The third place in the local church is that of a worker. The funny thing is that all these realities are already resident in you from salvation, even to the last thing we are going to see. Yet you are supposed to grow and unfold into them.

In 2 Corinthians 5 it is clear that when we get saved and become a new creation, we are meant to be workers. We are meant to serve. Yet it is also clear that believers can be weak, and they may not yet be functioning in their role. So we are showing how to unfold.

Some people can unfold speedily. Someone can get saved, join, and even start serving in one day. It is possible. For another person it can be a journey. In both cases, what do we do? We make available everything to help them grow. Sometimes because we are seeing someone have speedy growth, we start to put pressure on someone else who is not like that, and someone who could have turned out strong collapses under the weight of pressure. That is not the best way to go.

But we are called to be workers. We are already designed as workers from the point of salvation. In fact, as we will see, you are already designed as a leader from the point of salvation too.

In 2 Corinthians 5:17, "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation." All things have become new, and all things are of God, who has reconciled us to Himself through Jesus Christ and has given to us the ministry of reconciliation. Verse 19 says He has committed to us the word of reconciliation. That is what we are supposed to minister: the message of reconciliation.

Then he shows that we can describe this reality with the status of an ambassador: "Now then, we are ambassadors for Christ, as though God were pleading through us." Then in chapter 6 he says, "We then, as workers together with Him..." So the third place in the local church is the place of a worker.

You are now stepping into your spiritual assignment, your responsibility in your spiritual assignment. At salvation, you are already designed for it.

We should also note the tone of ministry. We do not usually see the language of force. There is warning where necessary, but the natural disposition in the work of ministry is to plead, to beg, to persuade: "Take a look at this. Think about it." Not to be forceful, prideful, dismissive, obnoxious, or standoffish.

So let us recap. The first place in the church is saint: you are saved, you have believed, and you are learning. The second place is member: you have committed to a church. The third place is worker: you are serving.

## Your Fourth Place: A Deacon

The fourth place in the local church is deacon. Yes, deacon. You are supposed to serve as an assistant leader.

The word deacon simply means servant. The idea of a deacon is that the deacon is serving a person in the work. That is why one of the best ways to describe a deacon is as an assistant leader.

In Acts 6, when the number of disciples was multiplying, the structure they had could no longer carry the demand, so some people were being neglected. This is why structure is important, and that structure involves people.

The apostles said, "It is not desirable that we should leave the word of God and serve tables." That means this other task can distract us from our primary duty. "Therefore, brethren, seek out from among you seven men of good reputation, full of the Holy Spirit and wisdom, whom we may appoint over this business." In other words, let them help us in doing it. "But we will give ourselves continually to prayer and to the ministry of the word."

So there is the role of deacons, and it is actually a training ground for unfolding into your leadership. Once you are in the church, you are already a worker. You are doing your spiritual assignment. You are helping in natural affairs in the church, basic things, music, arranging chairs, all of that. Availability is key. When someone is available, leadership can say, "Can you help us with this?" But many people are not available.

As you grow and become available, when leadership burdens increase, responsibility is passed on to you. You are now functioning as a deacon.

In 1 Timothy 3:8 Paul says, "Likewise deacons must be reverent, not double-tongued, not given to much wine, not greedy for money, holding the mystery of the faith with a pure conscience. But let these also first be tested; then let them serve as deacons, being found blameless."

That means there is a point where they were just workers before you gave them that leadership role as deacons. So that word deacon, servant, is a leadership role. There is even a test to be a deacon, but being a deacon is also a test to become something else.

## Your Fifth Place: A Leader

The fifth place in the local church is bishop, leader, pastor, elder. That is the fifth place in the local church.

You start as a saint. You become a member, then a worker, then a deacon, then a leader or bishop. You are leading a unit, or eventually you may be leading a church. But if you never function in leadership, brothers and sisters, I am not sure you have fully fulfilled God's purpose. I will be honest with you. There may be exceptions because of circumstances and time, but the general trajectory of our spiritual assignment and our participation in the church ought to terminate in leadership.

Before we come back to 1 Timothy 3, see Ephesians 4:7. It is already designed by God for this to happen to you. "But to each one of us grace was given according to the measure of Christ's gift." How many people? Each one of us. There is nobody here who does not have grace according to the measure of Christ's gift.

What is the gift of Christ? Verse 8 says that when He ascended on high, He gave gifts to men. There is a gift Christ is giving to humanity, and He has given us grace according to that gift. That means each of us is a gift to humanity. The Lord has given you as a gift to humanity.

There are people whose salvation, establishment in God's purpose, and fulfillment of God's purpose depend on you. You are a gift Christ has given, and you have grace to back it up. Verse 11 explains the gift further: apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers. Christ gave you as His gift to humanity in your own measure, and He gave you grace for that measure.

What are you doing about it? Your place in the local church cannot remain at attendance. You must unfold. You must move from saint to member, from member to worker, from worker to deacon, and from deacon to bishop, elder, or pastor, where you are serving and leading people.

To show you that leadership is open to believers, look at what Paul told Timothy. Because this is in the natural trajectory of the doctrine, all believers have grace to end up as leaders. He said it is not strange for anyone to desire it. If there were some special exemption, he would have said so.

1 Timothy 3:1 says, "This is a faithful saying: If a man desires the position of a bishop, he desires a good work." Very clear, very open. Then he lists the qualifications: blameless, husband of one wife, temperate, sober-minded, of good behavior, hospitable, able to teach, and so on. He speaks against greed, pride, and lack of character. He says the bishop must have a good testimony among those who are outside, lest he fall into reproach and the snare of the devil.

This is your place in the local church. You are in the family of God. Your place begins with being a saint, then a member, a worker, a deacon, and then a leader. Deacon is assistant leader; bishop is leader.

## What Determines How You Unfold

It is my desire for you that you unfold to that end, that you grow until you start to function in that place in the local church. You must know how that happens.

There are two things, or maybe three, that determine that unfolding.

Number one is your own growth, your personal journey of growth. You cannot qualify for leadership without the requisite knowledge, growth, stamina, and capacity to bear it. In leadership, you have to teach. If you do not know how to teach, your growth in knowledge and conduct is very key. Capacity is key.

Number two is your ministry. Your ministry is what will bring people into your hands. Because of the way ministry is these days, it is often a partnership. Sometimes it will be those you directly disciple. Sometimes it will be those who trickle in by the joint work of ministry that we are doing, and then they are put under your oversight because of your capacity.

Number three is orchestrations and leadings. God can orchestrate that you are supposed to go somewhere, and that is why your work will be done there. God can lead you that you are now to go and do something in a particular place. Or sometimes there is no dramatic leading; it is just normal orchestration. Events unfold over time because God wants you to lead some people.

So you have two things in your control: your growth and your service. Plug in. If you cannot do service because you are weak, start with your growth. There is no kind of weakness that means you cannot grow. That would be like saying, "I cannot eat." That is now a sickness. That is a problem.

Once you are growing, you will get to the point where you can serve. And once that is happening, God is always faithful to His orchestration and His leading. Then you will be able to do your specific roles in the body of Christ.

Sometimes it just happens over time. We say, "Brother so-and-so should lead this particular team, maybe this unit, maybe even a virtual unit." Events unfold that way. Then before you know it, you are becoming stronger. And then God may orchestrate that you are supposed to go to another place, and in that place maybe there are already people there, or maybe you are to start a work there. Or there may be a direct leading: "I want you to go to this particular place and do something there."

That is how it works. In your place in the local church, from just being an attendee, you should grow into a functional leader who can now even be specifically led on different things to do. Timothy grew to the point where he could be told, "Stay in Ephesus." That is how we should think about our life in God's purpose.

We have spiritual assignment and natural assignment to fulfill, and there is so much ahead of us to do in God's purpose.
