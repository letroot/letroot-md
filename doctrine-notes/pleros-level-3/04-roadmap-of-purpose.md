## God's Purpose in Ephesians 1

Let me start with Ephesians 1. Ephesians 1:9, Paul speaking, says, "Having made known unto us the mystery of his will, according to his good pleasure which he purposed in himself."

This word `purpose` is a very scriptural word. Often you hear people talk about God's purpose and they may not go to the text that addresses what God's purpose is. It is very common in the body of Christ. Many times I am listening to some teachers and I am wondering, why are they not going to the actual text? Why don't they just say it directly? It is almost like they are being blindsided somehow.

He is telling us in this text that there is something God purposed in himself. He told us that what God purposed in himself is the mystery of his will, which is what he wants, and he says it is according to his good pleasure. So there is a will that he has. There is a good pleasure that he has. And that is what he purposed in himself.

So if you ask, what is God's purpose? All you need to do is find out what his will is and what his good pleasure is. Good pleasure means what he likes. Will means what he wants.

I have used these examples before. They may not be perfect, but they are helpful. When you think about good pleasure, you are saying what someone dominantly likes. The person may not at the moment be craving or desiring it, but it is a dominant liking of the person. There are people that just like football. Some other people just like watching movies. At that moment they may not feel like watching a movie, but that is what they happen to like. It might be food. Some people just like a particular food. That thing they like can at different points become a craving, a want. You might have the craving at that moment, but you could ignore the craving. But if you accept the craving, that is like saying, I am going to go and get this thing.

So there is a liking, there is a wanting, and then there is a going ahead to pursue what you want to pursue. By the time it says God purposed in himself, it means there is this self-existent like that God has, this self-existent want that he has, and then there is the self-existent determination that, I am going to have it.

What is that thing that God likes, that he wants, that he decided to have? See Ephesians 1:5: "Having predestined us to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ to himself, according to the good pleasure of his will."

So it says God has predestined us to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ to himself. At this point, predestined just means he planned ahead for us to come to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ. He had something he planned ahead for us to come to, and it was based on the good pleasure of his will. That means what he planned ahead for us to come to is exactly what the good pleasure of his will is, because his planning ahead was because of the good pleasure of his will.

So Ephesians 1:5 shows us what God's will is, what God's good pleasure is, and what God's purpose is. And that is sonship. We have seen that severally.

## Predestination, Calling, Justification, Glorification

1. God 2. Good pleasure 3. Will 4. Counsel or determination 5. Purpose 6. Foreknowledge 7. Predestination 8. Election of Christ 9. Calling 10. Election of saints 11. Justification 12. Glorification

What is predestination about? Predestination is always about God's desired result.

For example, the initial desired result of God is sonship. The second result of God is also called sonship. Do you know that the glorification of our body is called sonship again? Because it is within sonship that our bodies must be glorified. But let me tell you this: the fulfillment of your purpose can also be called sonship.

But when you ask, what is God predestining to? God is never predestining to your own outcome. He is always predestining to his own outcome. Your alignment with that is a different thing. The meaning of predestination is that he has already predestined this world to his own eventual outcome.

He gave us the example of Abraham and Sarah. Sometimes you want to start drawing back. Forget about it. As long as you are still here, stay in faith. If someone can be barren so long and eventually give birth, a condition that can be there so long can eventually be reversed. Stay in faith.

Do you know how many years Abraham struggled with his faith walk for that situation to be resolved? At least twenty-five years, and I am sure he must have been trying to have children before then. In his faith life, he would be in faith, then doubt, then get back. Stay in faith.

## Jesus the Author and Finisher of Faith

Hebrews 12:2 says, "Looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith."

The nature of the Christian life is that there is a race set before us. The great cloud of witnesses are also people who had the race set before them.

Looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of faith, means the journey of faith. We saw that the Lord himself, God himself become man, fulfilled it. He had his purpose. He knew there would be sin. He wanted to redeem. He is the finisher of faith.

How many times does he finish faith? You can think about it in two ways. What is the first part of finishing faith? Salvation. What is the second part? Glorification. Which one was being talked about there? Glorification.

Remember the beginning of that discussion in Hebrews 10:38-39: we are not of those who draw back to perdition but of those who believe to the saving of the soul. And that saving of soul is the glorification of our body.

We have seen him do it in his earthly work. We have seen him do it about salvation. He has made just men perfect. He will do it also about glorification. We are taught to walk in faith. The way we walk in God's purpose is by faith.

So do not think of faith here as some small side issue. It is the mode of the journey itself. The way we walk in God's purpose is by faith. The way we continue when we do not fully understand is by faith. The way we continue after a mistake, with repentance, is by faith. The way we continue through delay is by faith. The way we continue through opposition is by faith. The way we continue until the end is by faith.

And because of that, whenever you find yourself in a place where you do not know everything, the right response is not to collapse. The right response is to remain in faith. Stay there. Keep walking. Keep running. Keep your heart right. Keep your eyes on Jesus.

## Joy Set Before Us

"Who for the joy that was set before him..."

So when we are walking in God's purpose, there is usually a joy. There is the joy of the salvation of people. There is the joy of their fulfillment of God's purpose. Paul talks about that. He wants to have joy concerning them at the end.

Do not let anybody confuse you, and please do not use your words wrongly. Sometimes people say it is not about the crowds you have, it is not about this, it is not about that. Sometimes people were with Jesus and they left him. Sometimes people were with Paul and they left him. But you do not see them anywhere desiring people to leave them.

If you start making that your confession, your heart disposition, it will start becoming your experience. Their expectation was that people would stay faithful and there would be joy at the end.

There is the joy of thinking about these people and saying, when I stand before the Lord, these folks are saved. These are people who will be able to I stood in your purpose, Lord. I knew about it. I stood in your purpose and fulfilled it because of this. You are to be joyful about that.

That is why Paul says in Philippians 2:15-16, "That you may become blameless and harmless, children of God without fault, in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation... holding fast the word of life, so that I may rejoice in the day of Christ that I have not run in vain or labored in vain."

Paul was never expecting anybody to go back, even though we all know those things happen. Let it never be your faith. You can use it as a shock absorber sometimes, but it must not be your faith. Expect people to be impacted and to stay faithful to the end.

## Guard Your Heart

Please guard your heart. Once your heart is negative, you will exude it, and you will be drawing the result of what you are exuding. You will have people around you put off because of the kind of thing you are carrying.

That is why I try not to keep talking about negative things people do. If negative things people do get into your heart, you will start drawing more negativity to yourself, and you will be repelling positive things from you.

I refuse to be associated with negativity. They will tempt you, but if you dwell there, you will start attracting people that have similar misery with you. Ah, people are bad, people are this. Be positive. I do not mean worldly positivity. I mean it in a Bible way. Let us be full of righteousness. Refuse negativity.

So there is a joy set before us. There is no gloating set before me. Some people are waiting to gloat. I believe God has worked on my heart and is working on my heart. I shall not gloat.

Sometimes in life there is always that temptation. You face all sorts of attack from all sides, from above, from below, from the side and all that. But cleanse your heart. Do not be out to gloat. The joy before you cannot be gloating.

Do you know if you gloat, you will start competing? You are not going to be doing the work for people again. It is not compassion anymore. It is competition. If your plan is to gloat, you can never be doing the work in compassion. It will become competition.

## The Road Map Begins With Sonship

Let me take you to the road map of purpose itself. Where do I turn? Where do I take the next step? What are the things I do in the journey of God's purpose? Sometimes when we do not give people the complete road map, they keep going straight and they miss where they were supposed to turn. We need to tell people where to turn next so they do not miss the road or get delayed in their journey.

Let us start with Romans 8:16. Sonship is God's purpose, and we have received the Spirit of sonship. There are two realities in the Spirit of sonship.

Romans 8:16-17 says, "The Spirit Himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God, and if children, then heirs, heirs of God and joint heirs with Christ."

So we are seeing two realities that make up sonship:

1. Child of God 2. Heir of God

Everything we want to talk about in God's purpose can be summed up in those two things: child of God and heir of God.

## Child of God: What Must Be Maintained

If we open up the child of God part, what do we see? We are supposed to walk in fellowship with the Father. That one is obvious because verse 15 said that by the Spirit of sonship we cry, "Abba, Father." The primary expression of this reality is our fellowship with God. What God desired is a family.

So fellowship with God is the first thing we see in being a child of God.

Number two, we are supposed to walk in our nature.

Number three, we are supposed to walk in our character. You can put character before nature if you want. Nature here includes things like power, divine health, divine preservation, protection, provision. For example, the glorification of our body is part of walking in our nature, that the Spirit of God in us will manifest what is already true in us.

So we can say three things make up being a child of God:

1. Fellowship with God 2. Walking in our character 3. Walking in our nature

Now notice this carefully. For the child part of the equation in God's purpose, there is not as much a road map as there is something to be maintained. When we say I am a child of God, speaking about sonship as his purpose, there is not as much a road map as something to be maintained.

Which means every step of this journey, every step of this journey, every step of this journey, I am meant to maintain fellowship, walk in my character, and walk in my nature. That one is not, there is no turn, no this, no that. It is simple, straightforward, and clear. Every step of the journey, you maintain it. Like a human being, every step of the journey you are breathing, you are still alive, you are functioning your humanity. In this case, we are functioning our divinity and communicating fellowship with our Father.

That one is to be maintained throughout the journey.

## Heir of God: Assignment

Now there is the second side, heirs of God. In the general Scripture context, being an heir of God can mean that in some broad sense all things are ours, all of creation. But within that reality are the assignments that God gives to us, and we have studied that before.

There is natural assignment. Colossians 3 into chapter 4 shows that. Ephesians 6 from verse 5 to verse 9 shows natural assignment. Then there is spiritual assignment.

So the core thing we need to look at in the road map of purpose is our assignment.

## Natural Assignment: Start Where You Are

Turn to 1 Corinthians 7. There must be that mentality in believers about God's orchestration or providence.

1 Corinthians 7:17 says, "But as God has distributed to each one, as the Lord has called each one, so let him walk."

Do you see the outline of God's plan here? As God has distributed to each one, as God has called each one, let him walk. That walk will be righteousness, justification, and so on.

What is this issue of distribution? It is election. He has distributed or apportioned to each one. Whatever God has called you to, walk in it.

At that point the actual issue in the text was marriage. He was telling them that yes, ordinarily we are to marry believers, but if you had already married an unbeliever before you got saved, God knew. He says, "So I ordain in all the churches." This is a principle of God's purpose and of our faith.

It also touches identity and natural estate. If you were circumcised, no big deal. If uncircumcised, no big deal. God knew. You are male, you are female, God knows.

It even touches unusual complications. We are meant to marry believers. We are meant to be monogamous. But if someone gets born again and the person is already not monogamous, I have seen this cause problems. As God has distributed to him, as God has called him, let him walk. I am serious. This is Bible truth. The only problem may be that such a person may not be able to lead in certain ways because of the example. So you may scale their work in spiritual leadership minutely, maybe within the family or neighborhood. You must not throw away the truth of God's word.

The principle is: God knows your steps. There was a time you did not know about God's purpose at all, and he knew that you would know about his purpose and be in this situation. You will now start to appreciate your natural assignment at this time. He knows where that will start to happen. He knows when you will start to appreciate that you have a spiritual assignment. And that is where he wants you to start walking from.

So the first truth in the road map of purpose as touching natural assignment is: start where you are.

It should change your attitude towards what you have been doing before. Instead of having an ambition that is selfish, you have an ambition like a steward: I must fulfill what God has given me this thing for.

Even if it is the richest man in the world or in Africa that gets born again, he should think about it this way: I have been diligent, yes, but this thing is not my own. It is for service. It is an allotment. It is an assignment. What am I supposed to use it for? The way I am treating people, the way I am using the proceeds, what more can I possibly do that may be of blessing?

Sometimes someone's vision and ambition may need to be expanded. In some cases it may need to be curtailed. We must teach right so that we do not mess people up. Sometimes someone can be ambitious in the natural, then the person hears the word and just becomes unserious. Is that really the Spirit of God? I am not saying the person should continue in greedy and selfish ambition, but we must be careful.

## Natural Assignment: Use Opportunities

1 Corinthians 7 goes on: "Were you called while a slave? Do not be concerned about it; but if you can be made free, rather use it."

So the first truth is start where you are. The second one is use opportunities.

These ones are not things already fixed by nature. They are opportunities. If you can be made free, use it. Do not be the kind of person that does not take advantage of opportunities.

He did not if you can be made free, God must speak a voice to you before you use it. Of course, always be open to the leading of the Spirit. But the ordinary disposition should be that this is an opportunity, use it, while still being open to the leading of the Spirit.

Not that you are always waiting for every i to be dotted and every t to be crossed. That is not the disposition of the believer's heart in these matters. Otherwise you sabotage yourself by self-doubt. You measure, you doubt, you question, you doubt again, and you do nothing. He said, use it.

Who knows? It might work. It might not work. Use it. Take a risk. Your calculated risks, go. Everything in this life is a risk anyway. Naturally, you do not know anything with absolute certainty. All we do is trust God. It is on the strength of revelation that we I believe God.

So if you can be made free, use it.

This means the believer's heart should have an eye for opportunities. If the believer's heart is, if I can be made free I will use it, then you will have an eye for opportunities. Your heart should be trained for opportunities. You should be able to interpret and understand by wisdom opportunities. That means clarity, wisdom, thinking through.

## Natural Assignment: Pursue Your Desires

The third step is pursue your own desires. Unlike spiritual assignment, natural assignment allows for the liberty of your desires in what you pursue.

Look at 1 Corinthians 7:29-31, especially the end of it: "Those who use this world as not misusing it."

Do you notice? They are the ones using it. It is talking to believers. You should be like this, you that are using this world. It is not that you were specially told each time to use it like that. There is a liberty there. So the third step is pursuing your own desires.

If you notice, these unfold one by one. Many times you start with constraint. Not everybody does, but many do. Someone born into a family with a trust fund may start with more liberty already. Another person may begin from slavery or lack. But generally it unfolds like this:

1. Start where you are. 2. Take advantage of opportunities that come to you. 3. Use this world. Pursue your desires within righteousness.

You I do not know what to do. Are you sure you do not know what to do of all the things in your hand? Or are you blind to opportunities? That would be unfortunate, because part of God's word is that you are supposed to use opportunities.

Imagine looking at people who saw an opportunity to be free. They go and suggest to a boss that they can do this, and they get free. You are just there saying, this life is not fair. That is not the believer's heart.

You should have an eye for opportunities. Look at this thing, look at that thing, think through it, see how you can do this and manage it and maybe do something else later. Who knows?

If you train your heart wrongly, you will not even notice opportunities when they are in front of you. But if your heart is trained by Scripture, you will begin to interpret opportunity by wisdom. You will begin to think, this can lead to this, and this can lead to that. This does not mean greed. It means stewardship. It means you are attentive to what God has placed in front of you.

For the form of this world is passing away. That means do not be too attached to the world.

1 Timothy 6:17 says, "Command those who are rich in this present age not to be haughty, nor to trust in uncertain riches but in the living God, who gives us richly all things to enjoy."

So he said we have all things to enjoy. Let them do good, that they may be rich in good works, ready to give, willing to share. You are at liberty to use the world and even enjoy the world. God is not against you enjoying the world. He is against greed, not against enjoying or taking advantage of pleasures in this world.

So the road map of God's purpose in natural assignment looks like this:

1. Start where you are. 2. Take advantage of opportunities that come to you. 3. Pursue your desires. Use the world rightly. 4. Be open to the leading of the Spirit. 5. Engage with maximum productivity.

Notice how these can unfold in sequence. Many times you begin with a constrained state. Then opportunities arise. Then your use of the world expands. Then the leading of the Spirit can become more specific in how you steward those things. Then productivity multiplies what is in your hand. It is usually not one dramatic leap. It often unfolds.

## Natural Assignment: Be Open to the Spirit

Number four: be open to the leading of the Spirit. There is nothing in a believer's life where he is not supposed to be open to the leading of the Spirit.

This is a principle of believers generally, but we can also see it from narrative in Scripture. God led people on natural things. He told Abraham, get out of this place and go to this place. He told Isaac, do not go anywhere, sow in this land. He told Jacob to do this and do that. So it can happen.

Sometimes it can be to caution you. Sometimes it can be to steer you. God can show you a vision. These things have happened and they are still happening.

If you have closed it in your mind via doctrine, it is not that God will not be sending things to you. It is that you will not be receiving, and that would be unfortunate.

Why can't God speak to you about natural assignment? For goodness' sake, who called you to do natural assignment? Did he not put you in a world where you have natural assignment even for survival? How can you then say he cannot speak to you on it?

It is not that he always does in the same dramatic way. He has already spoken by his word, by the direction of his word. But you must now be open to the leading of the Spirit, whether an inward witness, still small voice, sight and sound, some visual impression. Be open.

Sometimes it may simply be a caution. Sometimes it may be a stirring. Sometimes it may be a directional nudge. Sometimes it may be something much clearer. The point is that you do not close that door in your understanding. You stay wholesome. You stay open.

## Natural Assignment: Maximum Productivity

The last point underneath all this is maximum productivity.

No matter what you are doing in your natural assignment, always engage with maximum productivity. Progress in your natural assignment is in your productivity. Really, the next thing you are supposed to do will usually be there.

People looking for opportunities, sometimes those opportunities are hidden in what they are already doing. If you do not put in everything, if you are not maximally productive in what is in your hand right now, how do you want the next things to unfold?

Even if you have a seed and you plant the seed, if you do not cater for that one, how will it bring many more seeds you can now plant, giving you several trees? And you do not even know what will be in the next set of seeds. There may be so much more ahead of you. The one in your hand, give it maximum productivity.

That is the road map of God's purpose in natural assignment.

And really, a lot of people are looking for the next thing while neglecting the present thing. But usually the next thing is hidden in the present thing. The next opportunity, the next level of trust, the next increase of capacity, all of that usually grows out of what is already in your hand. So give what is in your hand maximum productivity.

## Spiritual Assignment: Commit to a Local Church

Now let us move to spiritual assignment. Our spiritual assignment is the work of ministry, to preach and to teach God's word, which should eventually end up in our leadership to different degrees.

The first step in spiritual assignment is commitment to a local church. That is it.

What leads to you doing the work of ministry is that you have been discipled by those doing it. So the very first step in the road map for your spiritual assignment is commitment to local church.

## Spiritual Assignment: Seek Every Avenue

Number two, after commitment to local church, is seeking every avenue of communicating God's word.

Another way of saying that is: go. In our world today, this `go` means go offline and go online. We must let believers know that their ministry in this age, except we want to lie to them, involves them talking the gospel online.

Does it mean they should only talk the gospel online? No. We might limit what they talk about. There are things I see people talk about and I think, maybe wait for your understanding to be better before you talk about them. But why can't you talk about basic things? I was in church today. I learned this and that. The redemptive truth of God's word is so powerful. This and that.

And even beyond broad public posting, there is also the simple flow of the Spirit between people. Sometimes not every ministry expression is, let us all gather and minister to one another formally. Sometimes you just have a word for a person. Sometimes God just tells you to text somebody. Sometimes he tells you to tell someone something specifically, and that is it. If you shut your mind to only one expression, you will miss many other expressions of ministry that are very real.

You may leave aside some more contingent things for later. But there are things you can talk about. You can share on Twitter one day. Share on TikTok. Just do a video.

I have seen fantastic people like that online. They are not pastors and they are sharing the gospel. Some people will listen to you. I have seen people just share something and someone says, this thing you said is timely, or you just blessed me. They are not pastors. They are just members of a local church. When I see them, I almost get jealous in a good way. Those are the kind of people I want.

You flow in the Spirit in church, yes, but not only in church. Sometimes anywhere. It does not have to be only structured moments. God can just tell you to tell someone something specifically and that is it. It will not be every time, but if you shut your mind to only one expression of ministry, you close out many other things God might be trying to do.

Has it happened to you that you woke up in the morning and God said, text this person and say such and such? It has happened to me a number of times, and then the person says, why did you do this, this was exactly what was going on.

If you do not know that is part of it, you will miss many opportunities. Sometimes you just I am about to tell you this, I do not even know what it is about, let me just tell you this. And God will say so many things he wants to say to people, some like confirmation, some like instruction and direction.

Go. Seek every avenue.

So do not reduce ministry to one narrow thing. Yes, serve in church. Yes, be discipled in church. Yes, communicate the word in ordered ways. But also recognize that the Spirit can flow in those smaller moments. If you close your mind to that, it is not that God has stopped speaking. You are the one not receiving.

## Spiritual Assignment: Publicize, Evangelize, Disciple

So first: commitment.

Second: seek every avenue of communicating God's word.

Third: publicize the message through all those avenues. Determine how you want to publicize the word. I will go offline like this at this time to that time. Online I will do this. I will like and share things I know will bless other people, help those things spread further through the algorithms that have become gatekeepers today. Then I will personally write my own. Determine what you want to do.

Fourth: evangelize. Evangelism is that you are preaching the message unto salvation to any and everybody.

Then disciple those who are recipients. If you see an avenue you can afford to put the word on, and it is righteous and not offensive, put it out there. The gospel should never be offensive.

I do not like when people present the gospel in an offensive manner. Sometimes they think they are helping the gospel. They are not.

There was something someone did. It was quite a bold move, and people were commending his boldness. I was so happy when I started watching the video. I thought, wow, this is what I should be doing. But as I went on, the manner in which he went about it was offensive. It was counterproductive.

You can be bold without being offensive. You do not need to be offensive. You are affecting the Lord Jesus by being offensive. You will just be creating hardness of heart for people. We will now need double prayer and double explanation first to let their guard down and then explain to them, because you do not need to speak to people as if you are attacking, insulting, denigrating them or what they believe. Rise up to the occasion. Do it at a better level.

You can confront error without a fleshly spirit. You can disagree without contempt. You can preach Christ without sounding like you enjoy humiliating the hearer. We must do better than that.

That is why you must always leave avenues for people to be able to get back to you. Someone can come to your DM or send you a message. Maybe you put your number there. Ah, I was really blessed by this and that. What do I do next? You can just put there, need further help, call this number. I am here to help you. Need further help in God's purpose, call this number.

That is now discipleship. If it is direct, your discipleship is that you stay with the person until the person commits or pushes you away. And even if someone needs some distance, you do not have to be harsh. You can you know what, if you need peace for a while, that is but maybe be listening to this podcast or this teaching, and after a while maybe you will see me again. That kind of heart shows you have faith in God.

Do not act as if once a person withdraws a bit, that means everything is over. Sometimes wisdom is to give a little space while keeping a gentle avenue open. That itself can be part of discipleship.

## Spiritual Assignment: Serve Unto Leadership

The fifth step is serve in the local church unto leadership.

Very key. Our spiritual assignment must lead to leadership. We are still going to see that later.

So let me stop there, because our time is far spent on this teaching already.

Natural assignment. Spiritual assignment. Let it unfold.
