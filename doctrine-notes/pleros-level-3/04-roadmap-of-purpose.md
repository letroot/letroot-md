# Roadmap of Purpose

We are looking at the road map of purpose. We have already established what God's purpose is, so here we are not mainly trying to talk about what God's purpose is per se. We will recap that, but our concern is the road map of purpose. This teaching will be important for other teachings in level three where we will be focusing more entirely on our assignments.

There is knowing what you are supposed to do, there is knowing the journey of doing it, and there is being capable of doing it. If someone tells you that your purpose is to go on a particular path carrying two heavy bags to a destination, first you need to know that you have such a purpose. Second, you need to know the exact path you are meant to take. Third, you need to be able to bear the burden and carry those bags toward the destination. What we are dealing with today is that second part: what the road map of God's purpose is and how it leads to the destination we are meant to arrive at.

## God's Purpose Is Sonship

Ephesians 1:9 says, "Having made known unto us the mystery of his will, according to his good pleasure which he purposed in himself."

This word "purpose" is a very scriptural word. The text tells us that there is something God purposed in himself. It also tells us that this is the mystery of his will, according to his good pleasure. So if you ask what God's purpose is, you need to find out what his will is and what his good pleasure is. Good pleasure is what he likes. Will is what he wants.

Ephesians 1:5 says, "Having predestined us to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ to himself, according to the good pleasure of his will." What he planned ahead for is exactly what the good pleasure of his will is. So Ephesians 1:5 shows us what God's will is, what God's good pleasure is, and what God's purpose is. That is sonship.

## The Outline of God's Purpose and Plan

Ephesians 1:11 says, "In him we have obtained an inheritance, being predestined according to the purpose of him who works all things after the counsel of his will."

When we talk about God's purpose and plan, we are not only talking about his purpose in the simple sense. His purpose is sonship. But the plan is the entire scheme from the reality of his purpose to how he is going to accomplish it.

The outline of God's purpose and plan is this:

1. God
2. Good pleasure
3. Will
4. Counsel or determination
5. Purpose
6. Foreknowledge
7. Predestination
8. Election of Christ
9. Calling
10. Election of saints
11. Justification
12. Glorification

Counsel is determination. God has the will, then he determines to have it, and that creates purpose. After that comes foreknowledge. Once you understand the dynamics of God's purpose and plan, there is a way you live differently.

## All Things Working Together for Good

Romans 8:28 says, "We know that all things work together for good to those who love God, to those who are the called according to purpose."

Contextually, the good here is specific, but the statement is also a principle. All things work together for the fulfillment of God's purpose. In the most powerful sense, this is true about the eventual outcome of glorification. But the principle also applies to the fulfillment of God's purpose for your life.

The church you are in, the family you were born into, the fact that you were born into a family of believers or unbelievers, all of that belongs within these dynamics of foreknowledge, predestination, and election. There is a God's part in the equation, and it is important that you see it.

If you align in faith, there may be rough patches here and there, but you will see evidence of God's working step by step in your life. The timeliness of certain things can become very real. So when a situation happens that you were not expecting, you should not start doubting at that point. Does God know? Yes. Then he will find a way to ensure that what must be done is done.

As long as it has happened, and God foreknew it, and I am in faith, there must be provision within his working. That is part of seeing God's side of the equation in the walk of his purpose.

## Faith, Repentance, and the Right Orchestration

The descriptions in Romans 8:28 are describing people that believe. While the general principle applies for salvific faith, the second kind of faith makes for the application of that principle in our walk in God's purpose.

If a man does not walk by faith after he has been saved, all things will still work together for good in terms of his glorified body, new heavens, and new earth. But if that same principle is to apply in his walk in God's purpose, he must be aligned with God in faith. That means sincerity. That means repentance. That means a right heart.

Walking by faith should be sincere. If you make a mistake, you repent. You may even need to apologize to people you made a mistake toward. If a man is walking that way, God will use even the mistake and the repentance within his orchestration. But if you are walking in unrepentance, you are aiding another kind of orchestration. You are aiding the orchestration of the enemy.

Do not live in unrepentance, and do not walk in the flesh. It is the fastest way to have a wrong orchestration. If you walk in the flesh, you can create another event entirely because you are partnering with the other side of the spiritual world.

## Foreknowledge, Predestination, and Human Response

Romans 8:29 says, "For whom he foreknew, he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son." We can say he predestined to sonship. Ephesians 1:5 already said that he predestined us to adoption as sons.

Predestination is always about God's desired result. The initial desired result of God is sonship. The glorification of our body is also called sonship. Romans 8:23 says that we are waiting for the adoption, the redemption of our body. So your fulfillment in God's purpose for your life is also still within sonship.

When you ask what God is predestining to, God is not predestining to your own outcome. He is always predestining to his own outcome. Your alignment with that is a different thing. The meaning of predestination is that he has already predestined this world to his own eventual outcome.

What is good for you, then, is to find out his eventual outcome and align with it in your own part. Predestination has already happened. Salvation is guaranteed. Glorification is guaranteed. But the in-between, your walk in God's purpose for your life, requires partnership. That one involves individuals with free will, and God is not bent on bending their will.

What he wants will eventually happen. Delay can happen. Failure of what God wants will not happen. If one person does not do what should be done, someone else will still do it, believer or unbeliever. But your own fulfillment in that purpose is not automatic. That requires your response.

## Calling, Election, and Walking in Righteousness

After predestination comes election. Then calling. Then justification and glorification.

For your personal walk in God's purpose, once you are saved, God foreknew what would happen when you would be saved. He has what he wants you to do, the result he wants you to get in your walk in his purpose. He will elect where you should be, what should happen, even things like the local church you should be in. Then he will call you to it.

That calling may come by the word and by the Spirit. You are led by the word first, and sometimes by the Spirit in ways that do not contradict the word. If I accept that calling, then I am functioning in that role. If I do not accept it, I am not functioning in that role.

But even after accepting it, there is still righteousness. There is still walking right while at it. That is why sincerity, repentance, and faith are very important. That is what brings about glorification, the fulfillment of your assignment.

The simple path to fulfilling God's purpose is sincerity, repentance, and faith. Do not let people mess up your heart. No matter how offended you feel, and even if you feel justified in your offense, do not let people destroy the state of your heart, your sincerity, your repentance, and your faith.

Preserve your heart so that you can actually fulfill God's purpose for your life.

## The Believer's Walk Is a Journey of Purpose

Acts 11:23 says that they should "with purpose of heart continue with the Lord."

Our walk with God is meant to be done with purpose of heart. It is not meant to be a maintenance of status quo. Purpose means something is set before you. This is not just continuing in place. This is another day in progress in purpose. This is another day in pursuit.

So when we say road map of purpose, your conception of purpose must not be merely, "I know what God's purpose is." The knowledge of God's purpose is not the heart of purpose. The knowledge is good, but there is also the heart of purpose.

Acts 20:24 says, "That I may finish my race with joy, and the ministry which I have received from the Lord Jesus, to testify of the gospel of the grace of God." He uses the word race to describe it.

John 4:34 says, "My food is to do the will of him that sent me, and to finish his work."

John 17:4 says, "I have glorified you on the earth. I have finished the work which you gave me to do."

2 Timothy 4:6-7 says, "I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith."

You cannot remove this thinking from Scripture. There is a race. There is work to finish. There is righteousness to walk in so that there can be glorification.

## Running the Race by Faith

Hebrews 12:1 says, "Let us run with endurance the race that is set before us."

We are surrounded by a great cloud of witnesses, people who walked in God's purpose and ran the race by faith. Faith always involves sincerity, though it does not mean perfection. People made mistakes, but they stayed in faith.

Lay aside every weight. Do not step out of faith. Do not get into doubt, unbelief, compromise, or drawing back. Sometimes there are situations where the honest answer is that we do not know. We do not need to force explanations and create more problems than we solve. There are things where the right answer is simply, "We do not know, but we stay in faith."

As long as you are still here, stay in faith. A condition that has been there for so long can still be reversed. Abraham's situation took many years, yet he still continued in the faith journey. The way we walk in God's purpose is by faith.

## Joy, Faithfulness, and Guarding the Heart

There is usually a joy set before us in God's purpose. There is the joy of the salvation of people and the joy of their fulfillment in God's purpose. Paul said in Philippians 2:16 that he wanted to rejoice in the day of Christ and not have run or labored in vain.

So do not let your disposition become negative. If your heart is negative, you will start drawing more negativity. Refuse negativity. Be full of righteousness. Cleanse your heart.

The joy set before you cannot be gloating. If your plan is to gloat, you will start competing. Then it is no longer compassion; it becomes competition. You cannot be doing the work for people in compassion if your heart is fixed on gloating.

## The Road Map Begins With Sonship

Romans 8:16 says, "The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God." Then it says, "If children, then heirs, heirs of God and joint heirs with Christ."

So there are two realities that make up sonship:

1. Child of God
2. Heir of God

Everything we want to talk about in God's purpose can be summed up in those two things.

## The Child of God Side Must Be Maintained

If we open up the child of God side, we see that we are supposed to walk in fellowship with the Father. Verse 15 already says that by the Spirit of sonship we cry, "Abba, Father." The primary expression of this reality is fellowship with God. What God desired is a family.

Then we are supposed to walk in our character. Then we are supposed to walk in our nature. In that sense, we are talking about things like power, divine health, divine preservation, protection, provision, and even ultimately the glorification of our body.

So being a child of God includes:

1. Fellowship with God
2. Walking in our character
3. Walking in our nature

This side of the road map is not so much a series of turns as something to be maintained. Every step of the journey, I am meant to maintain fellowship, walk in character, and walk in nature. There is no turn there. It is straightforward and constant.

## The Heir of God Side Includes Assignments

The second side is heirs of God. Within that reality are the assignments that God gives to us. There is natural assignment, and there is spiritual assignment.

That is where the road map becomes more specific. For the child part of the equation, the concern is maintenance. But when we now talk about our assignments, especially our natural assignments, we have to understand where to turn and what to do next.

## Walk as God Has Distributed and Called

1 Corinthians 7:17 says, "But as God has distributed to each one, as the Lord has called each one, so let him walk."

Here again we see the language of God's plan. He has distributed. He has called. Then we are told to walk.

The point there in context was marriage. Paul was saying that if someone was already in a situation before salvation, God knew. So there is a principle here of God's purpose and of our faith: as God has distributed, as God has called, let him walk.

That means God knows your steps. There was a time you did not know about God's purpose at all, and he knew that you would one day know about his purpose while in your present situation. He knows when you will begin to appreciate your natural assignment. He knows when you will begin to appreciate your spiritual assignment. That is where he wants you to start walking from.

It should change your attitude toward what you have been doing before. Instead of having an ambition that is selfish, you have an ambition like a steward: I must fulfill what God has given me.
