# The New Creation: Who You Are in Christ

**00:00 \- Introduction to the New Creation**

We are looking at the new creation, who you are in Christ. Turn your Bibles to Ephesians chapter 1\. Just before we see the scripture, I want to say that for the believer, once you have believed the gospel, the reality of God, and the reality of redemption in Christ Jesus, you are saved from sin and death by His death, burial, and resurrection. You have now entered into God's purpose. Particularly when you know God's purpose—that He has called you to sonship as His child and His heir, and He wants you to fulfill His purpose—there is the tendency to start thinking, "So what do I do next?"

At the point where you have believed the gospel and are aware of the reality of God's purpose upon your life, you are wondering what the next step is. Am I supposed to pray next? Am I supposed to do this next? The very first thing God wants us to understand when we have believed the gospel and know the reality of His purpose is who we are. Right after we have believed the gospel and know His purpose, the first thing He wants us to do is not an action. He wants us to know who we are.

I want to show you that from Ephesians chapter 1, verse 13\. Look at what it says about what happens when we believe the gospel. It says, "In Him," talking about Christ Jesus, "you also trusted, after you heard the word of truth." You have heard this message, seen that the truth is the gospel, and seen it as a matter of truth. "After you heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation, in whom also, having believed, you were sealed with the Holy Spirit of promise." There is a reality that you received, a reality that was done to you. You were sealed at the instance of faith in Christ. Verse 14 says that Spirit is "the guarantee of our inheritance until the redemption of the purchased possession, to the praise of His glory."

Something happened to us once we believed the gospel. We were given the Spirit. When we talk about God's purpose for life being sonship, do you notice that where sonship was spoken of in Romans chapter 8, verse 15, we were told that something happened to us to bring us into His purpose? Something happened to you when you believed the truth, redemption, and salvation. That is the first place to start from. What happened to you? You were sealed with the Spirit.

Romans 8:15 says, "For you did not receive the spirit of bondage again to fear, but you received the Spirit of adoption by whom we cry out, Abba, Father." It is not a case of just being brought inside something while remaining the same old person. Something happened. You did not receive the spirit of bondage again, showing you that there was a kind of spirit you had that was constrained under a situation called bondage—a bondage of sin, death, darkness, and evil. But you received the Spirit of adoption, or sonship, whereby you cry, Abba, Father. Something happened to you at the instance of salvation.

**05:00 \- The Direct Contrast of the New Creation**

This is the biggest issue people have. They might hear the gospel, their hearts might truly be broken, their eyes might be enlightened, and their minds might be convinced. They might receive it indeed, but they do not know what happened to them—that something substantial happened. Consistently, the scriptures emphasize what happened whenever they talk about what we believed or entered into. Whether they say you believed the gospel, they say you were sealed with the Spirit. When they say you entered into God's purpose, they say you received the Spirit of sonship.

I want you to notice something about receiving this Spirit. Let us look at Romans chapter 8, verse 9\. We received the Spirit at the instance of salvation. Romans 8:9 says, "But you are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if indeed the Spirit of God dwells in you. Now if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, he is not His." Do you see a parallel between Romans 8:9 and Romans 8:15? Romans 8:15 says you did not receive the spirit of bondage again. Romans 8:9 says you are not in the flesh. What happened to you at the instance of salvation is a direct contrast, not a continuation of what you had been. It is a direct contrast with what was true about you before the reality of receiving the Spirit happened.

One of the best explanations to fully capture what happened to you at the instance of believing the gospel and entering God's purpose is 2nd Corinthians 5:17. It captures the idea that something happened contrasting you from what you were before and bringing you into something else. In Ephesians 1, we saw that when you heard the word of truth and received the gospel, you received the Spirit. You are no longer in the flesh; you are now in the Spirit.

Look at how 2nd Corinthians 5:17 expresses this clearly. It says, "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ..." This is the reality we are in when we believe the gospel. You are baptized into Christ by the Spirit of God. 1st Corinthians 12:13 says, "For by one Spirit we were all baptized into one body—whether Jews or Greeks, whether slaves or free—and have all been made to drink into one Spirit." When you were put in Christ and received the Spirit, something happened. 2nd Corinthians 5:17 continues, "...he is a new creation."

What Ephesians 1:13, Romans 8:15, and Romans 8:9 have been describing is best encapsulated in this phrase: new creation. "Old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new." That connects to the idea of not receiving the spirit of bondage again. Notice that it does not say old things *shall* pass away or *can* pass away; old things *have* passed away. All things *have* become new. Not that they are becoming new, but they have become new. Everything we have been told that happened to us at salvation is captured in this description: we became new creations.

The word "new" means there was a former reality that is gone. There is no talking about salvation or redemption without that reality being mentioned. There is no discussion of God's purpose without talking about it. What you received to be in His purpose is the Spirit of sonship, not the spirit of bondage again to fear. We can never speak of a man being saved or being in God's purpose without simultaneously mentioning the reality of the new creation. What makes a man saved is that at that very instant he became a new creation. We cannot separate Christianity from the new creation. There is no talking about being saved, forgiven, or delivered from damnation without talking about the new creation.

**12:00 \- The Dual Reality of Man**

Who you are in Christ—the new creation—is too core to salvation to be separated from it. When you enter into God's purpose, knowing that you are a child of God and an heir of God means understanding the fact that you are a new creation. However, this is usually where the problem lies. When we say we are a new creation, that we received the Spirit of God and did not receive the spirit of bondage, we must be careful. Notice that what we are told we received at the new creation is the Spirit.

What confuses many people is that at the point of salvation, they still have the same physical body they had before they were saved. They wonder, "How can you say I am a new creation? New in what way?" They also realize that the same songs and habits they knew before they got saved are still in their heads. They might think, "I believe the gospel, but these things are still there. How am I a new creation?"

The reality of the new creation requires that you understand the dual reality of man—that man is both spiritual and natural. It is a prerequisite. You cannot understand the new creation without grasping the dual reality of man. Preceding 2nd Corinthians 5:17 is 2nd Corinthians 5:16. The reason Paul told them they were new creations is that he had already explained to them, and they were well aware of, this dual reality.

2nd Corinthians 5:16 says, "Therefore, from now on, we regard no one according to the flesh." The flesh here is different from the flesh in Romans 8:9. Here, it means we do not regard people according to the natural. He continues, "Even though we have known Christ according to the flesh, yet now we know Him thus no longer." He meant they once knew Christ in the natural, but not anymore. Then he says in verse 17, "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ..." The knowledge we have of Christ, and of who we are in Christ, is not according to the natural.

Let me show you where he started explaining this dual reality. Look at 2nd Corinthians chapter 4, verse 16\. It says, "Therefore we do not lose heart. Even though our outward man is perishing..." When 2nd Corinthians 5:16 says we do not know Christ after the flesh, it means we do not know Him after the outward man. What did they know about Him outwardly? He was a male, a Jew, perhaps tall or short. But those are not the things we discuss when talking about who you are in Christ.

Let us read 2nd Corinthians 4:16 again: "Therefore we do not lose heart. Even though our outward man is perishing, yet the inward man is being renewed day by day." The inward man here is not the mind. There are places where "inner man" or "hidden man of the heart" refers to the mind, such as in 1st Peter 3:4. But the "inward man being renewed day by day" means that while the outward man is aging, the inward man never ages. It is talking about your spirit, your spiritual reality.

**18:00 \- The Outward Man vs. The Inward Man**

At this point, Paul was discussing the fact that our outer man—the natural body—will be glorified at the end. Both are parts of your humanity. But notice that he wants you to distinguish between the outward man and the inward man. To help you distinguish them, he later describes the outward man as a tent for the inward man. This shows that the real issue concerning human behavior is the inward man. When you observe how someone acts, their physical body is not the root issue. It is the inward man that is responsible for, and facilitates, what the person does. That same physical body could behave like Jesus, or it could behave terribly. The body is merely the tent; it is not the problem by any stretch.

See how 2nd Corinthians 5:1 helps us think about the dual reality of man. It says, "For we know that if our earthly house, this tent, is destroyed, we have a building from God, a house not made with hands, eternal in the heavens." That is the promise of the glorified body. Verse 2 continues, "For in this we groan, earnestly desiring to be clothed with our habitation which is from heaven, if indeed, having been clothed, we shall not be found naked." Humanity is not meant to exist as just an inward man alone. Our humanity is only wholesome and complete when it consists of both the inward and outward man.

Look at verse 4: "For we who are in this tent groan, being burdened, not because we want to be unclothed, but further clothed, that mortality may be swallowed up by life." Read verse 5: "Now He who has prepared us for this very thing is God, who also has given us the Spirit as a guarantee." Where is that Spirit? It is fused with your inward man. That is why Romans 8:16 says, "The Spirit Himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God." They have the same testimony because they are now fused. 1st Corinthians 6:17 says, "But he who is joined to the Lord is one spirit with Him."

We can say the Spirit of God is in you, but in reality, your spirit and God's Spirit are now fused as one reality. God has prepared us for this and given us the Spirit as the guarantee of the glorification of our body. That Spirit is in our inward man. So the moment you are born again and the scriptures declare you are a new creation, it does not mean your physical body has changed. Furthermore, it does not mean your mind has instantly changed. Your mind is a part of your spirit, much like your brain is a part of your body. Therefore, your mind will still hold the same information it used to have, even though the fundamental nature of your spirit has changed.

Let us use an example. Imagine if what changed was your outward body, but your brain remained the same. Suppose your body transformed into an eagle-like human, granting you the physical ability to fly. If you never thought you could fly, had no mirror to see your new form, and were unaware of the physical change, you would continue with your old mindset and never fly. This is how many believers walk. Their spirit has completely changed, but they have zero idea that it has changed.

**26:00 \- Addressing the Old Man**

In fact, the new creation is sometimes taught as an instruction—people are told to try to walk as a new creation, to stop doing this and stop doing that. So anyone who tries and fails starts to think, "Maybe I am not a new creation." Other believers might try to condemn them. The person then admits they cannot say they are a new creation because of the way they are living. But that is exactly the point. In the analogy of the man whose body changed to an eagle-like form, the fact that he is not flying does not mean he is not an eagle. The challenge is that he does not know who he is, and he does not know how to fly. The problem is that he is operating with the same old mind despite having a new capacity.

The scripture tells the believer: "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new." Your inward man has been entirely changed. The next statement in 2nd Corinthians 5:18 is, "Now all things are of God." This means all things that changed about your inward man changed into being of God. There is not an iota of the flesh left in your inward man. There is not an iota of sin, darkness, or death left in your inward man. All things are of God.

The new creation admits the reality of the past as something that has already changed. Wherever the new creation is spoken of, there is an acknowledgment of a past reality that has been altered. Why do the scriptures admit this? So that it can effect a change in our walk. Everyone recognizes their old man in different ways—from the standpoint of pride, sinful lust, greed, or ungodly habits. But we are now told that this has changed.

Let us look at Romans chapter 6, verse 1: "What shall we say then? Shall we continue in sin that grace may abound? Certainly not\! How shall we who died to sin live any longer in it?" Notice that the inward man died to sin; it has no relationship with sin anymore. What the grace of God did was not just forgive sins, but render the inward man dead to sin. In receiving the Spirit, what Christ went through was effected upon your inward man.

Verse 5 says, "For if we have been united together in the likeness of His death, certainly we also shall be in the likeness of His resurrection, knowing this, that our old man was crucified with Him, that the body of sin might be done away with." The old man—the former reality of the inward man—was crucified with Christ. Therefore, the body of sin has been done away with. Verse 7 says, "For he who has died has been freed from sin." This is a reality. If you have believed in the death, burial, and resurrection of Jesus, you have been freed from sin. It matters not what your walk looks like at the moment; the reality of the inward man is that it has been completely freed from sin.

**32:00 \- The Permanence of the New Nature**

Galatians 5:24 says, "And those who are Christ's have crucified the flesh with its passions and desires." They did not ignore the flesh; they addressed it. This means part of what the flesh had was not just the ability to sin, but the desire to sin. For the man who is in Christ, both the ability and the desire for sin are gone. Your inward man no longer possesses the old man nor has any association with it.

The new creation admits the reality of the past as something that has been addressed, but it emphasizes the permanence of the new. "Behold, all things have become new." Romans 8:9 says, "But you are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if indeed the Spirit of God dwells in you. Now if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, he is not His." Christ is the defining essence of the new creation. The new creation is not defined by human abilities; it is divinity at work in humanity. Your nature is His nature.

Look at verse 10: "And if Christ is in you, the body is dead because of sin, but the Spirit is life because of righteousness." This means your inward man has life solely because of the righteousness of Christ. The old man is completely crucified, and Christ is the only nature of the inward man. Christ is not sharing space with a sin nature in your spirit.

Colossians chapter 3, verses 9 to 11 explains this again: "Do not lie to one another, since you have put off the old man with his deeds, and have put on the new man who is renewed in knowledge according to the image of Him who created him." This new man has the image of God. Verse 11 says, "where there is neither Greek nor Jew... but Christ is all and in all." In the new creation, there are no biological or cultural distinctions. Christ is all the new creation has.

Colossians 2:9 states, "For in Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily; and you are complete in Him, who is the head of all principality and power." The word "Godhead" refers to divinity. In Him dwells the fullness of divinity bodily, and you are complete in Him. What is your state on the inside? Fullness of divinity. You are one spirit with Him. Peter confirms this in 2nd Peter 1:4, stating that a man in Christ is a "partaker of the divine nature." If you are looking for sin in the new man, you cannot find it in his inward man. The only place you might find such a thing is in an unrenewed mind.

**41:00 \- Three Implications of the New Creation**

We are now of God, of Christ, of the Spirit of God, of life, and of light. What does the new creation mean for us today? Having this new reality of the Spirit implies three things: we have a new status, we have a new character, and we have new abilities.

The moment you are in Christ, your status changes. Let us look at John chapter 5, verse 24\. "Most assuredly, I say to you, he who hears My word and believes in Him who sent Me has everlasting life, and shall not come into judgment, but has passed from death into life." Once you have passed from death to life, you are not going into condemnation. Romans 8:1 says, "There is therefore now no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus."

Secondly, under this new status, you have bold access to God. Ephesians 2:18 says, "For through Him we both have access by one Spirit to the Father." Your status relative to God has changed. Thirdly, your status has changed relative to all other spiritual entities, including the devil and his cohorts. You have authority over the works of darkness. You are not fighting with them; you already have victory over them. You enforce that victory. Look at Ephesians 1:19-21: God "seated Him at His right hand in the heavenly places, far above all principality and power and might and dominion." This means forever we are far above.

The second implication of the new creation is a new character. Everything of unrighteousness and hate is gone. Our character is now righteousness and love. Galatians 5:22 says, "But the fruit of the Spirit is love..." The Spirit we have received has the nature of love, therefore it bears the fruit of love. We no longer have a nature that produces hate, selfishness, unrighteousness, or laziness. The nature we have produces discipline and self-control. Any fruit you see comes from the underlying nature.

The third implication is new abilities. There are now things we can do by the Spirit of God. The Lord Jesus said in John 14:12, "He who believes in Me, the works that I do he will do also; and greater works than these he will do, because I go to My Father." He was talking about miraculous works. The abilities we now have are abilities just like Jesus. Paul explains these abilities in 1st Corinthians chapter 12, from verse 7 to 11, as the gifts of the Spirit. The believer can confidently reckon himself as having the gifts of the Spirit.

**48:00 \- Walking in the New Creation**

To wrap up: when you know God's purpose after salvation, the next thing is to find out who you are in Christ. You find out that you have received the Spirit, you are a new creation, you are dead to sin, and you are alive to God. You have a new status, a new character, and new abilities. But what do you do with this?

Having this new nature does not automatically mean you are walking in it. The reason God gave us this nature is for us to walk in it so we can fulfill His purpose. The question then is, how do we walk in this reality? Galatians 5:25 says, "If we live in the Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit." The call, when you are made new and alive in the Spirit, is to walk in it. Being a new creation is not enough; it is a call to walk.

God's word teaches us precisely how to walk in the new creation, which we will explore further in another teaching on faith's stand. It is critical that you know exactly how to walk in it so that you can fulfill God's purpose for your life. As a believer in Christ Jesus, born into God's purpose as His child and heir, you have received the Spirit of sonship. You must commit yourself to finding out how to walk in the Spirit and to growing in the knowledge of who you are, so that you may fulfill God's purpose. Commit yourself to what it takes to walk as a new creation, to know about it, and to grow in it.
