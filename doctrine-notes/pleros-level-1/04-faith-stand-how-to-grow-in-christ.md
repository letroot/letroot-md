# Faith Stand: How to Grow in Christ

**00:00 \- The Reality of the New Creation**

Brothers and sisters, we are looking at "faith stand: how we grow in Christ." Upon believing the gospel, one thing is clear. Let's look at Ephesians chapter 1\. One thing is clear is that at the instance of believing the gospel, we become brand new. We've seen that when a man believes the gospel of Christ, what he has is not just salvation as a result. Let's say someone was about to be hit by a vehicle and someone runs and saves the person. The person doesn't have anything other than a result of salvation. He was not hit by that car. Someone was about to be killed and you saved him from the person about to kill him. What that person will be grateful for is, "I was saved from that situation. I could have lost it there, it could have ended there." Sometimes this is the idea and conception people have about salvation, but that idea is definitely incomplete. Because the picture painted to us about what happens at faith in Christ is that we become brand new.

Even more, as we proceed from the text we are looking at, we don't just become brand new at the instance of salvation, we enter into God's purpose via that same means by which we are made new. Now let's see Ephesians 1:13. But there's a big question: when we are made new, how do we walk in this new reality that we have? It is fantastic to be a new man, but it means nothing if all that reality does not translate into the essence of it, to walk in it. That's what we want to get to.

Ephesians 1:13 says, "In Him you also trusted, after you heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation." Someone can say, "Well, I just got saved. I won't be damned. I'm no longer going to perish, or I was about to perish, He took me out of that." Fantastic, but it didn't stop there. "In whom also, having believed, you were sealed with the Holy Spirit of promise." Something happened. You didn't just get saved; there was a substantial work done to you. You were sealed with the Holy Spirit of promise. We have seen that in this same Ephesians 1, we were told about God's purpose. We were told that God's purpose is what He wants, that He likes. Ephesians 1:9 says He has made known the mystery of His will, according to His good pleasure which He purposed in Himself. So His purpose is what He wants and what He likes.

Then we were told what He wants and what He likes in Ephesians 1:5. He has brought us to sonship, adoption as sons or adoption of children, by Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the good pleasure of His will. When sonship was explained to us in Romans 8:15, we were told that we entered into sonship by receiving the substance of the Spirit. We didn't enter into sonship just by God saying, "Walk into it and you are now in it." There was a real work done to us. We received the Spirit. There was a substantial thing that happened. Entering into God's purpose is not just an experiential result where the person did not suffer damnation; that person is now in a reality of purpose. There are substantial realities: you have the Spirit. Romans 8:15 says, "You did not receive the spirit of bondage again to fear, but you received the Spirit of adoption, whereby we cry, Abba, Father."

Faith in Christ makes a man new. He has the Spirit. It makes the man new such that the former or old reality is gone. Notice, you didn't receive the spirit of bondage again to fear. Romans 8:9 says, "You are not in the flesh, you are in the Spirit, if so be that the Spirit of God dwells in you."

**05:00 \- The Instruction to Walk in the Spirit**

So we have become a new creation. The best way to encapsulate this reality of receiving the Spirit, where the former is gone, is to sum it up by saying that we are a new creation. 2 Corinthians 5:17 says, "If any man is in Christ, he is a new creation." The man is in Christ by faith, when he believes the Spirit puts him into Christ. 1 Corinthians 12:13 says, "By one Spirit we were all baptized into one body, whether Jews or Greeks, slaves or free, and have all been made to drink into one Spirit." The Spirit puts him in Christ and so he has the Spirit in him. If the man is in Christ, he is a new creation. Old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new, and all things are of God. Everything about this man is of God.

The old reality is gone, the new reality has come. What's the essence of it all? Let's see Galatians 5\. Galatians 5:24 says, "And those who are Christ's have crucified the flesh with its passions and desires." The old reality is gone, with its affections and lusts, the very wants to sin and the very ability for sin. They have crucified it. It is dead and gone. Their inward man is new. The outward man remains the same, except it changes naturally, which is a different issue. The inward man is changed completely. The old man is gone. The body of sin is gone. They are now new on the inside.

But look at verse 25\. If we live in the Spirit, we have crucified the flesh. Now we live in the Spirit. If we live in the Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit. Notice that he didn't say, "If you live in the Spirit, you will surely walk in the Spirit." That means being new in Christ does not guarantee walking in the newness. Being new in Christ does not automatically guarantee walking in the newness. Rather, the walk is an instruction. That you are new, you are now instructed. Now that you live in the Spirit, you have received the Spirit of sonship, you have been sealed with the Holy Spirit, you are no longer in the flesh, you are now in the Spirit, you are alive in the Spirit, you are alive unto God—now, walk.

The walk is an instruction. If we live in the Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit. You will see the instruction to walk in verse 16 as well. It says, "I say then: Walk in the Spirit, and you shall not fulfill the lust of the flesh." So walking in the Spirit is an instruction. Being new is not an instruction; it is your reality when you have believed. You don't do anything to be new; you are already new. You don't do anything for the old man to be gone other than faith in Christ. Once you have believed on the Lord Jesus, the inward man is changed from the old to the new. You are now a partaker of the divine nature. The old man is dead and gone. The new man is here and now, and is forever. But you are now instructed to walk, to not be caught up in the bondage of these things. You are instructed to walk.

**10:00 \- Growth: The Prerequisite for Walking**

What I want you to realize is that walking in the Spirit, walking in this reality of the new creation that we are, can only happen by growth. Let's go to Ephesians chapter 4\. As we look at verse 17, Ephesians 4:17 says, "This I say, therefore, and testify in the Lord, that you should no longer walk as the rest of the Gentiles walk." He's saying, "I want you to walk, but not as you used to walk." It doesn't mean stop walking entirely; he's saying walk in a different way. You used to walk in a particular way; I don't want you to walk that way anymore.

Another thing I want you to notice in Ephesians 4:17 is the word "therefore." When you see the word "therefore," find out what it is there for. "Therefore" is the conclusion to certain premises. What are the premises that led to that? What led him to say "therefore walk"? The sentence preceding Ephesians 4:17 actually begins all the way in Ephesians 4:11. Ephesians 4:11 was talking about the fact that the Lord Jesus gave the leaders of the church. He gave the apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers. What did He give them for? Verse 12: "For the perfecting of the saints for the work of ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ."

He continues speaking about what the Lord gave them for from verse 11 all the way to verse 16\. In telling us why the Lord gave these leaders of the church, which he said is to perfect the saints, verse 13 says the perfecting of the saints will bring the saints to a particular point. When you are new, that doesn't automatically guarantee your walk. The walk is an instruction. Every believer has to change. There is nothing wrong about changing; everybody needs to change. If you don't change, you have set yourself up for problems because the way you are going to start out is in the flesh. Everybody will start out in the flesh. Everybody needs to change so they don't end up in the wrong state. We are all called to change. You should all be comfortable changing; you should not even apologize to anybody for changing, and you should not allow anybody to blackmail you for changing.

People like to blackmail people for changing, saying, "Oh, you have changed." It means you have become wiser, you have seen something better, you now understand this in the right way, you have aligned yourself to it. What is the crime in that? Are you supposed to be going worse or are you supposed to be getting better? So He is calling us to walk. He says, "No longer walk as you used to walk; you need to walk in a different way." The premise of that "therefore, walk this way" is what he was talking about earlier: what the church is meant to do to us. The leaders of the church are to perfect the saints until the saints come to a particular point.

Verse 13: "Till we all come to the unity of the faith and of the knowledge of the Son of God, to a perfect man, to the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ." He's talking about knowledge. Unity of the faith is about knowledge. Knowledge of the Son of God, that's clearly knowledge. Perfect man, this is clearly pointing to the development of a man. The measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ. If you were in any doubt that he's talking about the development of a man, the word "stature" makes it clear. You should come to the measure of the stature. Paul is saying that the goal Jesus has is very high. He wants you to come to the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ, a state in your development.

I want to notice something. They will describe this development that Jesus wants us to have with the word "growth." Remember we are looking at "walk." That instruction to walk is premised on growth. You are new in Christ, having a new nature completely. But walking in that nature is premised on growth. You have a mind to your spirit like you have a brain to your body. If your outward man should change but the brain remained the same, and you had no mirror whatsoever to notice that you have changed, you will continue acting the same old way despite the new capacity that you have. This is a challenge that people have with their inward man. Their inward man has changed entirely. All things have passed away, all things have become new. All things that became new are the old things that passed away. Every iota of who they were changed to new, and all of it became of God. But sometimes they have no idea what is going on.

**15:00 \- Growing Up Into Christ in All Things**

So Jesus wants us to be brought to the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ. That measure of stature is talking about growth. How do we know? Look at verses 14 and 15: "That we should no longer be children." You should no longer walk as the rest of the Gentiles walk. You should no longer be children, tossed to and fro and carried about with every wind of doctrine. Every wind of doctrine means one person tells you this, you move that way. Another person tells you that, you move that way. Sometimes "tossed to and fro" is peer pressure. Sometimes it is parental pressure. You are pulled by the pressures of your background into all sorts of things. There's an anchor in every man because every man is made by God a truth-seeking being. You are pulled to and fro by every wind of doctrine, by the trickery of men, in the cunning craftiness of deceitful plotting.

Verse 15: "But, speaking the truth in love, may grow up in all things into Him who is the head—Christ." By speaking the truth in love, the preachers speaking the truth in love, what will happen? You will grow up into Him in all things. The premise of verse 17 is growth. Speaking the truth in love, you will grow up into Christ. The bar set for you is clear: He wants you to grow up into Christ in all things.

There is nothing strange in having walked in the flesh. The only thing that is strange is staying there. There is no conduct of the flesh that is the problem God is having with you. The only problem He's having with you is a lack of repentance. I'm not talking about lack of repentance only for salvation. I'm talking about lack of repentance also for growth. Along the way, you will still stumble. You must always stay repentant to grow.

He says we will grow up into Him in all things, who is the head, Christ. The bar of your growth is set, and it is on that premise he now told you in verse 17, "This I say, therefore, that you should no longer walk as the rest of the Gentiles walk." Your walk is meant to change on the premise of your growth. Because of your growth, ministered to you by the church, you should no longer walk as the rest of the Gentiles do. Our walk in the new creation, so that we can fulfill God's purpose for our lives, can only happen by growth.

I want to show you other texts that show growth follows right after the reality of becoming new. Let us look at 1 Peter chapter 1, and we will read it into chapter 2\. When you have been made new, the next thing God is hungry for is your growth. He's not hungry for your perfection next. He is hungry for your growth. Your growth will set you right. Don't set a bar that God didn't set for you. If your bar is perfection, how are you going to have growth? You will have to stumble here and there and get yourself up, keep walking, keep growing. Then later you'll be stable, you'll be able to walk firmly. The bar is set, and it is that of growth.

**20:00 \- Nature, Growth, and Walk: The Divine Sequence**

1 Peter 1:22 says, "Since you have purified your souls in obeying the truth through the Spirit in sincere love of the brethren, love one another fervently with a pure heart." This is instructing believers to walk in love. But he told you the reason in verse 23: "Having been born again." You can walk in love, having been born again. This is talking about nature. They want your walk, but they'll show you that the bridge between your nature and your walk is growth. If you are going to walk in such a way that you are going to fulfill God's purpose, after you receive the nature of Christ, you need to grow.

He says, "Having been born again, not of corruptible seed but incorruptible, through the word of God which lives and abides forever." The word by which the gospel was preached to you. When you heard the gospel, you were born again by that word. You now have the seed of God in you, incorruptible seed. You have a new nature. So you can walk in love. Walk in love because you are born again, you have a new nature. Because of your new nature, how are you going to have that walk? Look at 1 Peter 2:1. "Therefore, laying aside all malice, all deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and all evil speaking." When you say laying aside there, if you already have a new seed, that laying aside is that you are recognizing on your mind, "I am no longer this." You are setting it aside. "As newborn babes, desire the pure milk of the word, that you may grow thereby." Desire the sincere milk of the word that you may grow thereby.

After you receive your nature, the next call they are making is a call for growth so that you can walk. Nature, growth, walk. We are looking at "faith stand: how we grow in Christ." We have seen that when you become new in Christ, the point of being new in Christ is so that you can walk new in Christ. But your walking new in Christ is dependent on your growth. How did Peter say you will grow? "Desire the sincere milk of the word." It looks as if it is just knowledge that will make you grow. But you need to put it somewhere in your mind. The sincere milk of the word that you may grow thereby. When we have a new nature, the next call is for our growth. Knowledge comes first.

Let's see the third person that will say this issue of growth. James chapter 1\. James says that when you have a new nature, the next issue is growth. James 1:17 says, "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow of turning." Look at verse 18: "Of His own will He brought us forth by the word of truth, that we might be a kind of firstfruits of His creatures." He has brought us forth. What do you need next? He says, "So then, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath; for the wrath of man does not produce the righteousness of God. Therefore lay aside all filthiness and overflow of wickedness, and receive with meekness the implanted word, which is able to save your souls."

**25:00 \- The Danger of Misreckoning Our Identity**

Again, what do you need? Knowledge. Notice the "implanted word" is speaking about growth. It is able to save your souls. He used a description that pertains to growth: a seed growing. After receiving the nature of God, after becoming a new creation, what it calls for is growth so that you can walk. You can't jump into walking. Sometimes people say, "I'm now new, I don't know, I will try my best to behave like this," and it is not working. Because they are putting the cart before the horse. They are trying to walk before they grow. The man knows he's new, he loves the Lord, his heart is bleeding to repent, to do what is right, to align with God. But he is trying to walk without growing. The premise of walking is growth. It is the premise of growth that makes them say you should walk. You can't put walking before growing.

What makes a man walk is not willpower. What makes a man walk is growth. You say, "I want to walk, I don't know, I've had this issue, I've battled this particular thing, maybe one particular habit, one particular addiction, one particular tendency." It could be anything. "Why do I do things in this particular way? Why am I always contradicting what I should do?" Don't jump to trying to do first. You ought to start with growth.

Which is why the very next statement from James, immediately after verse 21, is verse 22: "But be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves." This one, because of how people have read it, has caused a lot of problems. Someone says, "I'm only hearing, I'm not doing." They try to do, they get disappointed, they give up again. Because when James said "be doers of the word, not hearers only," being a doer of the word is a result of doing something else. If you don't understand what he expects you to do to be a doer, you will try to be a doer without having done the first thing and you will get disappointed.

"Be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves." The word "deceiving yourselves" is the word in the Greek *paralogizomai*. It is from two words: *para* (beside) and *logizomai* (to account). That word is an accounting term: to take into account, to take an inventory of. When you take an inventory of something, you are not creating the inventory; you are only accounting for the inventory. Let's say you go into the store and they say, "Go and take an inventory of what is in the store." You don't take an inventory of what is not there. You don't create things; whatever is there, you take an inventory of. If he says the "hearer only" is not taking inventory of what's in the store, he is taking inventory aside. It means he's not acknowledging what is there. He's misreckoning.

The man is misreckoning himself. It's very possible for someone to look into the mirror of the word, to the reality of who he is in Christ, and instead of acknowledging "I am a new creation, I am dead to sin," he misreckons himself. "I don't know why I'm like this, all the things I've been through, what I've seen in my life." He's not reckoning what he has become in Christ; he's reckoning what he no longer has. His inward man has completely changed, but he's still reckoning what he used to have. He has a mind to his spirit like we have a brain to our body, but the mind to the spirit is still reckoning the old things. His problem is not that he has the same nature. His problem is the state of the mind. He's misreckoning himself.

**30:00 \- The Response Distinguishes the Believer**

The interesting thing about that "hearer only" is that the "hearer only" who is misreckoning himself actually agreed with the word but still went ahead to misreckon himself. Look at verse 23: "For if anyone is a hearer of the word and not a doer, he is like a man observing his natural face in a mirror; for he observes himself, goes away, and immediately forgets what kind of man he was." He observed himself. He did really see who he was. The man that is a hearer is like a man observing. He's there and indeed he observed himself. He saw it. He went to the mirror of the word, he saw that "I am the righteousness of God, I am dead to sin, the old man is dead and gone." He saw that in the mirror. But he goes away and immediately forgets what manner of man he was.

That "forget" there can never be that if you ask him he cannot remember. If you ask him, he will likely remember. He observed himself. The "forget" there means he was not conscious. He was not mindful. He was not conscious of it. He goes away and straightaway he forgets the manner of man he was. That was the problem of the hearer. Despite what he has seen, he was living in a state of forgetfulness of who he was.

But look at verse 25\. "But he who looks into the perfect law of liberty and continues in it, and is not a forgetful hearer but a doer of the work, this one will be blessed in what he does." He looks into the perfect law of liberty and continues. This one continues, not being a forgetful hearer but a doer of the work. Look at the order. He continues, so he is not forgetful. He is a doer, and therefore he is blessed. The doer is a result of continuing and not forgetting. The significant action that they both took was not looking at the mirror; they both looked at the mirror. It's what they did with looking at the mirror. One goes away, one continues. He abides with what he has seen. "I am a new creation." He abides with it.

That word is very important to how we grow. They both looked at the mirror. They both received the knowledge of the word. But the knowledge of the word was not the distinguishing factor in their growth. It was the response to the knowledge of the word that was the distinguishing factor. The response of one person was going away. The response of another was continuing (*parameno*). The response was the distinguishing factor.

Remember, when we looked at the new creation who we are in Christ, Romans 6 was a very major text. We saw in Romans chapter 6 that the old man is gone, the new man has come. Paul kept emphasizing that you should already know this. Romans 6:3 says, "Or do you not know that as many of us as were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into His death?" Knowing this, that our old man was crucified with Him. He was taking their knowing it for granted. We have gone through all of that, knowing that the old man is gone, knowing that the new man is here. Knowing that Christ, having been raised from the dead, dies no more; death no longer has dominion over Him. You should know that the old is gone, the new is here. He was emphasizing knowing this. Notice that he never instructed them to know. He was assuming their knowledge.

**35:00 \- Reckoning and Presenting the New Man**

By the time he gets to verse 11, he gives an instruction. He was taking the knowledge for granted, but there was an instruction he gave in verse 11: "Likewise you also, reckon yourselves to be dead indeed to sin, but alive to God in Christ Jesus our Lord." Knowing it is not enough. He said you have to reckon yourself to be dead indeed to sin and alive to God. That one was the instruction. You must consciously reckon. The reality is already there and you know it. Take it into account. Reckon yourself to be dead indeed to sin and alive to God.

Knowing is not enough; you have to reckon yourself. That was the instruction. And then, verse 12: "Therefore do not let sin reign in your mortal body, that you should obey it in its lusts." Armed with the tools of how to handle the situation: you know, and you are reckoning. Therefore, you won't let sin reign. He didn't say there's any other thing you need. People are looking for magic all over the place. He mentioned what you need. Knowing this, and therefore reckoning, therefore do not let sin reign in your mortal body. Sin is not to reign in your mortal body because of what you know and what you reckon. Do not let sin reign in your mortal body that you should obey it in its lusts.

Verse 13: "And do not present your members as instruments of unrighteousness to sin, but present yourselves to God as being alive from the dead, and your members as instruments of righteousness to God." The word "present" (or "yield") is *paristemi*. It is from two words: *para* (beside) and *histemi* (to stand). That word "present" means to stand beside yourself. When you are standing beside yourself, you are not making yourself anything; you are already who you are. You are only standing beside it, affirming it.

If you are making a presentation of something you have created, you are not creating something new at that point, you are only showing what that thing is. If you are presenting yourself, you are not making yourself anything, you are only showing yourself what you are. You are presenting yourself. I'm an instrument of righteousness to God. You are presenting yourself as you are.

What we are told is the process of our growth. Our growth happens by knowledge and then by reckoning. James says we need that implanted word that saves our soul, and then we shouldn't be hearers only but by not misreckoning ourselves. We need the knowledge, and then we need the reckoning and continuing, not going away. What makes the seed grow? Reckoning. Continuing.

What did Paul say? You already know. Likewise, reckon. Present. Stand beside yourself. We can say reckoning, presenting, continuing. It's the core issue in how we grow. Reckoning, presenting, continuing in the knowledge of who we are in Christ. You receive that seed of the knowledge of who we are, and you are presenting it. I am a new creation. I am dead to sin. I don't have the flesh. You are presenting it. How conscious of it are you? Are you forgetful about it? How are you going to walk in it? How conscious of it are you? As the consciousness of it pervades your mind, your actions begin to change. That is an exact science. You don't have to wish it.

**40:00 \- Renewing the Mind to Walk in Newness**

There's a way you can personally know that you are so conscious of it that, in the face of particular temptations, you are certain that you are going to prevail. That's what the Lord Jesus did at Gethsemane. He prayed lest you enter into temptation. He Himself went there to the end that He doesn't enter into it, and it was done in Him that He was fine. He was sure that it was done. There's a way you present yourself that you know it.

Growth is a reality. Stature is a reality. You can know: I have developed this thing. I have developed this particular area. I have addressed this particular issue. I've become strong in this particular regard. Let's go to Ephesians 4\. I want us to wrap up at Romans 12, but the premise upon which we are told to walk is our growth. Notice that our nature has changed, but every issue about our growth is really about our mind. Our mind is the only reason why we are walking or we are not walking. We are walking because of our growth, and every issue about our growth is about our mind: knowledge, and then not forgetting (consciousness, reckoning, presenting). The issue is about our mind.

Your nature has changed. Your inward man has changed. Now it is the mind that you need to address. The same way, if someone has a brain that has changed from an animal-like state to an eagle-like state, and he has the same brain, the only thing that needs to change is what is on that brain so that he may now act in accordance with what has changed about his outward man. It is the same way about our inward man. Your inward man has changed. What you need is a change of your mind. In knowledge, and in consciousness and reckoning.

Ephesians 4:17 says, "This I say, therefore, and testify in the Lord, that you should no longer walk as the rest of the Gentiles walk, in the futility of their mind." Futility there means emptiness; something is absent that should be present on their mind. The mind is never a vacuum, so he is addressing futility relative to what ought to be on their mind. They are walking in the futility of their mind, "having their understanding darkened, being alienated from the life of God, because of the ignorance that is in them, because of the blindness of their heart; who, being past feeling, have given themselves over to lewdness, to work all uncleanness with greediness." The problem with why they are walking that way is their mind. The knowledge on their mind and the consciousness (reckoning) on their mind or forgetfulness.

But you have not so learned Christ. Your mind is not futile. There are things you have been told. Your understanding is not darkened; you are not ignorant. You are not blind to this. You have not so learned Christ, if indeed you have heard Him and have been taught by Him, as the truth is in Jesus: "that you put off, concerning your former conduct, the old man which grows corrupt according to the deceitful lusts." If you leave that information there, it will keep worsening. It grows corrupt according to deceitful lusts, and there will be a lot of lusts in the world to keep encouraging it. "And be renewed in the spirit of your mind, and that you put on the new man which was created according to God, in true righteousness and holiness."

**45:00 \- Transformation Through Devotional Worship**

Your mind is the issue. This "put on" and "put off" is the same implication as standing and reckoning. Whenever you say, "I am no longer this," you are invariably affirming the new creation. "I'm dead to sin, the flesh has been crucified with its affections and lusts, I'm not in the flesh, I am in the Spirit, I am born of God, I'm a partaker of the divine nature, I have the life of God in me." You are putting it on your mind, as you are putting off the other one off your mind. You are laying aside, setting aside. You are affirming who you are.

Romans 12 wraps it up. Romans 12:1-2 says, "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service." It is your logical service. Present your bodies. The word "service" there is *latreia*, talking about temple worship. The temple worship of the Jews was the regular worship morning and evening before God. He's saying that this standing beside ourselves is our worship, our prayer. What happens when we do that? He says, "And do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind."

By doing this, you are not conformed to the world, but you are transformed (*metamorphoo*). Another word reflecting growth. You are changed, the kind of growth we are going to see from an egg to a larva or caterpillar, to a pupa, and then to a butterfly. You are transformed by the renewing of your mind. By affirming on your mind again and again, by presenting on your mind again and again. "I am brand new. I'm dead to sin. I'm alive to God." You are transformed by the renewing of your mind, that you may prove what is that good and acceptable and perfect will of God. You prove or do God's purpose. You are able to carry out God's purpose that is good, acceptable, and perfect. You are able to do it when you are presenting yourself or renewing your mind. When you are affirming, you are renewing your mind. "I'm brand new, I'm dead to sin, I'm alive to God." And you are doing it as your reasonable worship. That means it is a dedicated devotional life you have.

Maybe you take a particular issue. You are looking at your character, or you are looking at the supernatural, or you are looking at your love walk. You are looking at a particular habit. You stay there devotionally. "I will address this issue. I am dead to sin. I am alive to God." You stay there devotionally addressing that issue. It is your reasonable worship, your devotional worship of God. You stay there devotionally addressing the issue. You are fulfilling God's purpose that way. You are new to walk. You walk by growth.
