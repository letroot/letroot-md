Redemption is the primary act of resistance and opposition to the reign of the devil, and it is a legitimate act. That work of redemption has gotten us a legal right to the victory of life. Jesus has obtained life.

But in this age, the devil is still present as a rebel. Despite the victory of the Lord Jesus, he is still present as a rebel. The devil has been defeated, but he is still active as a rebel. What does this mean? It means we still need to engage him.

So death and darkness still push as rebel forces. They still push deception. They still push influence, temptation, and lust. They still seek to affect people toward harder bondage. They still seek, where possible, to move into demonic attachment and to produce supernatural effects in bodies, in events, and in material situations. That is why the believer cannot be careless. The devil has lost authority, but he still seeks activity. The answer is not fear. The answer is informed engagement based on redemption.

Colossians 2:15: "Having disarmed principalities and powers, he made a public spectacle of them, triumphing over them in it." Again, this word disarm is to put off, to take away from them their authority. He has stripped them of their authority. If you think disarm means he has taken away their weapons absolutely, you will be making a mistake.

That is why we say redemption has taken away his authority, not his operative power in the present age. So our engagement now is not to erase his existence or ability from the age. Our engagement is to restrain the use of that power by the legal victory of Christ. That is an important distinction.

So Luke 11, when we read it, is that he has stripped them of their authority. They have no right to act. That means by the right and power of redemption we can always restrain the activity of the enemy. We can always reject it, restrain it. By redemption the devil has been defeated, and Ephesians will now say he is under our feet.

It also means that where there are supernatural effects of darkness on bodies, on events, or on material situations, those things too can be restrained. We are talking about all the dimensions we have mentioned: the natural effects of death and corruption as they press on this age, deception, lust and temptation, demonic activity, and supernatural effects on matter, body, and events. In every one of these, redemption gives the believer a basis to stand and restrain.

That will be the second way we engage. Redemption is the first way we engage. The second way we engage is personal. The third way we engage is ministry, which involves prayer.

How do we engage the enemy? The first one has been done. Redemption has engaged the enemy and has taken away his authority. Second engagement is personal. The third engagement is ministry. How? By preaching the word to cast down every imagination. By prayer, setting up events and circumstances against the event he wants to do. By exercise of authority or commands. Or let me just say ministry of the Spirit. By the word, prayer, and ministry of the Spirit. Or we could have said by prayer, the teaching of the word, and the ministry of the Spirit. That's the third part of our engagement.

## The End of the War

When will spiritual warfare end? Despite the defeat of the enemy, which Revelation 12 also describes, he is still active as a rebel spirit. They are still active as rebel forces. When will the war end?

Revelation gives us several accounts of the end of this war. The clear one that showed us the defeat of the devil, because some of the accounts show us the defeat of the beast and of the false prophet, but the one that shows the defeat of the devil is Revelation chapter 20.

The war is going to end when God's purpose is consummated. The Lord said this gospel of the kingdom will be preached in all the world and then the end will come.

So it is on that note, brethren, I encourage us that we need to understand the war we are in. The war we are in cannot end until the coming of the Lord. That means we have an occupying duty now. Occupy till I come. Which is to put the devil under check, to restrain him by enforcing what redemption has accomplished in our lives personally and then in our ministry, our ministry of prayer, the word, and the Spirit.

This is why not knowing that you are at war is dangerous. If you don't know that you are at war, your defenses are down. If you don't know that you are at war, you won't launch the attacks that are needed. But if you understand the reality of the war, the origin of the war, the nature of the war, and the victory that redemption has brought, then you know how to stand and how to engage.

You know what the attacks are, what the defenses are, and what the proper responses are. That is part of the wisdom required in spiritual warfare.

But for now, I hope spiritual warfare is clear. We are at war. There is an ongoing attack, whether I like it or not, of falsehood. Ongoing attack of the influence of lust, of the flesh, of darkness. Ongoing attack of the devil seeking to become demon-attachment to people that are hardened by deception or ignorance or willful hardening of their heart. There is the devil seeking supernatural effect of darkness on matter and on bodies and in events. He is actively doing those things.

But we can engage that war and restrain all of that. All those things, every one of them, all the five, we can restrain it. We can restrain it in our lives personally and also in our ministry. And that is what we are supposed to do in spiritual warfare.
