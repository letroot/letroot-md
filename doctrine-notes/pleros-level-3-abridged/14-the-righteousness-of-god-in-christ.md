## Righteousness as a Descriptive Word

Let me explain the reason why we are going through all of this. The word righteousness is a word that, at its core, is a descriptive word. There are words that are not descriptive words. There are words that are a thing itself. But righteousness is always a qualifying word. At the core of righteousness is that something is right.

So when you say that thing is righteous, or righteousness, you are referring to something being qualified. It is not a word whose value is in itself. Its value is in what it is saying about something else. If you are going to talk about righteousness, the state of something being right, you must first talk about what right is, and right is just a qualifying word to say something is correct.

Sometimes in the Greek, when you see the word right, it can be like justified. It is still someone's judgment they are talking about, someone judging that thing correct or not correct. This is why, when we were looking at the issue of truth and morality, we were seeing goodness and righteousness as qualifying words. So unrighteous is not correct in someone's judgment. Whether that person's judgment is by truth is another question. But it is correct or not correct.

Righteousness means this thing is correct. This thing is not correct. That is why the legal idea comes into righteousness, even though righteousness at its core is ontological. It is also moral.
