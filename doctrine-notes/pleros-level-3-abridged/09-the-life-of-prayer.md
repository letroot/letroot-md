## Prayer Is Fellowship With the Father

As children of God, it is already clear from this text that the very first thing that we do as children of God is to fellowship with God. From verse 15 it says we have received that Spirit whereby we cry, Abba, Father. It means the primary thing that we do as ones who have the spirit of sonship is that we cry, Abba, Father. It means we interact with God. We fellowship with him. We engage with him.

The word prayer itself in the Greek is the word `proseuche`, whilst the verb to pray is the word `proseuchomai`. I will spell it. The word prayer is `proseuche`, `P R O S E U C H E`. The word to pray, the verb to pray, is `proseuchomai`. That is why you see that in almost all languages you hear statements like, I pray thee, O king. It might not even be God. You can use the word pray for a human being. It can even be that I am praying anybody here, like I pray thee, please let me do this. It is like I beseech you.

The second word there just means to wish or to desire. So the word prayer in its mildest form is to desire towards. It can be to long towards. There are times where it is not about asking for anything, so it is not about beseeching for something. It is just that you wish or you desire towards. You are just fellowshipping with the individual in question. You wish to, you desire towards, you long towards.

If we have the Spirit of God and by the Spirit of God we cry, Abba, Father, it means the spirit of sonship that we have received is a spirit that leads to prayer, that ordinarily by design leads to prayer. So I repeat again, the life of purpose is a life of prayer.

Sunship, the spirit of sonship, is the spirit by which we cry, Abba, Father. Sonship means we are children of God and heirs of God. As children of God, what is expected of us is fellowship with the Father. Prayer is fellowship, interaction, communion, engagement, communication with the Father. So whatever kind of communication, at the mildest that desire and longing towards another, is what prayer is.

That is why you are going to see that when Jesus was told to teach them to pray, and we also see from the example of Paul, the Father reality in how we speak with God, how we engage with God, how we fellowship with God, is at the fore. I want you to turn your Bibles to Luke chapter 11. The disciples of the Lord asked him, Lord, teach us to pray, as John also taught his disciples.

Whilst it becomes more obvious with the coming of the Lord Jesus and the fulfillment of redemption in him, it is what God had always desired from the beginning. God made us so that we can interact with him as a child with the father. "Our Father who art in heaven, hallowed be your name." That is what the Lord told them to pray.
