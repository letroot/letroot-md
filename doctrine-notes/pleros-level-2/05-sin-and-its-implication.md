# Sin and Its Implication

## Sin as a Doctrinal Summary

Let us turn our Bibles to Ephesians chapter one. We are looking at a very important subject matter. This is still doctrinal summaries: sin and its implications. Many times we make assumptions about different things in God's word. And one thing that we see is that when we don't have truth, we don't have certainty of truth, we are going to be easily tossed to and fro. That is something that Paul said in Ephesians 4 verse 14\. He says that you be no longer children tossed to and fro. He is not talking about children by age; he is talking about spiritual maturity. One way to tell that you are not getting strong and mature spiritually is that you don't have certainty. When you don't have certainty, you are in doubt, you are wandering. It is like being uncertain if someone should ask what is the color of the chair that you are sitting on, and you look at it and you are like, "I'm not too sure." That is because you have not been taught. The moment you have been taught, you are very certain. The color is maybe white, the color is this or that. Whenever we don't have certainty of truth, we are bound to have instability.

This is one of the challenges we face with many believers. They are found having instability in the way they do many things, in the way they pray, in the way they walk with God, in the way they fellowship with Him, because they don't have the certainty of truth. The work of the church is to give us the truth. In Ephesians 4, right after he said in verse 14 that you shouldn't be tossed to and fro, the next verse says the preachers are to speak the truth in love. When they teach us the truth, we are no longer going to be tossed about.

That is what we want to see today. We want to look at what is the truth on sin and its implication. What really is the reason for many of the things that we see today? You remember our study on doctrinal summaries; we are studying this teaching for one reason. Because we have seen that at the point of salvation, you enter into God's purpose for your life. Sometimes people don't answer that part of their life's question very well. If they are asked, "Why are you a Christian?" they say, "Because I don't want to go to hell." Christianity is not just about salvation from sin, death, darkness, and hell. Christianity at its core and at its roots is about why God made you in the first place. God didn't make us to save us, because He didn't make us to be in a problem in the first place. He made us for His purpose. Something went wrong, which is what we are studying today, and He brought us back to His purpose via the redemption that He has accomplished in Christ.

At the point where you get saved, one of the important things you must know is that you are now found in God's purpose for your life. Salvation is that you have now entered into God's purpose. You are not just another face in the crowd. You are not just an entity that exists in this world. There is a purpose that God has for your life. Something about God's purpose is that it is never too early and it is never too late to enter into it. Of course, there is a sense in which we can say it is too late in the sense that someone is finding out late, but at any point where you find out about God's purpose, that is the point God already foreknows you will, and He is planning to work with you from there. The moment you find out about what God's purpose for your life is, it is time for you to align with it. There is a reason that God made you.

Having entered into God's purpose, your major desire and goal should be to fulfill this purpose that God has for you. The only way you can fulfill God's purpose is by growth.

## Growth, Discipleship, and Paul's Doctrine

Let me give an example I've been using. If someone is told, "What I want you to do is to carry these two bags of cement from this place to that second gate," the person looks at it and says, "I can't carry these two bags of cement from this place to that second gate, let's face it." But the person knows that if they go and train, gym, grow, and build themselves to the point where they are able to do that, then they will be able to carry out that purpose. It is the same way with God's purpose. When God gives you His purpose, what He wants you to accomplish, it means there is another goal you must have. You must first have the goal to grow to be able to do that purpose. That is the reason for the church. The church is meant to build you so that you will be able to carry out God's purpose for your life.

The church building us to carry out that purpose is via discipleship. You need to be taught the Word of God, which is exactly what we are looking at today. You need to be taught through the doctrine that Paul has given us. One of the things that Paul says we need to understand if we are going to walk effectively with God is sin and the implication of sin.

Let me take us to the text I'm starting with, Ephesians 1 verse 7\. I hope you remember that in Ephesians chapter 3, Paul told us that he had given the summary of the entire doctrine of Christ in Ephesians 1\. In this summary of the doctrine, I want you to notice what he said. "In Him we have redemption through His blood, the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of His grace, which He made to abound toward us in all wisdom and prudence." We have redemption, which is the forgiveness of sins. What Christ has accomplished is because there is first a problem called sin or sins. The reason for what Christ had to do is because of the problem called sin.

The way many of us think about sin is that bad thing that someone is doing. You are not supposed to do that bad thing, that is a sin. That is true, there is that understanding of sin. But the primary understanding of sin in God's Word is not like that. That is an effect. If we say what is sin according to what God's Word teaches us, there are two things that will be primary. The first thing we need to know about sin is that sin just means a state or a thing that is contrary to what ought to be. In the Greek, as well as in the Hebrew, the word sin means to miss the mark. Let's say someone puts a target: shoot that arrow and it must touch this target. What is sin? Sin is any shot you make that does not touch that target. Because the person has given you what you must do, anything besides what the person told you to do is sin, it is contrary to the target that the person has given you. Any situation or condition contrary to what someone originally expects is sin.

If you look at our world, our world is in a state that is contrary to what God expects. Our world is in a state of sin. For example, in our world we are seeing death. And yet in the same world we are seeing life. If you think about life and death, death can only happen because there is first life. Let's use light and darkness. You can only have darkness because there is first light.

## Sin as a Contrary Condition in the World

It is when the light goes that you are going to see darkness. If you see a dead lizard and someone says, "That lizard is dead, what happened?" The only reason why you are feeling bad for the lizard is because the lizard had life. If you go and buy a toy lizard, nobody can say that lizard is dead, because it never had life. You just say that toy is non-living. The presence of death means there must first have been life. The life has gone, that is why you are seeing death. Death itself is describing sin, a condition that is not the original. Darkness as well is describing sin or the condition that sin brings, which is that there is initially light, but the light has gone, so we are seeing darkness.

When we talk about sin, sin is any condition or even action that is contrary to what is originally intended or expected. We can bring it to action. If someone does something evil or bad, you say, "No, that's not the way people are supposed to behave." Maybe someone speaks evil of another person, and you say, "No, that's not the way we should behave, we should be kindhearted, we should be good." What you did is contrary to what is expected. That's the way the scripture explains sin.

When we look at our world today, we see the condition of sin in the world in three ways. Number one, people believe falsehood. There is the truth they are supposed to believe, but people are believing something else. There are those who believe there is no God. That's falsehood. There are those who think they can do whatever they like. That's falsehood. The fact that people can believe falsehood shows something has gone wrong with our world. This is not the way things should be. The truth is what is expected, but we are seeing falsehood. The second way we see sin is in morality, what people do, the conduct of people. Someone steals, someone lies, someone kills. That person is doing contrary to what should have been done. So it is a condition of sin.

The third way that we see sin in this world is in nature, the nature of the world itself. That's why we can have sickness and disease in this world. All of these are contrary to what should be there originally. Health is what is expected in our world. Life is what is expected, but sickness can happen. Something has gone wrong with the world itself, showing us that sin is what is dominant, is what is reigning, is what is playing out in this world.

Then we must ask a question: how can sin be playing out in the world when what is originally intended is life, is light, is good? Life must be there first before we can see death. Light must be there first before we can see darkness. So why are we seeing a situation where there is death, where there is darkness, where there is evil in the world? What happened that led to that? That's what we want to look at in this teaching.

How am I supposed to think about sin? Many of us personalize sin too quickly. We think sin is about us and how bad we are, rather than sin being about the problem that is affecting us. We personalize sin too quickly. We are living in a world where there is sin in beliefs, in moral conduct, and in nature. We are seeing sin around us, not just in what people are doing. Whether people do anything or not, we are still seeing sin around us. We are seeing it in nature. We are seeing it in the things people believe. We are also seeing it in the things that people do. How come there is sin in this world?

I will give us two answers. The first answer is a philosophical answer, then the second one is a biblical and revelatory answer. The moment you see sin, or you see another example of sin—death or darkness—it should already tell you how come the darkness is happening. If you see death, it should already tell you how come there is such a thing that is dead. Because the moment I see death, it means this thing first had life, and the life has gone. Either someone took the life away, the life went away on its own, something happened that took the life away, and so that is why I am seeing death. If I am seeing darkness, it means there was first light. Something happened taking the light away, and therefore I am seeing darkness. That means if we are seeing sin in our world, evil in our world, in the conduct of people, in the nature of the world, it means there was initially the state of good, the state of light, the state of life. The original state of things could not have been like this. It also means something has happened that has pushed back that original nature, which is why we are now seeing this contradiction in the world.

That is the philosophical answer. It is something that God expects you to be able to know just by thinking about it. It is why Paul said that those who are suppressing the truth in unrighteousness, they are deliberately not believing the gospel. He is saying the evidence of the gospel is that something has gone wrong in our world. There is righteousness, but now we are seeing unrighteousness. It should be obvious to them this is not how things were meant to be originally, so they should believe in redemption.

## Sin Entering the World Through Man

This one is the revelatory and biblical answer. We are saying that the only way we can see the condition of sin, the condition of death, the condition of darkness is that there was first life and light and goodness and righteousness, but it went away for one reason or the other. Maybe someone pushed it back, maybe someone rejected it, maybe something happened. We need to find out, how did we arrive at this condition? Romans chapter 5 verse 12\. Paul speaking says, "Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world..." He is giving us a revelatory explanation beyond just the philosophical, rational explanation that something must have happened that took life away. He is now saying it was because of one man that sin entered the world.

Notice that he said sin entered the world. In the Greek, which is the language that Paul wrote, that word entered means to enter into. This means sin was not in the world before; it entered. How could sin enter the world if God created everything? Does it also mean that God created sin, but then it was not in the world? Where was it? Was it now with Him? If there was only God, then God created the world, so was sin hiding somewhere, then later it now entered into the world? The answer is no.

When we say sin entered into the world, one of the explanations we use to make it clear to us is that before sin entered the world, there was the possibility of sin, but there wasn't the reality of sin. Imagine God created the world and there was no salt at all. But we know today that salt is sodium chloride. That means it is formed from sodium and chlorine. So imagine God did not create salt at all, but God created sodium and put it somewhere, and God created chlorine and put it somewhere. Let's say God put all the sodium in Africa and all the chlorine in Europe. Someone says there is no salt in this world. But there is the possibility of salt. There is no reality of salt yet. If they bring the sodium and the chlorine together, then salt has entered into the world, but before then there was the possibility of salt in the world. It is the same way when God made the world. God did not make sin. But there is the possibility of sin.

## Genesis and the Biblical Account of Sin

Let's see the biblical account of sin. We have seen the philosophical one: sin is something contrary to what is originally intended, and we are seeing it in our beliefs, in morality, in the nature of the world. But how did this come about? "Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin." Because of this sin, death has entered, and thus death spread to all men because all sinned. That means there is this one situation done by one man that has brought in sin and death into the world. There is this one act of sin by one man that has brought in death and darkness into the world. Man is the entity responsible for sin's entrance into the world. He is the only entity capable of that.

The real question at this juncture is, what did man do that brought sin into the world? The reason why this is important is that when you know what man did that brought sin into the world, you know what to do to have God's righteousness. Usually people will say a man sinned. What sin? Is it lying, stealing, fornication? No. What was the sin that man did that has rendered the world in the way it is? Let us go to Genesis chapter 2 to notice what the primary sin in this world is.

We have said that sin is evident in our world, all of us can see it. There is a contrary reality to that which God wants that we are seeing in this world. Now we are explaining how that sin happened. Sin is not a reality God made; it is a possibility from the world that God had made. We are going to see the setup that existed that was the possibility of sin that brought about the reality of sin.

Go all the way to verse 7 in Genesis 2\. "And the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul." God made man. At this point, man is someone that can reason; he is a rational agent. Then what did God do to man? Look at verse 8\. "The Lord God planted a garden eastward in Eden, and there He put the man whom He had formed. And out of the ground the Lord God made every tree grow that is pleasant to the sight and good for food." Man is in a garden, and God planted every tree. It makes sense to us, because in a garden there will be trees, and they are pleasant to the sight and good for food, so we are imagining there is mango, orange, watermelon, many beautiful trees.

The problem starts after that text. The next statement says, "The tree of life was also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil." There is no tree of knowledge that we have ever seen before. You don't see a tree and say, "This tree is a tree of knowledge." Trees are material things. Knowledge is not a material thing. Knowledge belongs in the domain of the mind. Even if it is knowing about something material, the knowledge itself is in the mind. When it says He planted a tree of knowledge, we know you are not talking about natural or physical things anymore. What you are talking about exists on the mind. The first one just told us tree of life. But the second one said tree of the knowledge of good and evil. What is the tree of life? What is the tree of the knowledge of good and evil?

## The Tree of Life as Wisdom and Truth

The Bible explains the tree of life clearest to us in the book of Proverbs chapter 3\. We have said all of us are seeing sin in the world. We can explain that this sin must have happened because what was first has gone. Therefore, we are seeing the absence of what was first. We are seeing the absence of light, absence of life. What exactly happened that brought about this absence of life and light? We are seeing the account being explained to us, first by Paul, now in Genesis. It has to do with this tree of life and tree of the knowledge of good and evil, and they are definitely not physical trees. One of the challenges we have today is that people interpret this as a literal tree in the garden, and that man took the literal tree. People will say man took that apple. I don't even know what started this whole apple business. They say it is the apple that man took, and he ate it, then we now have Adam's apple. But Eve was the first person to eat it, so where is Eve's apple?

Let's see Proverbs chapter 3 and verse 13\. It says, "Happy is the man who finds wisdom, and the man who gains understanding; for her proceeds are better than the profits of silver, and her gain than fine gold." Let's go to Proverbs 23 verse 23\. It says, "Buy the truth, and do not sell it, also wisdom and instruction and understanding." In many translations, the words "it" and "also" are italicized, meaning they were added by the translators to aid reading. If we remove them to test the meaning: "Buy the truth, and sell not wisdom, instruction, and understanding." That means when you buy the truth, what you have is wisdom, instruction, and understanding.

Truth is something that is the case, whether anybody is there to know it or not. Assuming there is no human being in the world, and my phone is on this pulpit. The truth is that my phone is on the pulpit, but nobody is there to know it. But the moment someone is there to know it, the person says, "There is a phone on this pulpit." The person has gotten knowledge, wisdom, understanding of that truth. When you receive truth, what you have is wisdom, instruction, understanding. Instruction is usually because somebody is going to teach you to know the truth.

Remember what we were reading earlier, Proverbs 3:13. It said, "Happy is the man who finds wisdom." What is the root of wisdom? Buy the truth. Buy the truth and do not sell wisdom, instruction, understanding. Wisdom is about the truth, the understanding of truth. It says, "Happy is the man that finds wisdom, the man that gains understanding. Her proceeds are better than the profits of silver, and her gain than fine gold. She is more precious than rubies, and all the things you may desire cannot compare with her. Length of days is in her right hand, in her left hand riches and honor. Her ways are ways of pleasantness, and all her paths are peace." Now verse 18: "She is a tree of life to those who take hold of her, and happy are all who retain her."

What did he call a tree of life? Wisdom. What is the root of wisdom? Truth. What did God present to man in the garden? The tree of life. What is the tree of life? Wisdom. What is the root of wisdom? Truth. God presented to man in the garden the truth. I give you the knowledge of it. And when you understand it, what do you have? Wisdom. God put on man's heart the knowledge of the truth. What was the opposing thing that was on man's heart? The knowledge of good and evil, something that was rejecting the truth: falsehood. The tree of life and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil are not physical trees in a physical garden. They are in the heart of man, what God has sown to the heart of man. This is the truth about life, about this world. Remember that the gospel itself is called the truth. Ephesians 1:13: "In Him you also trusted, after you heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation." The gospel is called the truth.

## Rejecting the Truth About Life

What God presented to man at the beginning is the truth about life. He is telling man, "I am God, I am the one that created the world, and this is the reason why I made you." But man can say it is a lie, nobody created the world, we are just here, I am going to decide what I want to do. That is exactly what man did. He wanted to be his own god, rejected the idea of a god, and decided his own wisdom about how the world is. What led to sin and death and darkness? What we see brought about sin, death, and darkness is that man rejected God's truth and God's purpose. We are seeing the setup that existed, the possibility of sin that brought about the reality of sin.

Are the tree of life and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil physical trees? No. Because they are about knowledge. The two trees are about knowledge. They were on the mind of man. It is about what God was telling man that man can accept or reject. It is not an apple, it is not a mango. It is using a figurative description. It is like the serpent issue. The serpent is not literal. There was no literal serpent talking to man. What it is showing us is the reality of man functioning in a spiritual world and in a natural world. In the spiritual world there is interaction with angels, which happens throughout the Bible. There is interaction with non-physical beings. One of them that became a rebel was later described as a serpent. What they were thinking about was also something that bothered those entities as well. Just like the serpent is not literal, the tree as well is not literal. Not everything is literal.

Notice Genesis 2 verse 15\. "Then the Lord God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to tend and keep it. And the Lord God commanded the man, saying, 'Of every tree of the garden you may freely eat; but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die.'" He is telling him, eat all the natural trees. Eat only one spiritual tree. Don't eat this other spiritual tree. They are not physical trees in the garden, but pointing to his mind: I planted the truth on your heart, but there is also falsehood. Eat only one of the trees. What was God offering man? He called it life. God was offering man His purpose. God's purpose is that He wants man to be in His image, He wants man to be His child, He wants man to be His son. He was offering that purpose to man.

## The First Act of Sin as Rejected Purpose

The first act of sin was man rejecting the truth and God's purpose. The first act of sin was not man stealing, man lying, man doing anything. It is man rejecting the truth and God's purpose. That is the very first act of sin that man did. The truth about existence, the truth about life, rejecting God's purpose is the very first act of sin that man did. How did he reject it? He did not believe it. He spoke contrary to it.

Let's look at Proverbs chapter 18\. How did man reject God's purpose of life? When we say sin and its implication, what sin is at its core is the rejection of God's truth and purpose. If you say, "I am born again, I have received the gospel, the truth is now on my heart. I'm no longer in sin," one of the core things we are going to see when you are no longer in sin is that you have received God's purpose for your life. If you are still living your life independent of God's purpose, you are still sinning, even though you are saved from damnation. God's purpose is what defines righteousness at its core. God made you for a life to walk in His light. He made you with an assignment He wants you to do. If you are living your life independent of God's purpose, then you are still walking in sin. It is very critical for you as a believer to find out God's purpose.

Proverbs 18:20 says, "A man's stomach shall be satisfied from the fruit of his mouth; from the produce of his lips he shall be filled." Does your mouth produce any physical fruit? No. It is saying what your mouth produces are words. Your stomach will be satisfied by the fruit of your mouth, and from the produce of your lips you shall be filled. Look at verse 21: "Death and life are in the power of the tongue, and those who love it will eat its fruit." When they believe either the truth or falsehood, they are going to speak it. The fruit of their mouth, words, the produce of their lips, they will speak it, and by so doing they will eat it. Speaking is the eating.

What man did at the beginning is he rejected God's purpose. He didn't believe it. He heard it, he thought about it, he understood what He was saying, but he was like, "Maybe this is not it." And he spoke in that way: "I don't really believe there's any God. I don't really think there's anything to this thing." By speaking in that way, he put himself under the conditions of death. We have seen very clearly what sin is. Sin at its core is rejecting God's truth and His purpose. The truth that God told you: I want you to have my life and walk as my child, and function as my heir where I'm assigning things to you to do, and doing those things faithfully. The truth that God has told you, you are not faithful to it. This is important because sometimes we joke with God's purpose, our natural assignments just as much as our spiritual assignments. You see people doing their natural assignments as heirs of God in God's purpose as if it's just their work. Your natural work, your career, your business, you are just doing it as if it's your work. The things you are supposed to do, you are not taking them seriously, like you are the one that just decided to be on earth.

## Sin's Effect on God's Purpose and the World

Taking your natural assignment seriously is not a matter of your personal ambition alone; it is a matter of God's purpose. I must do my part faithfully on earth. Whatever has been assigned to me, whatever He has put in my hands, whatever privilege I have in skill, in knowledge, I must do it faithfully, diligently, properly. God will judge that natural assignment and He will judge your spiritual assignment. It is sin not to do God's purpose.

Why are we seeing a world where there is sin? Because man rejected God's purpose. Not because God created sin. What did God create? Free will and His purpose. That is the sodium and the chlorine. If you use the sodium and the chlorine rightly, it will lead to the right thing. If you use it wrongly, it can lead to something explosive. God didn't create the reality of sin, but the possibility of sin. Man rejected God's purpose. The same free will could have accepted God's purpose and the conditions of our world would be different.

When we are witnessing the conditions of sin, death, and darkness, we are seeing that man has rejected God's purpose. What is the effect of man rejecting God's purpose? We are looking at the implications of sin. Sin is not primarily stealing, lying, fornication, cheating. Those things are the effects of sin. When you reject God's purpose, you reject God's life, you are going to walk in the flesh. You walk in darkness, you walk in death. Those will be the effects. The primary thing is you have rejected God's truth and purpose. When you accept God's truth and His purpose, you have His nature; you will now be able to walk in righteousness. Many times people are trying not to sin to get what God wants to give them, but it doesn't work like that. God has to give you what He wants to give you for you not to walk in sin. What is sin itself? It is rejecting what God is giving you. Sin is not, "I am doing something wrong, so God cannot give me what He wants." Sin is, "I have rejected what God wants to give me that will make me start doing things that are wrong." Righteousness is, "I have accepted what God wants, and therefore I can walk the way God wants me to walk."

When you see sin affecting you, it is because of the problem in the world. Maybe you are just on your own, but because of the influence of the world, the influence of sin in the world, seen in our songs, in our movies, you just find that sometimes you just get angry. Unrighteous anger. If someone is trying to kill a baby, you should be angry. That is righteous anger to stop evil. But you are just angry, not in righteousness. You are just walking in the flesh in one regard or the other. It is the effect of sin on you that you have to now resist. Since you have received God's life, you have to say, "No, I'm not like this. I'm righteous. I'm a new creation." Because you believe what God says you are, when you are standing in faith on who you are, you are strengthening the truth of righteousness to walk in it.

Sin entered the world because man rejected God's purpose. It has certain effects. It affects our morality, which is why sometimes we walk in the flesh. Even now that we are born again, the influence is still around. The nature is gone for us. We no longer have that nature of sin. What are the implications of sin? When man rejected God's purpose, Romans 5 verse 12 tells us the very first thing that happened is that sin and death became the authority of the world. The authority or the reign of sin and death became the primary condition of our world. Romans 6 verse 14 says, "Sin shall not have dominion over you, for you are not under law but under grace." Sin is being used as someone that has authority and is trying to rule. They are personifying sin as someone that has authority, rulership, dominion. This is the authority that the devil now takes hold of. He takes the authority of death to reign. Man did not automatically give authority to the devil. Man, by rejecting God's purpose, made sin an authority. The devil now takes advantage of that to be the lord under sin.

## Sin's Implications for Creation, Angels, and Man

Man has brought in the reign of sin, of death, and of darkness. That is why Colossians 1:13 says Jesus has delivered us from the authority of darkness and has translated us into the kingdom of His Son. He has taken us out of the authority of darkness and brought us into the kingdom of His Son. There is the reign of darkness, and there is the reign of life.

What else is the implication of sin? It said sin entered the world and death by sin. Where do we see death? In the nature of the world. Sin has affected the material world. The first thing that man's act of sin did was to bring about the authority or the reign of sin, death, and darkness. The first thing that authority did is to affect the material world. There is corruption in creation because of sin. Romans 8 talks about that. Ephesians 6 tells us there is wickedness in the spirit world because of sin. Ephesians 6 verse 12 says, "We do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this age, against spiritual wickedness in the heavenly places." The way heaven is often taught is as the spiritual world that overlaps the natural one. There is God's heaven, which is His throne, and the church. But heaven as a whole is the spiritual world overlapping the natural world. That's why angels are described with the stars of heaven, and those angels include both good and bad ones. There is now wickedness and corruption in the heavenlies. It is also why Jesus told us that He went to cleanse the heavens. He went to make it possible that in the spiritual world, we can now become part of God's house.

Sin affected the natural world, sin affected the spiritual world. What else did sin affect? Sin affected angels. Second Peter chapter 2\. We are now told that there are angels who sinned. The angels they are talking about here are the ones that we now call the angels of the devil, or demons. Second Peter chapter 2 and verse 4: "For if God did not spare the angels who sinned..." Even the angels that did not sin that he didn't mention here, those angels were also affected by sin, which means the work of redemption that Jesus did had to bring them out of the corruption that affected them in the spiritual realm. If redemption was not done, all of them would have perished under the reign of sin and death that man had brought into the world. They themselves needed to be elect or made holy via what Christ did. It is on the premise of redemption they are called elect angels or holy angels. Sin affected them for redemption to affect them. Sin has affected the natural world, the spiritual world, and the angels.

Lastly, sin affected man. Sin affects man in three ways. First, man is under the reign or dominion of sin, death, and darkness. When human beings are born, they are not born sinners by actions. Whether you sin or not, you are under the dominion of sin. You are a slave of sin even when you have not sinned. If you were born into a family in Israel when they were under slavery in Egypt, you are a slave, not because of anything you did, but just because of your status. The fact that you are born into the family of slaves makes you a slave. Someone is born into this world; the person is not a sinner by nature or by actions yet, but the person is yet a slave of sin by dominion. The fact that sin is the one reigning means you are under sin's reign. The idea that being born as a newborn child means you are free from sin is not true.

## Dominion, Mortality, and the Human Mind

You are free from sin by nature, and you are also not a sinner by conduct, but you are not free from sin relative to dominion or authority. You are under that reign and dominion and authority. This is why a child can be born sick. That is the effect of sin. That is why the child is already born mortal as well. The child is suffering an effect of sin having not sinned because it is a matter of dominion. We are not saying that if that child dies before reaching the age where they can believe, the child is going to hell. The Bible says he that believes not will be damned. Someone that could not believe or not cannot be damned. The verdict for damnation is not "he that never believed," but "he that believed not at the hearing of the gospel." He that did not accept the gospel that was presented to him is the one that will be damned. Not someone that never had the opportunity.

It's like saying someone that had a mental issue throughout their life is going to perish because they couldn't believe. It is when the person did not believe at all upon the presentation of the gospel that the person is damned. But it doesn't change the fact that the person was under sin. Redemption would avail that person since the person could not believe, the same way redemption avails creation itself.

Sin affects man. Man is under a spiritual reign of sin. That is the first effect of sin. We all experienced that immediately. Number two, man has a physical effect of sin. Man has mortality. Apart from mortality, man is susceptible to sickness and disease. Number three, when man reaches the age of rationality, that man is going to have the spiritual nature and conduct of sin. He will have the flesh. Romans 7:9 says, "I was alive once without the law, but when the commandment came, sin revived and I died." At the point of birth, the person is not yet by nature a sinner. But once the person reaches the age of accountability, as long as this dominion of sin is in this world, the person is going to sin and have that nature of sin. The person is going to align with sin.

## Redemption as God's Answer to Sin

The first thing that we saw is we can all see sin in the world, in our beliefs, morality, and in nature. That sin implies that there was the original thing that has gone, the contradiction. There was originally life, we are seeing death. Originally light, we are seeing darkness. Originally good, we are now seeing evil. What caused it? It is because man rejected God's truth and purpose. We saw the effect of that. It has brought about a spiritual dominion and authority that now affects the material world through corruption; the spiritual world through wickedness; the angels, some have sinned, some are now called elect or holy. It affected man in three ways: man is under the dominion of it, it affects man physically, and also spiritually in nature.

What is the final implication of sin on God? Sin affects the world, so it doesn't have any implication on God as a person, but it affects God's purpose. The implication of sin on God, in terms of affecting His purpose, is that God responds to sin with the promise of redemption. Immediately man sinned, He said, "Where are you?" And they were hiding. The man said, "The woman whom you gave to me has done this." The woman said, "It is the serpent that caused it." The Lord told the serpent, "Because you have done this, you are cursed." The curse was not God's initiative but the very effect of what he did. Even God didn't curse man. What man did put man in a cursed state.

One of the challenges we face today in the faith of Christ is that believers are usually moved a lot by testimonies that affect their doctrinal persuasion. There is one I heard, where someone did a charm and it affected some believers, but one was not affected and was able to help the others. As good as that testimony is, it is a problem. Why were those believers affected at all? The explanation they gave was because one had covered themselves with the blood of Jesus. It implies that if the others also said "I cover myself with the blood of Jesus," they wouldn't have been affected. That is a superstitious approach to faith.

If you start teaching things like that, what then is the essence of faith in God's word? As a believer you must be clear: things of death and darkness cannot affect me. I have left the reign of sin. It is not possible. You are giving more strength and power to the enemy by thinking otherwise. People put themselves in a cursed state by what they believe and therefore they are exposed to certain things. A believer must be bold. We shouldn't give such room to the enemy.

What has God given us? The promise of redemption. Immediately after man sinned, God gave the promise of the Redeemer. He said, "In pain you shall bring forth the Child or the Son," pointing to the generation of the Christ. Your desire shall be for your husband, talking about a demand for the Christ. When Eve gave birth to Cain, she said, "I have obtained a man from the Lord." They were expecting someone because God gave them the promise of redemption. When Cain killed Abel, she gave birth to Seth and said, "The Lord has appointed me another seed." They were expecting a seed because God gave them the promise of redemption. God's response to sin is that He promised redemption.

The understanding we must have of sin is that man messed up, but God promised redemption. Now we can say God has fulfilled redemption in Christ. I am going to wrap up for now with this part of our doctrinal summary, sin and its implication. Sin is about the rejection of God's truth and God's purpose. When we receive redemption in Christ Jesus, we know it is not just redemption for redemption's sake. It is redemption back into God's purpose for your life. You must find out God's purpose for your life. You have a part on this earth to play. Do not fail to play your role.
