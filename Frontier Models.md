# Frontier Models

The most capable and advanced foundational AI models available at any given time, typically developed by leading AI labs.

## Characteristics
- **Massive Scale**: Trained on vast datasets using massive clusters of GPUs/TPUs.
- **State-of-the-Art Capabilities**: They set the benchmark for reasoning, coding, multimodal understanding, and general knowledge.
- **High Capital Requirement**: Developing these models requires billions of dollars in infrastructure and talent, limiting the field of developers to a few well-funded entities (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta).

## Strategic Dynamics
- **Distillation Threats**: Smaller or open-source labs often attempt to use outputs from frontier models to train their own smaller models (distillation), essentially piggybacking on the massive R&D of the frontier labs.
- **Security Risks**: As models become more capable (e.g., Anthropic's Mythos), they pose advanced cybersecurity risks, prompting labs to restrict access or repurpose them for defensive measures.
