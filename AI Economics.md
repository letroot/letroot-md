# AI Economics

The study and business dynamics of how artificial intelligence changes cost structures, value capture, and capital allocation in the technology sector.

## Key Themes
- **Return of Marginal Costs**: Unlike traditional SaaS or internet platforms where serving an additional user costs almost zero, AI models require substantial compute (and therefore electricity and hardware wear) for every inference request. 
- **Opportunity Cost of Compute**: Because compute (GPUs/TPUs) is currently constrained, allocating it to one customer or workload means turning away another. Companies must choose between serving internal high-margin products vs. external cloud customers.
- **Capital Intensity**: Building and running frontier models requires unprecedented levels of capital expenditure (CapEx) on data centers, energy infrastructure, and silicon.
